Native Peoples: Cherokee, Tuscarora & Lumbee

  • Museum of the Cherokee People — Online Collections Over 18,000 images of records, letters, and diaries (1830s onward) related to the Trail of Tears; 7,000+ images of Cherokee life (1960–present); 2,000+ Cherokee language materials (1887–1890); and cultural artifacts, maps, and audio-visual resources.
  • Western Carolina University — Cherokee Studies Primary Sources Archives, census rolls, and newspapers related to Cherokee history and genealogy, including Eastern Band of Cherokee Indians records.
  • UNC Pembroke — Lumbee History & Culture Collections Primary sources on Lumbee history, culture, genealogy, and community, including journal articles, audiovisual materials, and archival documents.

Colonial Era: Roanoke to Royal Colony (1580s–1776)

  • Documenting the American South — Colonial and State Records of North Carolina Digitized primary documents covering North Carolina from European settlement through ratification of the U.S. Constitution, one of the longest-running digital publishing initiatives in the nation.
  • North Carolina Digital Collections (NC DNCR) Over 109,000 digitized items including colonial-era manuscripts, historic photographs, state government publications, and documents from the State Archives and State Library.
  • East Carolina University — Colonial Bath Digitized materials on North Carolina’s first town, Bath (established 1705), including documents, maps, and photographs.

Revolutionary War

  • NC State Archives — Revolutionary War Collections War of the Revolution Papers, Governors Papers, Troop Returns (1747–1893), and other military records from the period.

Antebellum Slavery & Plantation Records

  • Documenting the American South (DocSouth) Sixteen thematic collections including the North American Slave Narratives, Library of Southern Literature, the Church in the Southern Black Community, and 500+ oral history interviews.
  • Digital Library on American Slavery (UNC Greensboro) NC Runaway Slave Notices (5,000+ advertisements from 1751–1865) and Race and Slavery Petitions Project (150,000+ individuals from legislative and court petitions).
  • NC State Archives — Records of Enslaved People in Private Collections Bills of sale, correspondence, inventories, wills, and administrative documents relating to enslaved individuals.
  • NC State Archives — Slave Collection, 1748–1856 Original and photocopied documents including deeds of gift and bills of sale for enslaved persons.

Civil War & Reconstruction

  • NC State University — Civil War Era NC Primary sources including letters, diaries, and newspapers from prewar, wartime, and postwar North Carolina perspectives—civilians, soldiers, enslaved persons, and politicians.
  • NC Digital Collections — Civil War Letters, diaries, newspaper articles, reminiscences, troop histories, state records, maps, rosters, and books on North Carolina’s Civil War history.

Wilmington Massacre (1898)

  • UNC Wilmington — Wilmington 1898 Coup Resources Comprehensive research guide with links to primary source collections, newspaper resources, and manuscript collections on the November 10, 1898 white-supremacist overthrow of the legally elected biracial city government.
  • UNC Wilmington Archives — Wilmington Riot Source Documents Newspaper and periodical articles, letters, memoranda, resolutions, and memoirs from principal participants in the 1898 events.
  • UNC Chapel Hill — Wilmington 1898 Primary Sources Curated research guide to central figures and primary documents from the Wilson Special Collections Library.
  • Duke University — Wilmington 1898 Massacre Guide Primary source guide to materials on the Wilmington Massacre held at the Rubenstein Rare Book & Manuscript Library.

Jim Crow & Segregation

  • DigitalNC — Jim Crow South: Life in NC (1930s–1950s) Newspapers, photographs, and documents showing daily life under Jim Crow segregation.
  • DigitalNC — Jim Crow South: Post–Civil War through 1930s Materials spanning Reconstruction through the Jim Crow era, including documentation of racial violence and disenfranchisement.

Wright Brothers at Kitty Hawk

  • Library of Congress — Wilbur and Orville Wright Papers 10,121 items (49,084 digital images) including diaries, notebooks, correspondence, sketches, and 303 glass-plate photographic negatives (1898–1911) documenting the invention of powered flight.
  • Library of Congress — Wright Brothers Negatives Specialized collection of glass-plate negatives taken by the Wrights themselves at Kitty Hawk, Dayton, and elsewhere.

Greensboro Sit-Ins & the Civil Rights Movement

  • NC A&T University — Greensboro Sit-Ins Collections Articles, photos, programs, clippings, and reports related to the February 1, 1960 sit-in movement, including individual collections on the Greensboro Four members (Jibreel Khazan, Franklin McCain, David Richmond, Joseph McNeill).
  • Civil Rights Digital Library — Greensboro Sit-Ins Primary documents and information on the Greensboro sit-in movement and its national impact on the broader civil rights struggle.
  • UNC Greensboro — Civil Rights Digital Collections Searchable digital collection of civil rights materials related to Greensboro’s role in the movement.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • North Carolina State Archives — Digital Collections Colonial records, military records, government documents, and finding aids from the official state archives.
  • Duke University — Rubenstein Rare Book & Manuscript Library 75,000 digitized items from 350,000+ printed volumes and 10,000 archival collections, including the John Hope Franklin Research Center for African and African American History and Culture.
  • UNC Chapel Hill — Wilson Special Collections Library One of the most complete printed documentation collections of a single state, including slave narratives, Reconstruction-era materials, and Jim Crow–era documents.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — North Carolina Newspapers (Library of Congress) 128 digitized North Carolina newspaper titles, fully searchable.
  • DigitalNC (North Carolina Digital Heritage Center) Statewide digitization partnership of 366 institutions, with 1,000+ newspaper titles (1751–1898), photographs, manuscripts, and curated primary source sets.
  • Digital Public Library of America — North Carolina Aggregated search across North Carolina cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
