Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • Florida Dept. of State — Calusa State-curated research resources on the Calusa people including archaeological information and historical documentation.
  • Florida Dept. of State — Timucua & Yemassee Archival resources documenting the Timucua and Yemassee peoples of Florida.
  • Mission San Luis — Archaeological Collections Digital collections from 60+ acres of 17th-century deposits: 950,000+ artifacts from the Apalachee mission period.
  • P.K. Yonge Library — Spanish Colonial Records in Translation Translated documents from the Archive of the Indies on 16th–17th century Florida indigenous peoples.

Spanish Colonial Era (1513–1763, 1783–1821)

  • St. Augustine Colonial Heritage Archive (University of Florida) 25,000+ digitized photographs, maps, government records, and transcriptions of key Spanish colonial documents.
  • P.K. Yonge Library — Digital Collections Over 170,000 digitized items including Spanish colonial manuscripts, diaries, letters, and mission records from both Spanish periods.
  • Florida Dept. of State — Florida History & Heritage State archives materials on Spanish governance, Catholic missions, and colonial administration (1513–1763, 1783–1821).

British Period & Early American (1763–1845)

  • Florida Dept. of State — British Heritage Archival materials documenting the 20-year British rule (1763–1783) of East and West Florida including land grants.
  • P.K. Yonge Library — Historical Map Collection Over 2,300 maps from the mid-1500s to 20th century including British-period territorial and military maps.
  • Florida Dept. of State — Seminole Wars Military records, letters, diaries, and other documents from the three Seminole Wars (1817–1858).
  • FSU Libraries — Seminole Wars Research Materials Primary source materials including military records, diaries, and correspondence.

Antebellum & Civil War

  • Florida Memory — Florida in the Civil War Digitized primary source sets: letters, diaries, photographs, and government documents from the Civil War era.
  • Florida Memory — Guide to Civil War Records Comprehensive guide to state, federal, and private Florida Civil War records held in the State Archives.
  • Florida Dept. of State — Civil War in Florida State archives overview including Confederate pension claims and private papers.

Reconstruction through Early 20th Century

  • Florida Dept. of State — Rosewood Massacre State archival materials and research resources on the 1923 Rosewood massacre including oral histories and contemporary accounts.
  • Florida Memory — Railroads Change Florida Digitized materials on Henry Flagler's railroad development: letters, photographs, and promotional materials.
  • Flagler Museum — First Train to Paradise Digital materials and primary source excerpts on Flagler's Over-Sea Railroad and Florida East Coast Railway.

Modern Florida

Civil Rights

  • Florida Memory — Civil Rights Movement Digitized photographs, documents, and oral histories on sit-ins, desegregation, and civil rights activism in Florida.
  • USF Libraries — African American History Collections Civil rights papers, Weekly Challenger digital newspaper archive, and oral histories from African American leaders.
  • Florida Dept. of State — Florida’s Black History State archival resources on African American history: slavery, Reconstruction, civil rights, and cultural heritage.

Cuban Exile Community

  • Cuban Heritage Collection (University of Miami) Digital collections including speeches, radio programs, interviews, and documents from Cuban exile leaders and community.
  • University of Florida — Cuban Archives & Manuscripts Thirty-five archival collections documenting Cuban history and culture with focus on the exile experience since the 1960s.
  • Florida Memory — Cuban Experience Photographs, government documents, letters, videos, and interviews on Cuban refugee experiences in Florida.

Space Program & Everglades

  • Cape Canaveral Space Force Museum — Special Collections Boeing Inertial Upper Stage Collection and Stephen W. Gilley Collection documenting space program operations (1956–1968).
  • Florida Memory — Marjory Stoneman Douglas Digitized letters, writings, and photographs documenting Douglas's conservation advocacy and Everglades work.
  • Florida Memory — Everglades Historical photographs and primary sources documenting the Everglades landscape and conservation history.

Florida Government & Law

  • Florida Memory Over 320,000 digitized photographs, documents, audio, and video from the State Library and Archives of Florida.
  • State Archives of Florida — Online Catalog Searchable catalog of state government records, private manuscripts, local government records, and photographs.
  • Florida Dept. of State — Research Resources Comprehensive state portal to archival collections organized by historical period and topic.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Florida Digital Newspaper Library Over 2.6 million pages of Florida newspapers (1821–present) with full-text search.
  • Chronicling America — Florida Newspapers Digitized historic Florida newspapers via the Library of Congress.
  • Florida Memory — Maps Collection Over 300 digitized maps from 16th–20th centuries: township, military, railroad, and nautical charts.
  • University of Florida — Florida Historical Maps Over 2,300 rendered maps from the mid-1500s to 20th century covering exploration, settlement, and development.
  • University of Florida Digital Collections (UFDC) Over 300 digital collections with 622,000+ items: books, newspapers, oral histories, videos, photos, and manuscripts.
  • P.K. Yonge Library of Florida History University of Florida's specialized library with over 170,000 digitized items covering all periods of Florida history.
  • Library of Congress — Florida Guide Comprehensive research guide to Library of Congress and partner Florida digital resources.
