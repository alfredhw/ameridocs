Regional collections spanning Alaska, California, Hawaii, Oregon, and Washington. See also individual state pages.

Regional Digital Portals

  • Calisphere University of California’s free gateway to 200,000+ digitized primary sources from 2,000+ collections at 300+ California cultural heritage organizations: photographs, documents, newspaper pages, diaries, and oral histories.
  • Online Archive of California 250,000 digitized items and 78,000 finding aids from 350+ California institutions: manuscripts, photographs, artwork, and scientific data.
  • Archives West 40,000+ finding aids from archives across Washington, Oregon, Montana, Idaho, Utah, and Wyoming, with descriptions of primary sources and digital reproductions.
  • Washington Digital Newspapers 600,000+ pages of Washington’s territorial and historical newspapers (1852–2020), freely searchable through the National Digital Newspaper Program.

Pacific Northwest

  • University of Washington — Center for the Study of the Pacific Northwest Digital resources and essays on Pacific Northwest history: indigenous peoples, fur trade, settlement, the timber industry, and regional development.
  • Oregon Historical Society — Oregon History Project 1,000+ digitized and annotated primary sources from OHS’s research library and museum, organized thematically and chronologically.
  • Oregon Historical Society — Digital Collections 37,000+ digitized items: historical documents, letters, photographs, oral histories, films, maps, and architectural plans documenting Oregon and the Northwest.
  • UW Libraries — Salmon in the Pacific Northwest and Alaska Documents and photographs on the place of salmon in the late nineteenth- and early twentieth-century Pacific Northwest, and the roots of the contemporary salmon crisis.
  • Plateau Peoples’ Web Portal Collaboration between the Spokane, Colville, Umatilla, Coeur d’Alene, Warm Springs, and Yakama Nations and Washington State University—a gateway to cultural materials from multiple repositories.
  • UW Libraries — American Indians of the Pacific Northwest Essays, historic images, treaties, maps, and Indian Agent reports on Northwest Coast and Plateau Indian cultures spanning Alaska, British Columbia, Washington, Oregon, Idaho, and Montana.

Asian American & Pacific Islander History

  • Densho Digital Archive Nearly 2,000 hours of indexed, transcribed video interviews; 80,000+ photographs and documents; and the Densho Encyclopedia documenting the Japanese American experience from the early 1900s through WWII incarceration and redress.
  • Angel Island Immigration Station Foundation — The Vault Digital archive from the “Ellis Island of the West”: records from approximately 200,000 Asian immigrant case files, photographs, documents, and poetry carved into detention barracks walls.
  • Library of Congress — Chinese Exclusion Act Digital Collections Manuscripts, government documents, newspaper articles, and broadsides related to the Chinese Exclusion Act (1882) and Chinese American immigration history. (See also Immigration.)
  • UC Berkeley — Chinese Inclusion Project 8,000 images and pages of primary sources from the Bancroft Library and California Historical Society on Chinese immigration to California: photographs, letters, business records, and legal documents.
  • CSU Dominguez Hills — Filipino American Digital Archives Digital collections documenting Filipino American history: photographs, videos, oral histories, meeting notes, memorabilia, and family records.

Alaska

  • Alaska’s Digital Archives Photographs and primary source documents principally covering the Alaskan Territorial period (1867–1959), from the Alaska State Library, UAA, and UAF.
  • Library of Congress — Alaska Purchase Treaty Digital Collections Government documents, manuscripts, maps, and newspaper articles on the 1867 U.S. purchase of Alaska from Russia and the Russian colonial period.
  • Library of Congress — Meeting of Frontiers: Russian America Essays and primary sources on Russian colonization and settlement in Alaska (1741–1867), including materials from both American and Russian archives.
  • Alaska State Library — Gold Rush Research Guide Specialized research guide for the Klondike and Alaska gold rushes (1896–1914): archival collections, finding aids, and biographical resources.

Hawai‘i

  • Hawaiian Mission Houses — Digital Archive Journals and letters of Hawai‘i missionaries, The Friend newspaper (1843–1954), Mission Station Reports (1822–1865), and 80,000+ digitized items including one of the largest collections of Hawaiian-language books.
  • He ‘Ohina Nūpepa ‘Ōlelo Hawai‘i (Hawaiian Language Newspapers) Transcriptions and translations of Hawaiian-language newspapers published 1834–1948, searchable in English or Hawaiian—preserving a vast body of Native Hawaiian journalism and cultural documentation.
  • UH Mānoa — Hawai‘i War Records Depository WWII-era archival materials (1943–1949): 1,000+ newspaper photographs, correspondence, personal accounts, maps, posters, and memorabilia documenting Hawai‘i’s wartime experience including Pearl Harbor and martial law.

World War II in the Pacific States

  • Densho Digital Repository 80,000+ items documenting the WWII Japanese American incarceration experience at camps across the West Coast, with advanced search across video interviews, photographs, newspapers, and government records.
  • National Archives — Japanese American Internment Records Digitized Japanese American internee cards and 4,000+ War Relocation Authority photographs documenting the forced relocation and incarceration.
  • DPLA — Rosie the Riveter and Working Women in WWII Primary sources on the millions of women who entered West Coast shipyards and factories during the war. (See also Women’s History.)

Environmental History & Conservation

  • Yosemite National Park — Archives 4 million+ items documenting Yosemite’s history and conservation, with 6,000+ digitized photographs, maps, drawings, and management documents available on NPGallery.
  • Library of Congress — Voices from the Dust Bowl Audio recordings, photographs, and manuscripts documenting life in FSA migrant work camps in central California (1940–1941)—the voices of Dust Bowl refugees in the promised land.
  • CSU Bakersfield — California Odyssey 57 oral history interviews with Dust Bowl migrants to the San Joaquin Valley from Oklahoma, Arkansas, Missouri, and Kansas (1924–1939), with historical photographs.

Major Research Collections

  • UC Berkeley — Bancroft Library Digital Collections 300,000+ digitized items from one of the world’s great western Americana collections: California history, Chinese American immigration, Japanese American incarceration, and the trans-Pacific world.
  • Huntington Library 216,000+ digital objects from an independent research library with exceptional strengths in California history, Hispanic culture, the Pacific Rim, and American history.
  • Washington State Digital Archives The nation’s first digital archives dedicated to preserving electronic government records: digitized historical records, photographs, and audio recordings from state and local agencies.
  • California Revealed California State Library initiative digitizing archival materials from public libraries, archives, museums, and heritage organizations: video, audio, newspapers, scrapbooks, photographs, and manuscripts.
