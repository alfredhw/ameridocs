Indigenous Peoples of the Potomac Region

  • National Park Service — Native Peoples of Washington, DC Educational resource on the Nacotchtank (Anacostan), Piscataway Conoy, and Pamunkey peoples, including documentation from Captain John Smith’s 1608 accounts of the Potomac region.

Founding the Capital: The L’Enfant Plan

  • Library of Congress — L’Enfant–McMillan Plan of Washington (1792) Digitized original maps and drawings of Pierre Charles L’Enfant’s design for the new federal capital, from the Geography and Map Division.
  • Library of Congress — Plan of the City of Washington Historical map showing the original L’Enfant Plan layout, street grid, and monumental core of the capital city.

Slavery & the Slave Trade in Washington

  • Civil War Washington (University of Nebraska–Lincoln) Digital humanities project with slaveholders’ compensation petitions from DC (1862), comprehensive records of enslaved people by name, maps, photographs, medical records, and databases of people and places.
  • Library of Congress — Slavery in America: Digital Collections Guide to digital collections on slavery, including materials on the District’s role as a major slave-trading center before the Compromise of 1850 abolished the trade in the capital.

Civil War: The Capital Under Arms

  • Civil War Washington 1864–1865 newspapers, maps, photographs, hospital records, and interactive databases documenting Washington as military headquarters, including the ring of 68 forts and batteries encircling the city.
  • National Park Service — Fort Stevens Historical resources on the Battle of Fort Stevens (July 1864), where President Lincoln came under enemy fire during General Early’s raid on the capital.
  • National Archives — Freedmen’s Bureau Records DC field office records (1865–1870) with camp registers from Camp Barker and Mason’s Island, documenting freedmen’s camps and Reconstruction-era services in the capital.

African American History: Howard University & Black Broadway

  • Howard University — Moorland-Spingarn Research Center One of the world’s largest repositories documenting the global Black experience, with 175,000+ books and periodicals, the Black Press Archives (2,000+ newspaper titles), and extensive manuscript collections.
  • Black Broadway on U Award-winning digital platform with oral histories, historic photographs, interactive maps, and virtual reality tours documenting the U Street “Black Broadway” era (1900s–1950s).
  • Library of Congress — Civil War Era Black Washington Research guide with digital collections on Black experiences in Civil War–era Washington, DC.

The 1963 March on Washington

  • National Archives — March on Washington Primary documents including President Kennedy’s message to Congress, correspondence, and official plans for the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom (August 28, 1963).
  • Library of Congress — Civil Rights History Project: The March Articles, essays, photographs, and digital collections documenting the 1963 March on Washington.

Home Rule & Statehood

  • DC History Center — Statehood Research Guide Comprehensive guide to DC statehood with links to primary sources, activist papers, timelines, and digitized collections including the John Capozzi Collection and DC Statehood Commission Records.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • DC Public Library — The People’s Archive Digital repository preserving DC history including photographs, oral histories, and documents from the Washingtoniana Division, Black Studies collection, and DC Community Archives.
  • Dig DC (DC Public Library) Searchable digital collection of DC history materials including photographs, maps, newspapers, and archival items.
  • Historical Society of Washington, DC — Online Collections 100,000+ photographic prints and extensive DC historical records from the Kiplinger Research Library.
  • Georgetown University — Archives & Special Collections University archives with materials on Georgetown’s history and the broader history of Washington, DC, including DigitalGeorgetown online collections.
  • Library of Congress — Washington, DC Resource Guide Comprehensive guide to LOC’s DC materials including maps, photographs, manuscripts, and government documents.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — DC Newspapers (Library of Congress) Digitized District of Columbia newspaper titles, fully searchable.
  • Digital Public Library of America — District of Columbia Aggregated search across DC cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
