Abenaki Peoples

  • New Hampshire Historical Society — Abenaki Before 1600 Educational resources and primary source materials on Abenaki history in what is now New Hampshire.
  • Strawbery Banke Museum — Abenaki Heritage Initiative Interactive exhibits exploring Abenaki culture, arts, foodways, and storytelling traditions in the Piscataqua region.
  • Dawnland Voices — Indigenous New England Digital Collections Digital collections including historical treaties and documents, such as the Treaty of Portsmouth (1713).

Colonial Period: Portsmouth & Strawbery Banke

  • Strawbery Banke Museum — Online Archives Searchable archives with 25,000+ artifacts, documents, and photographs related to Portsmouth’s colonial and early history (1623–1960).
  • Portsmouth Public Library — Online Archives Photographs, maps, vital records, city documents, and postcards depicting Portsmouth and the Seacoast area from the colonial era forward.
  • University of New Hampshire — Provincial and State Papers (Vols. 1–40) Complete digitized set of New Hampshire’s Provincial and State Papers (1603–1835), including colonial governance records, court papers, town charters, and probate records.

Revolutionary War: First to Declare Independence

  • New Hampshire Historical Society — New Hampshire and the American Revolution Primary source sets on New Hampshire’s role in the Revolution, including its January 1776 constitution—the first among the colonies to establish an independent government.
  • University of New Hampshire — Revolutionary Documents (State Papers, Vol. 30) Miscellaneous revolutionary documents including the association test, pension rolls, and military records.

White Mountains & the Old Man of the Mountain

  • New Hampshire Historical Society — Old Man of the Mountain Primary Source Set Curated primary source set with historical documents and images of the iconic formation that collapsed in 2003.
  • Museum of the White Mountains (Plymouth State University) — Digital Catalogues Digital catalogues, the Daniel Noel Collection, photographs, and postcards documenting White Mountains tourism history.
  • Historic New England — White Mountains Library and Archives Library and archives resources on White Mountains tourism, photography, and the grand hotel era.
  • Dartmouth College — White Mountains Vertical Files Collection Brochures, travel guides, maps, postcards, photographs, and ephemera related to White Mountain tourism and recreation.

Mt. Washington Observatory

  • New Hampshire Historical Society — Mount Washington Primary Source Set Primary source set on Mount Washington history and scientific observations.
  • Mount Washington Observatory — History Official history of the observatory founded in 1932, with meteorological records dating to the 1870s and documentation of the world-record wind speed (231 mph, 1934).

Textile Mills: Manchester & Amoskeag

  • New Hampshire Historical Society — Amoskeag Manufacturing Company Curated primary sources on the world’s largest cotton textile plant, which at its peak employed 17,000 workers in Manchester.
  • Manchester Historic Association — Amoskeag Collections Guide Guide to Amoskeag Manufacturing Company records including accounting ledgers, engineering drawings, and production documents.
  • Internet Archive — Manuscript History of Amoskeag Manufacturing Company (1949) Digitized company history manuscript with internal records and historical narrative.

Franklin Pierce (14th President)

  • Library of Congress — Franklin Pierce Papers Approximately 2,350 items dating 1820–1869, including correspondence and manuscripts of the 14th president from Concord, New Hampshire.
  • New Hampshire Historical Society — Franklin Pierce Primary Source Set Curated primary sources on the life and presidency of New Hampshire’s only president.

First-in-the-Nation Primary

  • New Hampshire Historical Society — A Proven Primary Tradition Online exhibition covering New Hampshire’s first-in-the-nation primary tradition from its origins to the present.
  • New Hampshire Public Radio — Election Database Searchable database with town-level results from 1952 to the present, covering every presidential primary.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • New Hampshire Historical Society — Research Portal Gateway to collections catalog, genealogy resources, and online exhibitions with materials dating to the 17th century.
  • New Hampshire Historical Society — Moose on the Loose Award-winning educational platform with 18 units covering all of New Hampshire history, featuring curated primary source sets.
  • University of New Hampshire — Digital Collections Digitized materials from Milne Special Collections including rare books, manuscripts, photographs, and university archives.
  • Dartmouth College — Digital Collections Over 100,000 rare books, millions of manuscripts, and extensive Dartmouth College Archives in the Rauner Special Collections Library.
  • Concord Public Library — Digital Collections 1,500+ files dating back 200+ years including photographs, church and business records, and city documents.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — New Hampshire Newspapers (Library of Congress) Digitized New Hampshire newspaper titles including Among the Clouds (Mt. Washington, 1889–1917) and the New Hampshire Gazette.
  • Dartmouth College — New Hampshire Digital Newspaper Project Collaborative project with the NH State Library, Historical Society, and UNH to digitize the state’s historic newspapers.
  • Digital Public Library of America — New Hampshire Aggregated search across New Hampshire cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
