Online Sources for United States History
Home › Southeast

Regional collections spanning Alabama, Arkansas, Florida, Georgia, Kentucky, Louisiana, Mississippi, North Carolina, South Carolina, Tennessee, Virginia, and West Virginia. See also individual state pages.

Regional Digital Portals

  • University of North Carolina — Documenting the American South (DocSouth) The premier digital collection for Southern history: sixteen thematic collections of primary sources including books, diaries, posters, letters, and oral histories spanning the colonial period through the early twentieth century.
  • Civil Rights Digital Library 55,000+ digitized items from approximately 200 cultural heritage institutions: primary sources, historical news footage, and biographical information on 3,000+ civil rights figures across the South.
  • Digital Library of Georgia 830,000+ digitized items from approximately 200 institutions: photographs, manuscripts, maps, correspondence, and audio-visual materials documenting Georgia and the broader Southeast.
  • South Carolina Digital Library 200,000+ items from 40+ cultural heritage institutions: photographs, manuscripts, journals, oral histories, maps, and newspapers organized by county and historical period.
  • Louisiana Digital Library 400,000+ digital items from 25+ Louisiana archives, libraries, and museums: photographs, maps, manuscripts, oral histories, and cultural heritage materials.
  • DigitalNC: North Carolina’s Digital Heritage 90,000+ items from across North Carolina: historic photographs, state publications, manuscripts, and regional resources from UNC’s North Carolina Digital Heritage Center.
  • Florida Memory 320,000+ digitized photographs, manuscripts, war records, vital statistics, and audio-video recordings from the State Library and Archives of Florida.

Slavery & Plantation Records

  • DocSouth — North American Slave Narratives All known extant slave narratives published as broadsides, pamphlets, or books, plus biographies of formerly enslaved people—the most comprehensive digital collection of individual and collective stories of African Americans struggling for freedom.
  • Library of Congress — Born in Slavery: Federal Writers’ Project Narratives 2,300+ interviews with formerly enslaved people conducted 1936–1938, providing first-person accounts of slavery, slaveholders, family life, and freedom across the South. (See also Slavery & Abolition.)
  • Library of Congress — Voices Remembering Slavery Audio recordings of former slaves recorded 1932–1975 in nine states, featuring twenty-two interviewees discussing their experiences—capturing the actual voices of the enslaved.
  • College of Charleston — Lowcountry Digital History Initiative Online exhibitions on slavery, plantations, and the African diaspora in the Carolina Lowcountry: “African Passages, Lowcountry Adaptations,” “Hidden Voices: Enslaved Women,” and “Forgotten Fields: Inland Rice Plantations.”
  • DocSouth — First-Person Narratives of the American South Diaries, autobiographies, memoirs, and travel accounts by Southerners whose voices were historically less prominent: African Americans, women, enlisted men, laborers, and Native Americans.

The Civil War & Reconstruction

  • DocSouth — The Southern Homefront, 1861–1865 Primary sources on all aspects of Southern civilian life during the Civil War: government documents, personal diaries, religious pamphlets, political correspondence, and Confederate currency.
  • Library of Congress — Confederate States of America Records Records spanning 1854–1889 (bulk 1861–1865) from the Departments of State, Justice, Treasury, Navy, War, and Post Office, plus the Confederate Congress, President, and Constitution.
  • National Archives — War Department Collection of Confederate Records (RG 109) Confederate military and administrative records acquired by capture, surrender, or later donation: the comprehensive federal record of Confederate military operations.
  • University of Virginia — Valley of the Shadow Landmark digital history project comparing Augusta County, Virginia and Franklin County, Pennsylvania during the Civil War: letters, diaries, newspapers, military records, church records, and census data.
  • Civil War in the American South Portal ASERL collaborative initiative providing access to 10,000+ Civil War–era items (1850–1865) from 32 Southeastern research libraries: manuscripts, letters, sermons, economic data, and publications.

The Civil Rights Movement

  • Duke University — SNCC Digital Gateway Comprehensive digital archive of the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee: Freedom Rides, voter registration campaigns, and grassroots organizing across the South (1960–1966). (See also Slavery & Abolition.)
  • Stanford University — King Institute Over 1 million digitized documents: Martin Luther King Jr.’s sermons, speeches, correspondence, and papers connecting the Southern Christian Leadership Conference to the broader movement.
  • American Archive of Public Broadcasting — Eyes on the Prize Interviews 300+ raw footage interviews from the landmark civil rights documentary series, with extensive testimony from movement participants across the South.

Appalachian Collections

  • West Virginia & Regional History Center — History OnView 50,000+ historic images from West Virginia and Central Appalachia, with extensive manuscript, newspaper (1,200 titles), photograph (1 million+), and oral history collections from across the region.
  • Virginia Tech — Appalachian Collections Extensive Appalachian manuscripts and digitized materials covering Virginia and the broader region: community life, coal mining, agriculture, and cultural traditions.
  • Berea College — Sound Archives Major archival collection of Appalachian music: fiddle tunes, banjo music, folk songs, ballads, and sacred vocal music from the Southern Appalachian region.

Southern Folk Culture & Music

  • University of Mississippi — Blues Archive One of the world’s largest blues collections: 100,000+ sound recordings, 300,000+ photographs, 3,000+ videos, 6,000+ books, and 400,000 artifacts including rare 78-rpm Delta blues records.
  • Association for Cultural Equity — Alan Lomax Archive Digitized field recordings including Lomax’s 1941–1942 Mississippi Delta sessions (the first recordings of Muddy Waters and Honey Boy Edwards) and thousands of hours of Southern folk music documentation.
  • Library of Congress — Roots of Country Music Digital collections on Appalachian musical traditions: shape-note singing, ballads, unaccompanied singing, and the origins of country music in the Southern mountains.

The New South & Industrialization

  • DocSouth — Southern Oral History Program: Industrialization 360+ oral history interviews documenting workers in textile, tobacco, hosiery, and furniture factories across North Carolina, Tennessee, and South Carolina—voices of the industrial New South.
  • Duke University — Digital Durham Digital archive examining Durham, North Carolina as a case study of New South transformation: tobacco factories, textile mills, Black entrepreneurs, and industrial urbanization.
  • University of North Alabama — TVA Collection 2,800+ architectural drawings and photographs documenting the Tennessee Valley Authority: the massive New Deal regional development project spanning seven Southern states.

Major Research Collections

  • University of North Carolina — Southern Historical Collection 15+ million items in 4,600+ collections documenting the American South: letters, diaries, oral histories, photographs, financial records, and literary manuscripts from the antebellum era through the Civil Rights Movement.
  • University of South Carolina — Digital Collections Nearly 300 digital collections from South Caroliniana Library: rare manuscripts, Revolutionary War materials, Civil War letters, broadsides, and Audubon’s Birds of America.
  • Library of Virginia — Digital Collections Virginia’s official repository with digitized archival records, manuscripts, maps, and documents relevant to the broader Southeast.
  • Alabama Department of Archives and History — Digital Collections Official repository of Alabama state records with digitized documents, photographs, and materials on Alabama and regional Southeast history.
  • Filson Historical Society — Digital Projects Kentucky and Ohio Valley collections: exhibits, manuscripts, photographs, and artifacts documenting Louisville, Kentucky, Southern Indiana, and the Ohio Valley borderland.
