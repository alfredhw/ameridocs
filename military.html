Colonial Wars & the French and Indian War (1754–1763)

  • Library of Congress — George Washington Papers Approximately 65,000 items including correspondence, diaries, and military records from Washington’s service as a Virginia militia colonel during the French and Indian War (Series 6).
  • Founders Online — Washington Colonial Series Searchable papers from Washington’s pre-Revolutionary career (1744–1775), including military correspondence and orderly books from the French and Indian War campaigns.
  • Library of Congress — French and Indian War Resource Guide Curated guide to primary source collections on the Seven Years’ War in North America, including maps, manuscripts, and government documents.

American Revolution (1775–1783)

  • Founders Online (National Archives) 175,000+ searchable documents from Washington, Franklin, Adams, Jefferson, Hamilton, Jay, and Madison, including Washington’s Revolutionary War Series (1775–1783) with orderly books, court-martial proceedings, interrogations of British deserters, and strategic correspondence.
  • Library of Congress — Washington Papers (Complete) Full digitized collection of Washington’s papers including Continental Army correspondence, military orders, reports from Valley Forge, and the Yorktown campaign.
  • National Archives — American Revolution Records Central repository for military service records of the Revolutionary War, including compiled service records of Continental troops and state militia.
  • DocsTeach — American Revolution Primary Sources Interactive primary source documents from the National Archives with digitized originals from the Revolutionary period.
  • National Park Service — Valley Forge Documents Primary sources on the Continental Army’s 1777–1778 winter encampment at Valley Forge, including letters, journals, and military orders.

War of 1812 (1812–1815)

  • National Park Service — Fort McHenry Primary sources on the defense of Baltimore Harbor (September 1814), including Francis Scott Key’s original manuscript and eyewitness accounts of the bombardment.
  • Library of Congress — War of 1812 Resource Guide Curated digital collections and research guides on the War of 1812, including manuscripts, maps, prints, and government documents.

Mexican-American War (1846–1848)

  • Library of Congress — Mexican War Digital Collections Manuscripts, government documents, newspaper articles, maps, and sheet music related to the U.S.–Mexico War.
  • Library of Congress — Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo Primary documents and resource guides for the 1848 treaty that ended the war and ceded vast territories to the United States.
  • National Archives — Mexican War Military Records Finding aids and research guides for Mexican War military service records, pension files, and campaign documents.

Civil War (1861–1865)

Official Records & Government Documents

  • Cornell University — Official Records of the War of the Rebellion Complete 127-volume compilation of official military reports, correspondence, and orders from Union and Confederate forces—the most comprehensive Civil War primary source collection, fully digitized and searchable.
  • Ohio State eHistory — War of the Rebellion Official Records Alternative full-text access to the Official Records with browseable volume structure.
  • Library of Congress — Civil War Regimental Histories Research guide to locating regimental histories, unit records, and organizational documents from Civil War military units.

Photographs & Visual Sources

  • Library of Congress — Civil War Glass Negatives and Related Prints Approximately 7,000 photographic views and portraits by Mathew Brady, Alexander Gardner, and Timothy O’Sullivan, with detailed captions documenting battlefields, camps, officers, and the landscape of war.
  • National Archives — Civil War Photographs Extensive searchable collection of Civil War photographs including Brady and Gardner images and military documentation.

Soldier Letters, Diaries & Personal Accounts

  • Library of Congress — Donald Benham Civil War Collection Letters, photographs, documents, and memorabilia from soldiers on both sides of the conflict.
  • University of Iowa — Civil War Diaries and Letters Transcribed diaries and correspondence from Iowa soldiers during the Civil War.
  • Auburn University — Civil War Diaries Digitized diaries from Confederate and Union soldiers held in Auburn’s special collections.
  • University of Washington — Civil War Letters Correspondence from soldiers and their families during the Civil War.
  • Virginia Tech — Civil War Diaries Digitized diaries from Virginia soldiers and civilians documenting the conflict from a Southern perspective.

African American Soldiers & the USCT

  • National Archives — Black Soldiers in the Civil War Primary documents on the approximately 185,000 African Americans who served in the United States Colored Troops, including compiled military service records, pension applications, and letters from families.
  • University of Maryland — Freedmen and Southern Society Project 44+ transcribed primary documents (1861–1865) from National Archives originals with annotations, covering military and civilian perspectives on emancipation and Black soldiers’ experiences.

Medical Records & Naval Warfare

  • National Archives — Carded Medical Records (1821–1912) Compiled Surgeon General’s Office records with hospital records, case histories, and medical reports for Union and Confederate soldiers.
  • Civil War Washington (University of Nebraska–Lincoln) Digital humanities project with databases of people and places, slaveholders’ petitions (1862), hospital records, maps, photographs, and comprehensive records from the capital during the war.

Spanish-American War (1898)

  • Library of Congress — The World of 1898 Comprehensive digital resource on the Spanish-American War with primary sources covering campaigns in Cuba, the Philippines, Puerto Rico, and Guam.
  • Library of Congress — Spanish-American War Digital Collections Manuscripts, ephemera, early motion pictures, newspaper articles, and maps from the 1898 conflict and the emergence of the United States as a global power.

World War I (1917–1918)

  • Library of Congress — Stars and Stripes (WWI) Complete digitized run of all 71 issues of the official AEF newspaper published in France (February 8, 1918 – June 13, 1919), with soldier correspondence, news, cartoons, and photographs.
  • National Archives — American Expeditionary Forces Records (RG 120) 26,016 cubic feet of AEF records including correspondence, operations reports, intelligence summaries, and war diaries from 1917–1919.
  • Library of Congress — Military Newspapers Guide Guide to accessing Stars and Stripes newspapers and related WWI military newspaper collections across multiple conflicts.
  • National WWI Museum and Memorial — Military Records Research collections and military records database at the nation’s official WWI museum in Kansas City.

World War II (1941–1945)

General Collections & Oral Histories

  • The National WWII Museum — Digital Collections Thousands of oral histories, hundreds of thousands of photographs, letters, diaries, and military records browsable by topic including combat, home front, prisoners of war, and liberation.
  • Eisenhower Presidential Library — D-Day and World War II Primary sources including Eisenhower’s D-Day orders, the famous “In Case of Failure” message, 8th Air Force reports, amphibious operation plans, and 82nd Airborne Division records.
  • National Archives — D-Day Records Thousands of D-Day records including photographs, operational documents, and after-action reports from the Normandy invasion (June 6, 1944).

Tuskegee Airmen

  • Air Force Historical Research Agency — Tuskegee Airmen Chronologies, oral histories, photographs, and official documents on the 99th Fighter Squadron and 332nd Fighter Group—the first African American military aviators in the U.S. armed forces.
  • University of California, Riverside — Tuskegee Airmen Collections Archival collections on the Tuskegee Airmen including personal papers, photographs, and oral histories.

Navajo Code Talkers

  • Library of Congress — Navajo Code Talkers Guide Veterans History Project interviews with Navajo Code Talkers and research guide to primary source collections on the Marine Corps Code Talker program.
  • National Archives — Code Talker Files Federal records documenting the Navajo and other Native American Code Talker programs, including Marine Corps oral history transcripts from 1971.

442nd Regimental Combat Team

  • University of Hawaii — 442nd RCT Documents Monthly reports, general orders, and memoranda from 442nd RCT Headquarters (July 1944 – May 1946), documenting the most decorated unit of its size in U.S. military history.
  • Densho Encyclopedia — 442nd RCT Comprehensive entry with primary source references on the Japanese American combat unit that fought in the European Theater while their families were confined in incarceration camps.

The Manhattan Project

  • Department of Energy — Manhattan Project Historical Resources Declassified documents, the 36-volume Manhattan District History, and primary sources from the development of the atomic bomb at Los Alamos, Oak Ridge, and Hanford.
  • Atomic Archive — Manhattan Project Documents Key primary documents including the Quebec Agreement, the Franck Report, the Smyth Report, and the Szilard petition to the President.
  • National Archives — Manhattan Project Notebook Milestone document: a notebook from the Manhattan Project documenting early nuclear research, part of the National Archives’ most historically significant holdings.

Office of Strategic Services (OSS)

  • National Archives — OSS Records 23,973 descriptions of OSS personnel files (1941–1945) containing 750,000 pages, plus intelligence reports and approximately 400,000 pages declassified through the Nazi War Criminals Interagency Working Group.

Holocaust Liberation

  • U.S. Holocaust Memorial Museum — Primary Source Databases Databases documenting the liberation of concentration camps by American forces and Nazi war crimes investigations.

Korean War (1950–1953)

  • Truman Presidential Library — Korean War and Its Origins 275 documents (1,151 pages) covering 1945–1953, including official correspondence, photographs, and supporting materials on Korean War policy and military operations.
  • Truman Library — Korean War File Approximately 11,200 pages of State and Defense Department documents, UN Security Council reports (July 1950 – November 1952), briefing memoranda, daily world reaction reports, and Korea bulletins.

Vietnam War (1955–1975)

  • Texas Tech University — Vietnam Center and Sam Johnson Vietnam Archive The nation’s largest Vietnam War collection: 7 million scanned pages of documents, photographs, slides, oral histories, artifacts, film, sound recordings, and maps.
  • Vietnam Center — Oral History Project Extensive collection of oral histories from Vietnam War veterans and participants, initiated in 1999 and continually expanding.
  • National Archives — Pentagon Papers Complete 7,000-page declassified Defense Department history of U.S. decision-making on Vietnam, fully searchable.
  • George Washington University — National Security Archive: Pentagon Papers Annotated collection of Pentagon Papers documents with scholarly context from GWU’s National Security Archive.

Gulf War, Iraq & Afghanistan (1990–Present)

  • Library of Congress — Veterans History Project Over 121,000 collections of oral histories, photographs, letters, diaries, and memorials from U.S. military veterans across all conflicts from World War I through current operations. Thousands of interviews freely available online.

Service Branches: Archives & Historical Offices

U.S. Army

  • Army Heritage and Education Center (USAHEC) The Army’s primary historical research facility with over 15 million items spanning from the Revolutionary War to the present, including one of the world’s largest Civil War photograph collections.

U.S. Navy

  • Naval History and Heritage Command 500,000+ historic photographs, 18,000+ paintings and prints, 150,000+ books, 80,000+ manuscripts, plus oral histories, ship records, and biographies spanning the full history of the U.S. Navy.
  • National Archives — Navy Records Federal naval records and finding aids for Navy historical materials across all eras.

U.S. Marine Corps

  • Marine Corps History Division — Archives Personal papers, manuscripts, photographs, diaries, maps, and oral histories on Marine Corps history, with growing digital collections.
  • Marine Corps University — Digitized Map Collections Historical maps from Marine Corps archives documenting campaigns and operations.

U.S. Air Force

  • Air Force Historical Research Agency (AFHRA) Central repository for U.S. Air Force and Space Force documentation at Maxwell Air Force Base: unit histories, special studies, personal papers, oral histories, and end-of-tour reports.
  • Air Force History Index Searchable database indexing approximately 550,000 historical documents covering roughly 70 million pages of Air Force history.

U.S. Coast Guard

  • Coast Guard Historian’s Office — Digital Library Collections on Coast Guard history including materials from the Revenue Cutter Service, Life Saving Service, and Lighthouse Service.
  • Coast Guard Historian — Historic Documents Primary documents and historical materials from the Coast Guard Historian’s Office.

Service Academies

  • U.S. Military Academy (West Point) — Archives & Special Collections Approximately 1,500 linear feet of personal manuscripts from Academy alumni, including papers of Grant, Sherman, Lee, Bradley, and Patton. Searchable through the USMA ArchivesSpace database.
  • U.S. Naval Academy (Annapolis) — Special Collections Digital collections including the Naval Academy History Collection, The Lucky Bag yearbooks (digitized), manuscripts, and correspondence through the Trireme digital preservation system.

Honors, Recognition & Memorials

  • Congressional Medal of Honor Society — Recipients Database Searchable database of all Medal of Honor recipients with complete citations from the Civil War through the present.
  • Library of Congress — Medal of Honor Guide Nearly 150 digitized personal collections of Medal of Honor recipients from World War II through recent conflicts.

Diversity in Military Service

African Americans in the Military

  • Library of Congress — African Americans in the Military Primary source resources on African American military service across all conflicts, from the Revolutionary War through the present.
  • National Archives — U.S. Colored Troops 185,000 compiled military service records of USCT soldiers, officer records, family letters, and pension applications.

Native Americans in the Military

  • National Archives — Native American Military Service Federal records documenting Native American military service including Army Indian Scouts and Code Talkers, from the Revolutionary War through the present.
  • National Archives — Published Primary Sources on Native Americans Guide to published government documents relating to Native American military service and Indian–White relations.

Women in Military Service

  • Women in Military Service for America Memorial Memorial and archive repository for memorabilia, personal collections, and oral histories of American servicewomen from the Revolution to the present.
  • U.S. Army Women’s Museum Collections and materials documenting the history of women’s military service in the U.S. Army.

General Military Research

  • National Archives — Military Records Central hub for all military service records from the Revolutionary War to the present, covering Army, Navy, Marine Corps, Coast Guard, and Air Force.
  • National Archives Catalog Searchable database of 40+ million records including millions of military service records, photographs, maps, and documents across all branches and eras.
  • Library of Congress — Veterans History Project 121,000+ collections of oral histories, photographs, letters, and diaries from veterans of all conflicts since World War I.
