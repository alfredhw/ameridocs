Regional collections spanning Colorado, Idaho, Montana, Nevada, Utah, and Wyoming. See also individual state pages.

Regional Digital Portals

  • Mountain West Digital Library The primary regional hub: 1 million+ digitized resources from 699 collections across 164 partner institutions in Utah, Nevada, Idaho, Montana, and neighboring states. Serves as the DPLA service hub for the Mountain West.
  • Archives West 40,000+ finding aids from archives across Washington, Oregon, Montana, Idaho, Utah, and Wyoming: correspondence, diaries, photographs, and digital reproductions of manuscripts and archival materials.
  • Intermountain Histories Free website and mobile app curating histories of sites and events across Idaho, Wyoming, Montana, Nevada, Utah, Colorado, Arizona, and New Mexico, with photographs, video documentaries, and oral histories.
  • Montana Memory Project 66,000+ items from Montana cultural institutions: documents, photographs, letters, journals, maps, military records, livestock brands, audio files, and videos from dozens of collections.

The Mountain Fur Trade

  • Library of Fur Trade Historical Source Documents Primary source documents on the Rocky Mountain fur trade: William H. Ashley’s 1825 papers, Robert Campbell’s letters, James Clyman’s journal, and accounts of the Green River rendezvous system (1820s–1840s).
  • Alfred Jacob Miller Online Catalogue — Fur Trade Documentation of Miller’s paintings from the 1837 Green River rendezvous, with illustrations and primary source accounts of trappers, traders, and mountain men in the Rockies.

Mining Frontiers

  • Colorado Historic Newspapers Collection 7.5 million+ digitized pages from 1,000+ Colorado newspaper titles (1859–2025), with extensive coverage of gold and silver rushes, mining camps, and boomtown life.
  • History Colorado — Online Collection Artifacts, photographs, and archival materials on Colorado history with substantial holdings on mining, the Pikes Peak gold rush, and mineral extraction industries.
  • Archives West — Mines and Mining in Montana Photographs Photographs documenting Montana placer mining, hard rock mines, mills, and coal mines (1867–1920), including early views of Butte copper mines and smelters.
  • Nevada Historical Society — Online Collections Photographs, manuscripts, and library materials including Territorial Enterprise newspapers (1859–1891) and research materials on the Comstock Lode and Nevada mining.

The Transcontinental Railroad & Western Surveys

  • Library of Congress — Pacific Railway Act Digital Collections Manuscripts, newspaper articles, government documents, and railroad maps related to the 1862 Act and transcontinental construction through the Mountain West.
  • Library of Congress — Railroad Maps (1828–1900) 623 maps from the Geography and Map Division documenting railroad routes across the Mountain West, selected from 3,000+ maps in the collection.
  • USGS — The Four Great Surveys of the West Official documentation of the Hayden, Wheeler, King, and Powell surveys (1867–1879) that mapped the geology, resources, and topography of the Mountain West before USGS consolidation.
  • BYU — William Henry Jackson Collection Photographs from the Hayden Survey documenting Yellowstone, the Rockies, and western landscapes (1870s)—images that helped convince Congress to create Yellowstone National Park.

Native Nations of the Mountain West

  • Montana State University — Indian Peoples of the Northern Great Plains Primary sources on Assiniboine, Blackfeet, Cheyenne, Kootenai, Nez Perce, Salish, Shoshone, and Sioux nations of the Northern Rockies and adjacent Plains.
  • National Archives — American Indian Treaties All 377 ratified Indian treaties (1722–1869) digitized and freely available, including treaties with Mountain West tribes. (See also Native Nations.)

Mormon Settlement

  • The Joseph Smith Papers Complete scholarly edition of Joseph Smith’s documents, fully digitized with transcriptions. (See also Religious History.)
  • BYU — Overland Trails Database Emigrant diaries and journals including Mormon pioneer accounts of the trek to the Great Salt Lake Valley (1846–1869). (See also Westward Expansion.)

Public Lands & Conservation

  • Library of Congress — Act Establishing Yellowstone National Park Manuscripts, newspaper articles, government documents, and maps on the creation of the first national park (1872), including the campaign to protect Yellowstone from commercial exploitation.
  • Yellowstone Heritage and Research Center Over 6 million items documenting Yellowstone’s history and science, with selected materials digitized including photographs, maps, drawings, and management documents.

Water & Reclamation

  • USGS — John Wesley Powell, Report on the Lands of the Arid Region (1879) Powell’s landmark report proposing that western settlement follow watershed boundaries—a prescient analysis of water scarcity that shaped reclamation policy across the arid Mountain West.
  • Bureau of Reclamation — RISE Open Data Open data system for accessing water and water-related data across Mountain West reclamation projects: reservoir levels, streamflow, and irrigation records.

Major Research Collections

  • University of Utah — J. Willard Marriott Digital Library 400+ digital collections (1 million+ items) including Utah Digital Newspapers, Western Soundscape Archive, and Western Waters Digital Library covering the Mountain West.
  • BYU — Harold B. Lee Library Digital Collections Large Western Americana collection including George Beard’s 1,800+ photographs of Utah, Idaho, and Wyoming (1878–1928) and pioneer diaries and letters.
  • Denver Public Library — Digital Collections 1 million+ items: photographs, maps, documents, and architectural drawings on Denver and Colorado history with emphasis on mining, conservation, and western heritage.
  • Idaho State Archives — Digital Collections 80+ historic newspaper titles (1863–1925) and 800,000+ photographs documenting Idaho territorial and state history.
  • Wyoming State Archives — Digital Collections 500,000+ photographs of Wyoming and the Rocky Mountain region, digitized newspapers, and manuscript collections.
