Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • College of Charleston — Indigenous People in South Carolina Research guide documenting Catawba, Cherokee, Yamasee, and other South Carolina Indigenous nations with archival references.
  • Charleston Library Society — Colonial Native American Resources Research guide to colonial-era Native American documentation held in Charleston collections.

Colonial South Carolina (1663–1775)

  • Lowcountry Digital Library 200+ digitized collections from Charleston-area institutions: colonial manuscripts, trade records, and merchant documents.
  • South Carolina Historical Society — Archives Founded 1855; manuscripts, maps, and documents on European colonization, Atlantic commerce, and colonial governance.
  • Charleston Library Society — Digital Collections Founded 1748; includes John Locke's manuscript Fundamental Constitutions of Carolina, colonial newspapers, maps, and nautical charts.
  • Stono Rebellion (1739) — Two Views (National Humanities Center) Eyewitness accounts from Lt. Governor William Bull and other contemporary sources on the 1739 slave revolt.

Revolution & Early Republic

  • University of South Carolina — Digital Collections Correspondence, military records, and eyewitness accounts from Revolutionary War battles at Camden, Charleston, and Eutaw Springs.
  • Teaching American History — The Nullification Crisis Primary documents including Calhoun's South Carolina Exposition and Protest (1828) and Jackson's Proclamation (1832).
  • SC Digital Newspaper Program — Nullification Crisis Digitized newspaper accounts of the 1828–1833 nullification debates.
  • South Caroliniana Library (USC) Manuscript collections: family papers, business records, and broadsides from the Revolutionary and Early Republic periods.

Antebellum & Slavery

  • Lowcountry Digital History Initiative — Forgotten Fields Exhibition documenting inland rice plantation development (1700s–Civil War) with primary sources on plantation labor and enslaved life.
  • Denmark Vesey Trial Transcript (1822) — Library of Congress Digitized transcript of the Charleston court proceedings documenting the Denmark Vesey conspiracy with complete trial testimonies.
  • College of Charleston — Denmark Vesey Research Guide Links to trial transcripts, court reports, and scholarly annotated collections of Vesey-era documents.
  • Gullah Geechee Cultural Heritage Corridor National Heritage Area website with cultural history, oral traditions, and links to Penn Center educational resources on Gullah/Geechee culture.
  • Hidden Voices: Enslaved Women in the Lowcountry LDHI exhibition featuring enslaved women's narratives, cultural practices, and family records.
  • Avery Research Center — African American Collections 200+ manuscript collections: family papers predating the Civil War, freedom records, and antebellum free people of color documentation.

Secession & Civil War

  • Chronicling America — Battle of Fort Sumter Digitized newspaper accounts of the April 1861 bombardment from contemporary South Carolina and national press.
  • Genesis of the Civil War: The Story of Sumter (1887) — Library of Congress Digitized eyewitness account by Samuel Wylie Crawford with official reports on Fort Sumter.
  • 54th Massachusetts in Beaufort, SC (NPS) Primary source references including Corporal James Henry Gooding's correspondence and hospital records from the 1863 Fort Wagner campaign.
  • SC Dept. of Archives — Confederate Pension Applications 10,000+ digitized pension applications (1919–1938) with personal narratives and service records.

Reconstruction & Jim Crow

  • USC — Freedom’s Lawmakers: Black Leadership During Reconstruction Research guide to primary sources on South Carolina Reconstruction-era African American political leadership.
  • Hamburg Massacre (1876) — Zinn Education Project Links to the two-thousand-page official U.S. Senate investigation with raw testimony from Columbia hearings on the July 1876 massacre.
  • SCArchCat — South Carolina Archives Catalog Searchable catalog of state records including legislative journals (1692–), court records, executive papers, and treasury ledgers.

Civil Rights & Modern Era

  • Justice for All SC — Briggs v. Elliott Educational resource on the first school desegregation case (1951–1954) with primary sources on African American educational activism.
  • USC — Desegregation in South Carolina Schools Research guide to primary sources on desegregation litigation, resistance, and implementation.
  • SC ETV — Orangeburg Massacre (1968) Digitized press release and interviews with witnesses and participants in the February 8, 1968 shooting.
  • LDHI — Mother Emanuel Church Tribute Primary sources, oral histories, and archival materials related to the June 17, 2015 Charleston church shooting.

South Carolina Government & Law

  • SC Dept. of Archives & History — Digital Collections Official state repository with digitized pension applications, wills (1782–1855), and government records.
  • SCArchCat Online Catalog Searchable catalog of 350+ years of state, county, and municipal records including legislative journals from 1692.
  • South Carolina State Library — Digital Collections Digitized government documents, historical records, and educational resources.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • South Carolina Digital Library 200,000+ items from 40+ institutions: photographs, manuscripts, journals, oral histories, and objects organized by theme and era.
  • Historical Newspapers of South Carolina 300,000+ searchable pages of digitized South Carolina newspapers from 1815 to present.
  • Chronicling America — South Carolina Newspapers 125+ digitized SC newspapers (1756–1963) including ten African American newspapers (1865–1922).
  • USC — South Caroliniana Map Collection 300+ digitized historical maps (16th–20th centuries) of Native territories, battlefields, railroads, and plantations.
  • Lowcountry Digital Library 200 digitized collections focusing on Lowcountry and Atlantic World connections; partners include SC Historical Society and Avery Research Center.
  • University of South Carolina — Digital Collections Nearly 300 digital collections in partnership with South Caroliniana Library, SC Political Collections, and SCDAH.
  • South Carolina Encyclopedia Scholarly reference with linked primary source citations across all historical periods.
  • Library of Congress — South Carolina Guide Guide to LC digitized collections and external resources on South Carolina history.
