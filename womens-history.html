Colonial & Early Republic Women

  • Massachusetts Historical Society — Adams Family Correspondence The complete correspondence of Abigail and John Adams, including her famous 1776 “Remember the Ladies” letter, fully digitized and searchable.
  • Library of Congress — Mercy Otis Warren Papers Papers of the Revolutionary-era author, playwright, and political commentator, including her three-volume History of the Rise, Progress and Termination of the American Revolution (1805).
  • DPLA — Women in the American Revolution Primary sources on women’s contributions during the Revolution, including correspondence, diaries, and accounts of camp followers, spies, and domestic supporters.

Women’s Suffrage

Seneca Falls & the Early Movement

  • National Park Service — Declaration of Sentiments (1848) Full text of the founding document of the women’s rights movement, adopted at the Seneca Falls Convention, modeled on the Declaration of Independence.
  • Rutgers University — Elizabeth Cady Stanton & Susan B. Anthony Papers Digital edition of the Stanton and Anthony papers with correspondence, speeches, articles, and organizational records spanning 50 years of women’s rights activism.
  • Susan B. Anthony Museum & House — Research Resources Guides to Anthony’s papers with links to digital collections at the Library of Congress, Rutgers, Rochester, and the Schlesinger Library.

The National Campaign & the 19th Amendment

  • Library of Congress — NAWSA Records Over 26,700 items from the National American Woman Suffrage Association (1839–1961), including correspondence, speeches, organizational records, and state campaign materials.
  • Library of Congress — Women of Protest: Photographs from the Records of the National Woman’s Party Photographs documenting Alice Paul, the NWP, White House pickets, the “Night of Terror” at Occoquan Workhouse, and the militant wing of the suffrage movement.
  • National Archives — 19th Amendment (1920) High-resolution image, full text, and ratification history of the constitutional amendment guaranteeing women the right to vote.
  • DPLA — Women’s Suffrage: The Campaign for the 19th Amendment Curated primary sources including photographs, political cartoons, broadsides, and campaign materials from both pro- and anti-suffrage movements.

African American Women & Suffrage

  • Library of Congress — Shall Not Be Denied: Women Fight for the Vote Major exhibition with digitized primary sources highlighting the roles and exclusion of African American women in the suffrage movement, including Sojourner Truth, Ida B. Wells, and Mary Church Terrell.
  • Library of Congress — Mary Church Terrell Papers Papers of the suffragist, educator, and civil rights activist who co-founded the NAACP and the National Association of Colored Women.

Women & Labor

  • DPLA — The Lowell Mill Girls Primary sources on the young women workers in early New England textile mills, their labor activism, and the Lowell Offering literary magazine.
  • Cornell University — Triangle Shirtwaist Factory Fire (1911) Comprehensive archive on the fire that killed 146 workers, mostly young immigrant women, and galvanized workplace safety reform. (See also Labor & Industry.)
  • Library of Congress — Lewis Hine Child Labor Photographs Photographs documenting girls working in textile mills, canneries, fields, and domestic service, with Hine’s captions recording ages, hours, and working conditions.
  • DPLA — Rosie the Riveter and Working Women in World War II Primary sources on the millions of women who entered the workforce during WWII, including recruitment posters, photographs, and oral histories.

Reform Movements & Social Activism

  • Ramapo College — Jane Addams Digital Edition Comprehensive scholarly edition of Jane Addams’s writings, correspondence, speeches, and published works documenting the settlement house movement, Progressive-era reform, and peace activism.
  • NYU — Margaret Sanger Papers Project Digital edition of the papers of the birth control movement pioneer, with correspondence, published writings, and organizational records (1900–1966).
  • Library of Congress — Women Journalists Collection Papers of pioneering women journalists including investigative reporters, war correspondents, and editors who shaped American media.
  • DPLA — The Temperance Movement and Prohibition Primary sources on the women-led temperance movement, the WCTU, and the campaign for Prohibition, including Carry Nation’s activism and the 18th Amendment.

Women in the Civil Rights Movement

  • Duke University — SNCC Digital Gateway Primary documents highlighting the crucial roles of women in SNCC including Fannie Lou Hamer, Diane Nash, Ruby Doris Smith-Robinson, and Ella Baker’s grassroots organizing philosophy.
  • Library of Congress — Rosa Parks Papers Over 7,500 items including correspondence, writings, notes, and photographs from the civil rights icon, covering her activism from the Montgomery Bus Boycott through the 1990s.
  • American Archive of Public Broadcasting — Eyes on the Prize Interviews Over 300 raw footage interviews including extensive testimony from women activists in the civil rights movement.

Second-Wave Feminism & the Women’s Liberation Movement

  • Smith College — Sophia Smith Collection of Women’s History One of the nation’s premier women’s history archives with records of NOW, the National Women’s Political Caucus, Gloria Steinem Papers, and the Ms. Magazine Records.
  • Harvard Radcliffe Institute — Schlesinger Library World-renowned women’s history library holding over 3,500 collections of personal papers, organizational records, photographs, and audiovisual materials spanning the nineteenth century to the present.
  • DPLA — The Equal Rights Amendment Primary sources on the ERA from its 1923 introduction by Alice Paul through the ratification battles of the 1970s and 1980s, including campaign materials from both supporters and opponents.
  • National Archives — Civil Rights Act of 1964, Title VII Full text and legislative history of the landmark law that prohibited sex discrimination in employment, fundamentally changing women’s legal status in the workplace.

Reproductive Rights

  • NYU — Margaret Sanger Papers Project Papers documenting Sanger’s founding of the American Birth Control League (later Planned Parenthood) and the legal battles to legalize contraception.
  • Oyez — Roe v. Wade (1973) Complete oral argument audio, case summary, and opinion text for the landmark Supreme Court decision on abortion rights.
  • Smith College — Sophia Smith Collection: Reproductive Rights Records of reproductive rights organizations, women’s health activism, and the legal and political battles over birth control and abortion access.

Title IX & Education

  • National Archives — Title IX (1972) Full text and legislative history of the Education Amendments of 1972 prohibiting sex discrimination in federally funded education programs, transforming women’s access to athletics and education.
  • Women’s Sports Foundation — Title IX History Historical resources on the impact of Title IX on women’s athletics, including statistical data, oral histories, and legislative background.

Major Research Collections

  • Harvard Radcliffe Institute — Schlesinger Library on the History of Women in America 3,500+ collections spanning the history of women in America, with particular strengths in suffrage, feminism, women’s labor, reproductive rights, and culinary history.
  • Smith College — Sophia Smith Collection of Women’s History Internationally recognized collection of women’s history manuscripts including organizational records, personal papers, and audiovisual materials.
  • Library of Congress — American Women: Resources from the Library of Congress Comprehensive research guide to LOC’s women’s history resources across all divisions, including manuscript collections, photographs, maps, and government documents.
  • National Park Service — Women’s History Gateway to NPS sites, stories, and educational resources preserving women’s history across the national park system.
  • National Women’s History Museum — Digital Collections Online exhibits, oral histories, and educational resources covering the full sweep of American women’s history.
