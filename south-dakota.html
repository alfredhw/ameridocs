Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • Woksape Tipi Library & Archives (Oglala Lakota College) Tribal archives preserving Oglala Lakota history, genealogy, photographs, oral histories, and materials on Lakota culture and leadership from Pine Ridge.
  • American Indian Research Project (University of South Dakota) Over 2,300 oral history interviews with American Indians on the Northern Plains, conducted in English, Lakota, Dakota, and other Indigenous languages.
  • John Grabill Photograph Collection (Library of Congress) 188 photographs (1887–1892) documenting Lakota Sioux, gold rush, settlement, and contact with the U.S. military on Cheyenne River and Pine Ridge reservations.
  • Oglala Oral History Project (Archives West) Oral history records documenting Oglala Lakota community voices and historical narratives.

Exploration & Fur Trade

  • Journals of the Lewis and Clark Expedition (University of Nebraska) Nearly 5,000 pages of expedition journals with images and supplemental texts; covers South Dakota August–October 1804.
  • American Journeys: Eyewitness Accounts of Early American Exploration Digital library of searchable primary source documents on exploration and settlement, including fur trade narratives.
  • Library of Congress — Lewis and Clark Natural History Resources Curated online resources including journals, maps, and naturalist observations from the expedition.

Dakota Territory & Settlement (1861–1889)

  • South Dakota State Archives Microfilmed records of territorial Supreme Court, legislature, governors, and secretaries' correspondence (1861–1889).
  • South Dakota State Archives — Finding Aids (ArchivesSpace) Searchable finding aids for territorial government records, land records, and administrative documents.
  • South Dakota State Archives — Homestead Records Federal homestead entry records, township plats, survey records, and land transfer documentation (1862–1915).
  • Library of Congress — Homestead Act Primary Documents Federal land records, broadsides, government documents, and manuscripts related to Dakota Territory settlement.
  • National Archives — Dakota Territory Court Records U.S. Territorial District Court records from Dakota Territory (First through Fifth Districts, 1861–1889).

Lakota Wars & Wounded Knee (1860s–1890)

  • Fort Laramie Treaty of 1868 (National Archives) Full text and images of the original treaty recognizing the Black Hills as part of the Great Sioux Reservation.
  • DPLA — Wounded Knee Massacre Primary Source Set Documents, photographs, government reports, telegrams, and correspondence from 1890–1891 documenting the massacre and aftermath.
  • Chronicling America — Wounded Knee Documentation Historic newspapers from 1890–1891 reporting on the Ghost Dance, Wounded Knee Massacre, and military response.
  • NPS — Great Sioux War of 1876 Essays and primary source materials on the Black Hills War, Battle of the Little Bighorn, and treaty negotiations.
  • South Dakota State Historical Society — Military Records Military correspondence, battle reports, and government records related to Indian Wars and military operations in Dakota Territory.

Agriculture & Rural Life

  • South Dakota Agricultural Heritage Museum — Online Archives Digitized agricultural records, farm equipment documentation, and rural life materials from South Dakota's farming heritage.
  • South Dakota State Historical Society — Homesteading Materials Educational resources and primary source documents on the homesteading experience and settlement patterns.
  • Drawn to the Land: Homesteading Dakota Primary source materials and historical analysis of the Dakota Territory homesteading experience.
  • Library of Congress — Dust Bowl Primary Sources FSA photographs (1936–1944) of agricultural life during the Great Depression, with extensive South Dakota documentation.

20th Century & Modern Era

  • American Archive — 1973 Wounded Knee Occupation Primary sources documenting the AIM occupation of Wounded Knee, including contemporaneous footage and documents.
  • NMAI — Wounded Knee Occupation Records Photographs and materials from the 1973 occupation and its historical significance.
  • Minnesota Historical Society — American Indian Movement Archives AIM archival records including correspondence, organizational documents, and court records related to Wounded Knee (1973–1976).
  • U.S. Marshals Service — Incident at Wounded Knee Official federal government documentation and reports from the 1973 occupation siege.
  • Karl E. Mundt Archives (Digital Library of South Dakota) 1.9+ million documents from U.S. Senator Mundt's personal and political papers on 20th-century state politics.

South Dakota Government & Law

  • South Dakota State Historical Society — Archives Official state archives with government records, manuscripts, photographs, and historical documents with finding aids.
  • South Dakota State Archives — ArchivesSpace Comprehensive searchable database of government records, court documents, and administrative archives.
  • Library of Congress — South Dakota Guide Curated guide to South Dakota government records, legal documents, and official collections available digitally.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • South Dakota Digital Archives Premier digital repository: photographs, manuscripts, government documents, maps, and materials since 1891.
  • Digital Library of South Dakota Collaborative platform featuring materials from seven Board of Regents universities: photographs, manuscripts, audio, and video.
  • Chronicling America — South Dakota Newspapers 82 digitized South Dakota newspapers (1880s–1963) via the Library of Congress.
  • South Dakota State Historical Society — Newspapers Online Guide to digitized South Dakota newspapers dating back to 1880.
  • Northern State University Digital Collections Digitized newspapers, photographs, oral histories, and materials; specialized in Germans from Russia heritage.
  • Rapid City Public Library — Local Digital Archives Historical photographs, documents, and materials on Mount Rushmore construction, Native Americans, and Black Hills history.
  • Library of Congress — South Dakota Historical Maps Over 480 digitized historical maps from territorial and statehood periods showing settlement, boundaries, and development.
