Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • Ohio History Connection — Archaeology Collections Over 5,800 collections with 1 million objects spanning 15,000 years; 87,000+ archaeology records viewable online.
  • Wyandot Heritage Digital Archive Digital primary sources (17th–20th centuries) on Wyandot experience: treaties, diaries, correspondence, maps, and photographs.
  • Ohio Hopewell Project (University of Nebraska–Lincoln) Archival information on Hopewell archaeology from Field Museum collections: excavation documents, catalogs, and finding aids.
  • Digital Archaeological Record — Hopewell Sites Digital archaeological records for Hopewell and Adena mounds including historical field records and mapping.
  • Great Lakes–Ohio Valley Ethnohistory Collection (Indiana University) Written materials on Delaware, Wyandot, Shawnee, Miami, and other tribal groups from European contact through the Indian Claims Commission era.

Northwest Territory & Early Statehood (1787–1830s)

  • Ohio History Connection — State Archives Official state records including land records, military records, and legislative documents from Ohio's founding.
  • Ohio History Connection — ArchivesSpace Searchable descriptions and inventories of archival collections: state archives, manuscripts, and government records.
  • Ohio History Connection — Land Records Guide Comprehensive guide to land divisions, payment records, and property documents from the early settlement period.
  • Miami University — Walter Havighurst Special Collections Rare books and manuscripts on Old Northwest Territory history and transportation; 125,000+ volumes.

Antebellum & Civil War

  • Ohio Digital Network — Underground Railroad in Ohio Curated primary source set with teaching guides on Ohio's extensive Underground Railroad network.
  • Oberlin Sanctuary Project — Oberlin-Wellington Rescue Scholarly digital project integrating primary sources on the 1858 rescue of fugitive slave John Price.
  • Ohio University — Civil War Correspondence Over 1,000 letters, diaries, and documents from 12 manuscript collections: soldiers' accounts and home front correspondence.
  • Ohio History Connection — Civil War Resources Microfilm collections, Civil War Pension Index, regimental histories, and service records.
  • Cincinnati History Library & Archives Civil War correspondence and Cincinnati's role in antislavery movements.

Industrial Era & Labor (1870s–1920s)

  • Wilbur and Orville Wright Papers (Library of Congress) Approximately 10,121 items (49,084 digital images) spanning 1900–1940: diaries, notebooks, correspondence, and technical drawings.
  • Wright Brothers Collection (Wright State University) World's most complete collection: technical library, family papers, letters, diaries, financial records, and 2,000+ photographs.
  • Ohio Digital Network — Industry in Ohio Curated primary source collection on Ohio's industrial transformation: steel, rubber, and manufacturing.
  • Standard Oil of Ohio (SOHIO) Collections Manuscript and audiovisual materials from Standard Oil Company (Ohio).

20th Century

  • Kent State Shootings: May 4 Collection Over 750 cubic feet of materials: audio recordings, photographs, artworks, documents, and oral histories from May 4, 1970.
  • 1913 Dayton Flood Archival Collection (University of Dayton) Photographs, postcards, student magazines, original footage, and oral histories from the Great Flood of 1913.
  • Western Reserve Historical Society — Digital Collections Digital Cleveland History with images, audio, video, and texts on Northeast Ohio including African American archives.

Ohio Government & Law

  • Ohio History Connection — Collections Search Tools Primary gateway to state archives manuscripts, audiovisual materials, and government records.
  • Ohio History Connection — Local Government Records Guide to preserving and accessing county and municipal government records across Ohio.
  • Ohio History Journal Archive Fully searchable journal archive (1887–2004): peer-reviewed essays, research notes, and primary documents.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Ohio Memory 1,600,000+ images from 330+ institutions across all 88 Ohio counties: photographs, maps, newspapers, and documents.
  • Chronicling America — Ohio Newspapers 187 digitized Ohio newspapers (1834–1963), 520,000+ pages fully searchable.
  • Cincinnati & Hamilton County Public Library — Digital Library 1 million+ Cincinnati Enquirer photographs; Ohio Historical Newspapers (Cincinnati Post, Dayton Daily News, 1923–1986).
  • Ohio History Connection — Historic Atlases 440+ atlases containing 21,000 historical maps illustrating Ohio's county and community evolution.
  • Ohio State University — Digital Collections Billy Ireland Cartoon Library, Rare Books & Manuscripts, and University Archives digital materials.
  • Ohio University — Digital Archives Documentary photography, manuscripts, and local history collections from Southeast Ohio.
  • Library of Congress — Ohio Guide Comprehensive guide to Ohio digital resources in Library of Congress collections.
