Regional collections spanning Delaware, Maryland, New Jersey, New York, and Pennsylvania. See also individual state pages.

Regional Digital Portals

  • New York Heritage Digital Collections 275,000+ digitized books, manuscripts, maps, letters, photographs, and memorabilia from 350+ libraries, museums, archives, and cultural organizations across New York State.
  • Philadelphia Area Archives Research Portal Searchable descriptions of 5,000+ collections from 200+ regional institutions documenting Mid-Atlantic history across all subjects and time periods.

Dutch New Netherland & Colonial Heritage

  • New York State Archives — Dutch Colonial Records Digitization Project Digitized seventeenth-century Dutch administrative records documenting European colonization of New York, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Delaware, and Connecticut, with English translations of original documents.
  • New Netherland Institute — Digital Exhibitions “Voyages of New Netherland” and related exhibitions with translated Dutch colonial court records, council minutes, merchant correspondence, and ships’ journals from Dutch archives.
  • DPLA — Dutch New Netherland Curated primary sources on Dutch colonization featuring Adriaen van der Donck’s Description of New Netherland and colonial administrative documents with teaching guides.
  • American Philosophical Society — New Sweden Records Selected transcripts in Swedish and German of documents relating to the New Sweden colony in Delaware and Pennsylvania (1638–1680s): charter documents, governors’ instructions, and correspondence.

The Revolutionary War in the Mid-Atlantic

  • DPLA — Revolutionary War Turning Points: Saratoga and Valley Forge Curated primary sources including maps of Continental Army encampments, letters from military leaders, and documents from the crucial Mid-Atlantic campaigns of 1776–1778.
  • Valley Forge Muster Roll Project Digital project documenting every Continental Army soldier encamped at Valley Forge (1777–1778), with muster rolls and Philadelphia Campaign records.
  • Papers of the War Department (1784–1800) 42,000+ digitized documents from the U.S. Department of War, freely accessible, including Revolutionary-era military correspondence, reports, and administrative records.
  • Independence National Historical Park — Primary Sources Revolutionary-era primary sources from Philadelphia including documents on Black history, governance, and the founding of the republic (1776–1789).

The Immigration Gateway

  • Ellis Island Foundation — Passenger Search Free searchable database with digital images of 65+ million Ellis Island passenger records (1892–1957), the primary documentary record of mass immigration to America.
  • Castle Garden Online Database Free database of 11 million immigrants arriving in New York via Castle Garden (1820–1892), the predecessor to Ellis Island, with ship manifests and passenger lists.
  • Historical Society of Pennsylvania — Balch Institute Collections 75,000+ digitized images documenting 60+ ethnic groups through manuscripts, photographs, pamphlets, and organizational records, with emphasis on German, Irish, Italian, and Eastern European immigration to the Mid-Atlantic.
  • American Jewish Historical Society Thousands of digitized pages documenting American Jewish life from 1654 to the present, including immigration records, synagogue founding documents, and East Coast community materials from New York and Philadelphia.

The Erie Canal & Transportation Networks

  • New York State Library — Erie Canal Collections Digitized maps, construction plans, drawings, and administrative records from the canal that transformed American commerce after 1825, linking the Great Lakes to the Atlantic seaboard.
  • Erie Canal Museum — Collections Digitized artifacts, photographs, and documents from the Canal Society of New York State on canal construction, operations, and the communities that grew along its route.

The Industrial Corridor

  • Hagley Museum & Library — Digital Collections 300,000+ digitized images from one of the nation’s premier business history archives: DuPont Company records (1802–20th c.), trade catalogs, industrial photographs, and Delaware Valley business manuscripts.
  • Penn State — Pennsylvania Mines and Mining Digitized mining records including WPA-created mine maps (1934–1938) documenting bituminous coal operations across western Pennsylvania.
  • Indiana University of Pennsylvania — Coal Culture Exhibit Digitized primary sources and photographs from the Rochester & Pittsburgh Coal Company Records (1881–1998) documenting Pennsylvania coal mining culture and communities.

Philadelphia & New York as Cultural Centers

  • American Philosophical Society — Digital Library 14 million+ pages of manuscripts, 350,000 volumes, and 250,000 images including Benjamin Franklin papers (14,000 items), Declaration of Independence holograph, and Lewis and Clark journals.
  • New York Public Library — Digital Collections 755,000+ digitized images from rare books, manuscripts, maps, photographs, prints, and ephemera, with 180,000+ items in the public domain.
  • New-York Historical Society — Digital Collections Shelby White & Leon Levy Digital Library with 20,000+ linear feet of manuscripts: Revolutionary War documents, business records, and cultural materials spanning 200+ years.
  • Library Company of Philadelphia — Collections Rare books and manuscripts from America’s first lending library (est. 1731 by Benjamin Franklin), including colonial-era publications, German immigrant materials, and early American imprints.

Chesapeake & Delaware Bay

  • Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum — Bay History Digital resources on Chesapeake maritime history: Indigenous heritage, early English exploration, the Bay’s role in the Revolution and Civil War, and the region’s commercial fishing and oystering traditions.
  • Maryland State Archives — Digital Resources Over 10 million digitized images from 110+ record series including wills, land records, estate records, maritime navigation documents, and special collections.
  • Delaware Public Archives — Digital Collections 2 million+ records including government documents, 800,000+ photographs from 1850 to the present, and historical materials documenting the Delaware Bay and Mid-Atlantic coastal region.

Major Research Collections

  • Historical Society of Pennsylvania — Digital Library 60,000+ digitized images from 21 million records spanning 350 years: Cadwalader Family Papers, early Philadelphia photographs, maps, and extensive manuscript collections on Mid-Atlantic history.
  • Gilder Lehrman Institute of American History 87,000+ items including William Penn documents, colonial Mid-Atlantic materials, and transcribed primary sources with thematic finding aids and educational resources.
  • Library of Congress — American Folklife Center 4,000+ collections including the Pinelands Folklife Project (New Jersey), occupational folklife documentation, and field recordings from across the Mid-Atlantic region.
