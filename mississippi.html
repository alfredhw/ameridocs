Native Peoples: Choctaw, Chickasaw & Natchez

  • University of Mississippi — Native American Primary Sources Guide to primary source materials on Chickasaw, Choctaw, and Natchez peoples with links to manuscript collections.
  • University of Mississippi — Native American Manuscript Collections Indexed manuscript collections covering the Chickasaw, Choctaw, and Natchez nations in Mississippi.

Cotton Kingdom & Slavery

  • Library of Congress — Born in Slavery: Mississippi Narratives First-person accounts from formerly enslaved people in Mississippi, collected by the Federal Writers’ Project (1936–1938), with photographs and transcripts.
  • Mississippi Dept. of Archives & History — Ex-Slave Narratives (1936–1940) Typed and handwritten transcripts of interviews from the Federal Writers’ Project, held by the state archives.
  • University of Mississippi — Antebellum Mississippi Primary Sources Guide to primary sources on slavery, cotton, and plantation life in antebellum Mississippi.

Civil War: The Vicksburg Campaign

  • Library of Congress — Seth J. Wells Diary (Siege of Vicksburg) Complete digitized diary of a Union soldier during the siege, including weeks of preparation and occupation.
  • Library of Congress — Vicksburg Campaign Maps Detailed digitized maps of the siege of Vicksburg from the Geography and Map Division.
  • University of Mississippi — Civil War Primary Sources Comprehensive guide to Mississippi Civil War primary sources and manuscript collections.

Reconstruction & Redemption

  • University of Mississippi — Reconstruction Primary Sources Guide to Reconstruction-era primary sources including Freedmen’s Bureau records, political documents, and personal papers.

Jim Crow & the Mississippi Sovereignty Commission

  • Mississippi Dept. of Archives & History — Sovereignty Commission Online Searchable database of the Mississippi State Sovereignty Commission’s surveillance records (1956–1973), a state-funded agency that spied on civil rights activists. Records indexed by name.
  • Equal Justice Initiative — Lynching in America Report documenting 581 racial terror lynchings in Mississippi with case details, part of a broader national accounting.

Civil Rights Movement

Emmett Till (1955)

  • Library of Congress — The Murder of Emmett Till Primary sources and oral histories on the lynching of 14-year-old Emmett Till in Money, Mississippi, from the Civil Rights History Project.

James Meredith & Ole Miss Integration (1962)

  • University of Mississippi — James Meredith Collection Digitized letters, telegrams, and correspondence received by James Meredith during the 1962 integration crisis at the University of Mississippi.
  • University of Mississippi — Western Union Telegrams Digitized telegrams sent during August–October 1962 surrounding the integration crisis.
  • JFK Presidential Library — Ole Miss Integration Letters, recorded telephone conversations, and images documenting President Kennedy’s role in the integration crisis.

Medgar Evers

  • Mississippi History Now — Medgar Evers and Civil Rights in Mississippi Historical article with primary source links on the NAACP field secretary assassinated in Jackson in 1963.

Freedom Summer (1964)

  • SNCC Digital Gateway — Primary Sources Digital archive of Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee organizing documents, including Freedom Summer voter registration materials, letters, and photographs.

Mississippi Delta Blues

  • University of Mississippi — Blues Archive Research Guide Guide to the Blues Archive at Ole Miss, which holds 60,000+ sound recordings, 20,000+ photographs, and 1,000+ videos documenting the Delta blues tradition.
  • Mississippi Delta National Heritage Area — Blues Culture Information on 175 Blues Trail markers statewide documenting the people, places, and music of the Mississippi blues.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Mississippi Dept. of Archives & History — Digital Archives Main portal to all MDAH digital collections, fully searchable, including state government records, photographs, and manuscripts.
  • Mississippi Digital Library Statewide aggregator searching digital collections from 50+ Mississippi institutions.
  • University of Mississippi — eGrove Repository Institutional repository with thousands of digitized manuscripts, photographs, and documents on Mississippi history and culture.
  • Mississippi State University — Digital Collections Digitized materials from MSU including university archives, yearbooks, and special collections.
  • Mississippi Encyclopedia (Center for Mississippi History) Peer-reviewed reference with primary source links covering all aspects of Mississippi history.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Mississippi Newspapers (Library of Congress) 257 digitized Mississippi newspaper titles with 238,000+ pages, fully searchable.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Mississippi Aggregated search across Mississippi cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
