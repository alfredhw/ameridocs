Early Labor & Industrialization

  • Library of Congress — Printed Ephemera Collection Broadsides, handbills, and printed materials including early labor organizing circulars, strike notices, and workers’ appeals from the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries.
  • DPLA — The Lowell System and the Market Revolution Primary sources on the Lowell textile mills and the “mill girls,” documenting the transformation from agrarian to industrial labor in early nineteenth-century America.
  • Library of Congress — Lewis Hine Photographs: Child Labor Over 5,100 photographs taken by Lewis Hine for the National Child Labor Committee (1908–1924), documenting children working in mines, mills, factories, farms, and on the streets.

Railroad Workers

  • Stanford University — Chinese Railroad Workers in North America Project Recovering the experiences of 15,000–20,000 Chinese workers who built the Central Pacific Railroad through archaeological evidence, primary documents, and descendant interviews.
  • DPLA — The Pullman Strike of 1894 Primary sources on the nationwide Pullman railroad strike led by Eugene Debs and the American Railway Union, the federal injunction that broke it, and its impact on labor law.
  • West Virginia Division of Culture and History — Labor History Resources Primary sources on the West Virginia Mine Wars including the Paint Creek–Cabin Creek Strike (1912–1913), the Battle of Blair Mountain (1921), and other coal mining conflicts.
  • Library of Congress — Mining Photographs (Lewis Hine) Lewis Hine’s photographs of child laborers in Pennsylvania and West Virginia coal mines, coal breakers, and mining communities.

Garment & Textile Workers

  • Cornell University — Triangle Factory Fire (1911) Comprehensive digital archive on the Triangle Shirtwaist Factory fire that killed 146 workers: testimonies, photographs, building plans, trial records, and labor reform documents from the Kheel Center for Labor-Management Documentation.
  • Cornell Kheel Center — ILGWU Records Records of the International Ladies’ Garment Workers’ Union, one of the largest American labor unions, documenting decades of organizing in the garment industry.
  • DPLA — The 1913 Paterson Silk Strike Primary sources on the IWW-organized strike of 25,000 silk workers in Paterson, New Jersey, including the famous Madison Square Garden pageant.

The IWW & Radical Labor

  • University of Washington — IWW History Project Digitized materials on the Industrial Workers of the World including newspapers, pamphlets, photographs, and organizing materials from the Pacific Northwest and beyond.
  • University of Washington — IWW Encyclopedic History Comprehensive resource on the “Wobblies” with free-speech fights, timber worker organizing, and the suppression of the IWW during World War I.
  • DPLA — The Haymarket Affair (1886) Primary sources on the Haymarket bombing, trial, and executions in Chicago that galvanized the international labor movement and led to May Day observances worldwide.

The AFL, CIO & Organized Labor

  • Library of Congress — AFL Records (1883–1925) Over 172,300 items including correspondence, convention proceedings, and organizational records of the American Federation of Labor under Samuel Gompers and William Green.
  • Cornell Kheel Center — Labor Collections Records from dozens of unions and labor organizations, plus arbitration records, collective bargaining agreements, and labor-management documentation.

The New Deal & Labor Reform

  • Library of Congress — FSA/OWI Photographs 164,000 photographs by Dorothea Lange, Walker Evans, Gordon Parks, and others documenting Depression-era workers, migrant labor, and New Deal programs (1935–1945).
  • The Living New Deal Interactive map and database documenting over 15,000 New Deal projects across the United States, from public works to arts programs, with photographs and historical context.
  • National Archives — National Labor Relations Act (1935) Full text and history of the Wagner Act, which guaranteed workers’ right to organize, bargain collectively, and strike.
  • Library of Congress — Federal Writers’ Project Life Histories Over 2,900 first-person accounts of ordinary Americans collected by WPA writers (1936–1940), many documenting working conditions, labor organizing, and industrial life.

Agricultural & Farm Labor

  • Bracero History Archive 150+ oral histories, photographs, documents, and artifacts from the Bracero guest worker program (1942–1964), a collaboration of George Mason University, the Smithsonian, Brown University, and UT El Paso.
  • DPLA — United Farm Workers and the Delano Grape Strike Primary sources on the 1965–1970 Delano grape strike and boycott led by Cesar Chavez and Dolores Huerta, including photographs, union publications, and government documents.
  • UC San Diego — Farmworker Movement Documentation Project First-person accounts and documents from participants in the farmworker movement, with essays from over 100 volunteers, organizers, and strikers.
  • Library of Congress — FSA/OWI Migrant Worker Photographs Iconic images of Dust Bowl migrants and agricultural workers by Dorothea Lange, including “Migrant Mother,” documenting the human cost of agricultural displacement.

Labor & Civil Rights

  • Stanford University — Martin Luther King, Jr. Research and Education Institute King’s writings and speeches on labor justice, including the 1968 Memphis sanitation workers’ strike where King was assassinated while supporting striking Black workers.

Major Research Collections

  • Cornell University — Kheel Center for Labor-Management Documentation & Archives Over 5,000 linear feet of labor union records, arbitration files, collective bargaining agreements, and immigration labor materials at ILR School.
  • U.S. Department of Labor — History Agency history, landmark legislation timelines, and historical publications documenting federal labor policy from the establishment of the Bureau of Labor Statistics (1884) to the present.
  • Library of Congress — Labor History Research Guide Comprehensive guide to LOC’s labor history holdings across the Manuscript Division, Prints & Photographs, and the American Folklife Center.
