Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • Dawnland Voices — Indigenous New England Digital Collections Collaborative archive featuring tribal collections with letters, stories, petitions, and photographs from Wampanoag, Nipmuc, and other New England Indigenous nations.
  • Massachusetts Historical Society — Native American Collections Missionary correspondence, Cyprian Southack letters on Indigenous relations, and Josiah Cotton memoirs documenting Wampanoag missions.
  • Pilgrim Hall Museum — Indigenous Documents Digitized documents illuminating Wampanoag culture, language, and continuous presence in the Plymouth area.
  • American Antiquarian Society — Algonquian Language Collections Digital exhibition of 17th-century books printed in Algonquian language, including Wampanoag and Nipmuc materials.

Plymouth Colony & Early Settlement (1620–1690)

  • Plimoth Patuxet Museums — Primary Sources Access to William Bradford's manuscript, early colonial documents, and Wampanoag-settler records.
  • MayflowerHistory.com — Primary Sources Portal to foundational Mayflower voyage and settlement documents including Bradford's Of Plimoth Plantation and Mourt's Relation.
  • Pilgrim Hall Museum — Library Archives America's oldest continuously operating public museum, holding Pilgrim possessions, documents, and recruitment records.
  • Chronicles of the Pilgrim Fathers (Library of Congress) Digitized original records and contemporary printed documents of the Pilgrim colony, 1602–1625.
  • Norman B. Leventhal Map Center — 17th-Century New England Digitized cartographic materials depicting early Plymouth Colony and 17th-century New England settlement patterns.

Colonial Massachusetts (1690–1770)

  • Salem Witch Trials Documentary Archive (University of Virginia) Comprehensive electronic collection with transcribed court records, examinations, depositions, warrants, and execution documents from 1692–1693.
  • Salem Witch Trials: Original Court Records (Mass.gov) Official state repository for pre-trial examinations, arrest warrants, and execution documents.
  • Peabody Essex Museum — Salem Witch Trials Collection Digitized original 1692 documents, legal records, and related materials.
  • Congregational Library & Archives — Salem Witchcraft Trials Research Guide Curated guide with links to primary sources, bibliographies, and online projects.
  • Massachusetts Historical Society — Colonial Collections Foundational holdings of colonial diaries, letterbooks, legal documents, and official records.
  • Harvard Library — Colonial North America Digital Collections Digitized 17th–18th century manuscripts including colonial governance records, land transactions, and merchant papers.

Revolution & Independence (1770–1789)

  • Adams Family Papers (Massachusetts Historical Society) Premier collection including John Adams's papers, correspondence with Abigail Adams, diaries, and Declaration of Independence manuscripts.
  • Adams Papers Digital Edition Fully searchable digital edition of previously printed Adams Papers volumes with consolidated index.
  • Founders Online — The Adams Papers (National Archives) Free online repository of Adams family documents searchable by date and topic.
  • DPLA — Boston Tea Party Primary Sources Curated set including Samuel Adams letters, newspaper coverage, and merchant accounts of the 1773 tea destruction.
  • Massachusetts Historical Society — Digital Collections Digitized manuscripts including Paul Revere's firsthand account of his midnight ride and Revolutionary correspondence.

Early Republic & Antebellum (1789–1860)

  • Tsongas Industrial History Center — Lowell Mills Primary Sources Mill girl letters, documents, photographs, and the “World of Barilla Taylor” documenting factory labor experiences.
  • Lowell Offering Archive (UMass Lowell) Digital access to the 1840–1841 periodical written entirely by mill operatives with firsthand accounts of factory work.
  • Gilder Lehrman Institute — Lowell Mill Girls Primary source documents with scholarly context on mill girls' labor, wages, and social reform movements.
  • Boston Public Library — Abolitionist Collections Extensive holdings of antislavery movement documents, abolitionist tracts, and correspondence from Massachusetts reformers.
  • Digital Commonwealth Statewide collaborative aggregation of items related to 19th-century Massachusetts reform movements including transcendentalism and abolition.

Civil War & Gilded Age

  • 54th Massachusetts Regiment (National Park Service) Official repository and guide to 54th Massachusetts Infantry Regiment documents, letters, and military records.
  • Written in Glory — 54th Massachusetts Resources Regimental documents, soldier letters, and historical resources on African American Civil War service.
  • Gilder Lehrman Institute — 54th Massachusetts Collection Featured documents including soldier letters on pay discrimination against Black troops.
  • Boston Public Library — Civil War Collections Regimental records, soldier correspondence, and home front documents from Massachusetts participants.

20th Century

  • John F. Kennedy Presidential Library & Museum Over 8 million pages of JFK papers, photographs, scrapbooks, and interactive exhibits on the Kennedy presidency.
  • Digital Commonwealth — 20th-Century Massachusetts Aggregated holdings from 220 institutions: photographs, documents, and records of immigration, labor, and social movements.
  • Boston Public Library — Labor Movement Archives Manuscript collections documenting Massachusetts labor union history and workers' movements.

Massachusetts Government & Law

  • Massachusetts Archives Digital Repository Digitized government records from the colonial period to present, including legislative materials and official documents.
  • State Library Digital Collections (Mass.gov) Massachusetts Acts and Resolves (1692–2010), legislative reports, and state government publications.
  • Massachusetts Historical Legal Documents (Mass.gov) Historical legal statutes, constitutional documents, and judicial records from colonial to modern era.
  • Records of the Governor and Company of Massachusetts Bay Full-text searchable records of colonial governance and Massachusetts Bay Company proceedings.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Digital Commonwealth 431,000+ items from 220 institutions: photographs, maps, manuscripts, and documents across all periods of Massachusetts history.
  • Chronicling America — Massachusetts Newspapers Digitized historic Massachusetts newspapers (1756–1963) including the Liberator, Boston Gazette, and Springfield Republican.
  • Norman B. Leventhal Map & Education Center 10,000+ digitized historical maps including Boston urban atlases, Revolutionary War-era maps, and the Atlascope project.
  • Massachusetts Historical Society — Digital Collections 6,000+ digitized items from 13 million holdings spanning the full breadth of Massachusetts history.
  • Boston Public Library — Rare Books & Manuscripts Digitized John Adams Library, medieval manuscripts, early English playbooks, and rare Massachusetts documents.
  • Collections of the Massachusetts Historical Society (Internet Archive) 79 digitized volumes (1792–1939) with full-text search and cumulative indices.
  • American Antiquarian Society — Digital Resources Digital exhibitions and photographic collections of 17th–18th century Massachusetts structures and artifacts.
  • Harvard Digital Collections 6+ million digitized objects including colonial manuscripts, maps, and photographs from Baker Library and Houghton Library.
