Native Peoples: Shoshone, Arapaho, Crow & Lakota/Cheyenne

  • American Heritage Center — Native American Collections Extensive holdings including the Richard Throssel photographs (2,481 images of Crow and Northern Cheyenne life, 1902–1920s), Baker and Johnston studio portraits of Chief Washakie and other leaders, and John Roberts papers from the Wind River Reservation.
  • WyoHistory.org — Indigenous People of Wyoming Free encyclopedia entries on indigenous peoples in Wyoming and the West, with primary source references and historical context.

Fur Trade & the Mountain Men

  • Virtual Library of Fur Trade Historical Source Documents Transcribed primary source documents from the fur trade era (1820s–1840s), including accounts from mountain men and records of the Rocky Mountain rendezvous gatherings held in Wyoming.
  • WyoHistory.org — Fur Trade in Wyoming Articles on fur trade history, mountain men, and rendezvous sites in Wyoming with links to primary sources.
  • Alfred Jacob Miller Online Catalogue — Rocky Mountain Fur Trade Visual primary sources documenting the Rocky Mountain fur trade and rendezvous era through Miller’s paintings and sketches from the 1837 expedition.

Overland Trails: Oregon, California & Mormon Trails

  • National Park Service — Mormon Pioneer Trail: Wyoming Historical context and trail resources for the Mormon Trail through Wyoming, including Independence Rock, Martin’s Cove, and other significant sites.
  • Bureau of Land Management — Wyoming Historic Trails Map Historic trails map highlighting the Oregon, California, Mormon, Pony Express, and Overland trails through Wyoming.
  • Wyoming State Historic Preservation Office — Western Trails Digital collections of trail-related documents and materials from the overland migration era.

Women’s Suffrage: The “Equality State” (1869)

  • Library of Congress — Wyoming Women’s Suffrage Act (1869) Full text of the historic 1869 legislation making Wyoming Territory the first government in the United States to grant women the right to vote and hold office.
  • Wyoming State Library — Women’s Suffrage in Wyoming Comprehensive guide to Wyoming women’s suffrage materials with links to primary and secondary sources, including biographical information on key suffragists.
  • National Park Service — Wyoming Women’s History Historical article on Wyoming women’s pioneering role in the suffrage movement and the territory’s refusal to enter the Union without women’s voting rights.

Yellowstone National Park (1872)

  • National Archives — Act Establishing Yellowstone (1872) Full text and facsimile of the 1872 Act signed by President Grant establishing Yellowstone as the world’s first national park.
  • Library of Congress — Yellowstone Research Guide Comprehensive research guide with links to digital primary source materials on Yellowstone’s establishment, including the Hayden Survey reports and William Henry Jackson photographs that convinced Congress to protect the area.

Johnson County War (1892)

  • Archives West — Johnson County War Collection Comprehensive collection (1892–1998) of materials relating to the Johnson County War, including court records, manuscripts, and contemporary newspaper accounts of the cattlemen’s invasion.
  • WyoHistory.org — The Johnson County War Detailed article on the 1892 invasion of northern Wyoming by cattle barons against alleged rustlers, with historical context and references to primary sources.

Cattle Ranching & Cowboy Culture

  • Archives West — Wyoming Stock Growers Association Records Records (1857–1987) including correspondence, brand books, roundup records, photographs, and artifacts documenting the Wyoming cattle industry from the open-range era to the twentieth century.
  • WyoHistory.org — The Wyoming Cattle Boom Article on the rise and collapse of the Wyoming cattle boom (1868–1886), including the devastating winter of 1886–1887.

Japanese American Incarceration: Heart Mountain

  • Heart Mountain — Digital Resources & Archives Official digital collections including documents, photographs, paintings, sketches, the Heart Mountain charter, and a downloadable roster of incarcerated individuals from the Heart Mountain Relocation Center (1942–1945).
  • University of Wyoming — Heart Mountain Records Digital reproductions of Heart Mountain records including the camp charter, community minutes, resettlement plans, trial transcripts, and documents in Japanese.
  • Densho Digital Repository Oral histories, photographs, and documents on Japanese American incarceration, including Heart Mountain materials and the story of the Heart Mountain Fair Play Committee draft resisters.

Energy: Coal, Oil & Uranium

  • Wyoming State Geological Survey — Energy Overview Documentation of Wyoming’s oil, natural gas, coal, and uranium resources and production history—the state leads the nation in coal production and has been a major energy producer since the late nineteenth century.
  • American Heritage Center — Digital Collections Materials from one of the nation’s largest non-governmental archives (95,000+ cubic feet), including energy industry records, uranium mining oral histories, and coal company papers.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Wyoming State Archives — Digital Collections Over 500,000 historical photographs, nearly 340 historic newspapers (1849–1923) with 800,000+ pages, legislative records, and trademarks.
  • American Heritage Center (University of Wyoming) 95,000+ cubic feet of archival materials across 5,000 collections covering Wyoming, the American West, industry, environment, and military history, with growing digital access.
  • WyoHistory.org (Wyoming State Historical Society) Free online historical encyclopedia with articles, essays, oral histories, and field trips, with links to primary source documents throughout.
  • University of Wyoming — Wyoming Digital Repository Open-access repository for digitized materials from Emmett D. Chisum Special Collections, general collections, and partner institutions.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Wyoming Newspapers (Library of Congress) Digitized Wyoming newspaper titles, fully searchable.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Wyoming Aggregated search across Wyoming cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA via the Plains to Peaks Collective.
