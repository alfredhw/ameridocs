Taíno Peoples & Pre-Columbian History

  • Library of Congress — Puerto Rican Collection (1591–1900) Spanish colonial government documents including orders, decrees, military records, baptismal records, and slavery documentation spanning the entire colonial period, with materials on pre-Columbian and early contact-era Puerto Rico.

Spanish Colonial Era (1493–1898)

  • Archivo General de Puerto Rico — Digital Collections 3,653 items across 19 collections with approximately 191,557 digitized images, including Governor’s Office records (1857–2015), the Agustín Stahl botanical collection, and music scores from the colonial and early American periods.
  • Archivo Digital Nacional de Puerto Rico Searchable portal integrating hundreds of thousands of digitized document pages from archives and libraries in Puerto Rico and abroad, featuring newspapers, government gazettes, maps, and archival records.
  • Library of Congress — Puerto Rico at the Dawn of the Modern Age (1831–1929) Eleven monographs and 41 political pamphlets covering agriculture, economics, education, government, politics, history, and literature from the late colonial and early American periods.

Spanish-American War & U.S. Acquisition (1898)

  • Library of Congress — World of 1898: Puerto Rican Perspective Comprehensive resource on Puerto Rico during the Spanish-American War, including documents on the U.S. acquisition, military government, peace agreements, and contemporary perspectives from Puerto Rican leaders.

Migration to the Mainland

  • Digital Public Library of America — Puerto Rican Migration to the U.S. Primary source set with photographs, maps, and reports documenting the Great Migration (1945–1960), including materials on Operation Bootstrap, airline routes, agricultural labor programs, and urban settlement patterns.
  • Centro de Estudios Puertorriqueños (CUNY Hunter College) The only archival repository in the U.S. devoted exclusively to stateside Puerto Ricans, with 100,000+ photographs, 4,000+ audio/video recordings, and 2,000+ art posters documenting Puerto Rican communities on the mainland.
  • Centro — ArchivesSpace Finding Aids Online finding aids for over 300 collections of personal papers and organizational records covering the Puerto Rican diaspora.

Political Status & Vieques

  • Library of Congress — Vieques Island Protests Research guide with archival documents, congressional reports, photographs, and audio materials on the Vieques naval base protests (1977–1983 and 1999–2003) and community resistance.
  • Freedom Archives — Puerto Rico Collections Over 12,000 hours of audio and video recordings plus print materials (late 1960s–mid 1990s) documenting independence activism and social movements in Puerto Rico.

Folklife, Music & Culture

  • Library of Congress — Puerto Rico Folklife American Folklife Center collections spanning nearly a century documenting expressive culture from throughout Puerto Rico and the diaspora, including Alan Lomax’s 1938 field recordings.
  • Library of Congress — FSA/OWI Color Photographs (Puerto Rico) Approximately 1,600 color photographs documenting rural life and farm labor in Puerto Rico (1939–1944), including work by photographer Jack Delano. Public domain.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Library of Congress — Puerto Rico Digital Collections Guide Central research guide providing access to multiple LOC digital collections on Puerto Rico, including manuscripts, photographs, maps, government documents, and pamphlets.
  • Universidad de Puerto Rico — Biblioteca Digital Puertorriqueña Digital collections including El Mundo newspaper photographs, editorial cartoons, labor movement documents, and Santiago Iglesias Pantín Worker Documentation Center materials.
  • Digital Library of the Caribbean — UPR Collections Portal providing access to University of Puerto Rico materials including El Mundo newspaper (1919–1990) and other Caribbean digital collections.
  • Rutgers University — Puerto Rico Archival Collaboration Collaborative digitization project with eight institutions across Puerto Rico, creating digital archives and finding aids for collections on Puerto Rican history.

Newspapers & General Collections
