Lenape Peoples & Early Contact

  • New Jersey Historical Commission — The Lenape Overview and primary source references on the Lenape (Delaware) people who inhabited New Jersey before European colonization.
  • Library of Congress — New Jersey Primary Source Set Curated primary sources on New Jersey history from pre-colonial through modern eras, including materials on the Lenape.

Colonial New Jersey: East & West Jersey

  • New Jersey State Library — Digital Collections Digitized New Jersey government documents, legislative proceedings, and historical publications from the colonial era through statehood.
  • New Jersey State Archives Official repository for colonial-era records including East and West Jersey proprietors’ documents, land records, and early governance materials.

Revolutionary War: Trenton, Princeton & Washington’s Crossing

  • National Archives — Founders Online Searchable correspondence of George Washington, including letters and orders from the pivotal New Jersey campaigns: the crossing of the Delaware (December 1776) and the battles of Trenton and Princeton.
  • Library of Congress — Washington Crossing the Delaware (Map) Period maps and visual materials documenting Washington’s crossing of the Delaware River and the surprise attack on Hessian forces at Trenton.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Battles of Trenton and Princeton Curated primary source set with maps, letters, and documents from the turning-point battles of the American Revolution in New Jersey.

Industrial Revolution: Paterson & the Silk City

  • National Park Service — Paterson Great Falls National Historical Park Historical materials on Alexander Hamilton’s vision for Paterson as America’s first planned industrial city (1791) and its evolution into the “Silk City” of the nineteenth century.
  • NJ Digital Highway Statewide digital portal aggregating primary sources from New Jersey libraries, museums, and archives, with strong holdings on Paterson’s industrial and labor history.

1913 Paterson Silk Strike

  • NYU Tamiment Library — IWW Records Industrial Workers of the World records including materials on the 1913 Paterson silk strike, one of the most significant labor actions of the Progressive Era.
  • Library of Congress — Bain News Service Collection Press photographs from the early twentieth century, including images of the 1913 Paterson strike and the famous Madison Square Garden pageant organized by John Reed.

Thomas Edison & Menlo Park

  • Library of Congress — Edison Company Films and Sound Recordings 341 motion pictures (1891–1918) and sound recordings from Edison’s New Jersey laboratories, including some of the earliest motion pictures ever produced.
  • Rutgers University — Thomas A. Edison Papers Digital edition of Edison’s papers with over 5 million pages of documents, including laboratory notebooks, correspondence, and legal records from Menlo Park and West Orange.

Ellis Island & Immigration

  • Statue of Liberty–Ellis Island Foundation — Passenger Search Searchable database of 65+ million passenger arrival records at Ellis Island and the Port of New York (1820–1957).
  • National Park Service — Ellis Island History & Culture Historical documents, photographs, and oral histories from Ellis Island, which processed over 12 million immigrants in New York Harbor just off the New Jersey shore.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • New Jersey State Archives Official state repository holding colonial records, Revolutionary War materials, legislative documents, vital records, and government correspondence.
  • NJ Digital Highway Statewide aggregator of digitized primary sources from 40+ New Jersey cultural heritage institutions.
  • Rutgers University — Special Collections & University Archives Primary source collections at New Jersey’s flagship public university, including New Jersey political papers, social activism records, and the Sinclair New Jersey Collection.
  • Princeton University — Finding Aids Searchable finding aids for Princeton’s extensive archival holdings, including Mudd Manuscript Library collections on American public policy and diplomacy.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — New Jersey Newspapers (Library of Congress) Digitized New Jersey newspaper titles, fully searchable.
  • Digital Public Library of America — New Jersey Aggregated search across New Jersey cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
