Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • Texas Beyond History (University of Texas) Archaeological records and educational materials on Caddo, Karankawa, Comanche, and other Texas Native peoples.
  • Portal to Texas History — Indian Tribes in Texas Digitized primary materials on Texas Native American tribes, cultures, and pre-contact history.
  • Texas Archival Resources Online (TARO) — Native American Resources Finding aids across Texas museums and archives documenting Indigenous peoples.

Spanish Colonial Era (1519–1821)

  • Béxar Archives Online (Dolph Briscoe Center) 300,000 pages of Spanish colonial manuscripts (1717–1821) including mission, presidio, and administrative records with English translations.
  • Texas General Land Office — Spanish Land Grant Records Original Spanish and Mexican land titles and survey documents dating to 1720.
  • Portal to Texas History — Spanish Colonial Period Primary source sets for Spanish colonial exploration and mission settlement.
  • University of Texas Rio Grande Valley — Spanish Land Grants Primary resources on the Spanish colonial land distribution system in South Texas.
  • Texas A&M–San Antonio — San Antonio Missions Primary Documents Curated collection of Spanish mission primary sources and related institutional materials.

Mexican Texas & Revolution (1821–1836)

  • Moses and Stephen F. Austin Papers (Portal to Texas History) Personal and official records of the Austin colonization enterprises and settler administration.
  • Gilder Lehrman Institute — Stephen Austin’s Contract (1825) Primary document of Austin's colonization contract for bringing settlers to Mexican Texas.
  • DPLA — Texas Revolution Primary Sources Curated collection including Alamo prints, the Treaty of Velasco, and the Constitution of the Republic of Texas.
  • Dolph Briscoe Center — Texas Revolution Resources Subject guide to extensive collections on the Revolution, the Alamo, and San Jacinto.
  • Texas State Library — Texas 175 Exhibits Pivotal documents from Texas history including Revolution-era declarations, treaties, and founding materials.

Republic of Texas (1836–1845)

  • Sam Houston Papers (Dolph Briscoe Center) Military and government correspondence, personal papers, and official records (1813–1863).
  • Sam Houston Papers (Rice University Woodson Research Center) Government letters, personal correspondence, and administrative documents.
  • Texas State Library — Archives & Reference Andrew Jackson Houston Collection: 4,800+ items including Republic-era correspondence, proclamations, and reports (1835–1859).
  • Texas History Trust — Writings of Sam Houston Published compilation of Houston's writings and correspondence (1813–1863).
  • Texas State Library — Republic Legation Papers Official correspondence and records from the Republic of Texas government's diplomatic efforts.

Antebellum & Civil War (1845–1865)

  • Texas State Library — Declaration of Causes of Secession (1861) Full text of Texas's secession declaration documenting the state's reasons for leaving the Union.
  • Texas Runaway Slave Project (Stephen F. Austin State University) 1,285+ runaway slave advertisements and capture notices from Texas newspapers (pre-1865).
  • Freedom on the Move — National Runaway Slave Ads Database Searchable database including Texas newspaper advertisements for freedom-seeking enslaved people.
  • UTSA — Texas Civil War Primary Sources Curated collection of Civil War-era primary materials with annotations and analysis guides.
  • American Battlefield Trust — Secession Declarations Primary source analysis of Texas and other state secession documents.

Reconstruction & Frontier Era (1865–1900)

  • Texas Ranger Hall of Fame and Museum — Online Collections 20,000+ artifacts, photographs, and archival materials on the Texas Rangers from frontier era forward.
  • Portal to Texas History — Ranger Collection Digitized Texas Ranger documents, photographs, and historical records.
  • UTRGV — Early History of Texas Rangers Research guide with primary and secondary sources on Ranger history and borderlands conflicts.
  • Texas State Library — Beyond the Badge Exhibition documenting Texas Rangers records and frontier-era law enforcement.

20th Century

  • NASA Johnson Space Center History Collection (UH–Clear Lake) Apollo mission transcripts, press kits, correspondence, and JSC historical documents.
  • NASA Johnson Space Center History Portal Searchable electronic files including Space News Roundups, transcripts, and news releases.
  • Library of Congress — Dust Bowl Primary Sources WPA photographs, life histories, and New Deal program documents from the Texas Panhandle and beyond.
  • UTSA — Civil Rights Primary Sources Integration, desegregation, and civil rights documentation including the George I. Sánchez papers.
  • Texas A&M–San Antonio — Chicano Movement Primary Sources Chicana/o movement oral histories, interviews, and documentary materials.

Texas Government & Law

  • Texas State Library and Archives Commission (TSLAC) 200+ million pages of archival documents and 2+ million printed volumes of state government records.
  • Texas Digital Archive State government electronic records, agency documents, and digitized historical materials.
  • Texas General Land Office — Archive Collections 35.5 million land grant records (dating to 1720) and 80,000 maps and sketches.
  • Texas Archival Resources Online (TARO) Consortium portal to finding aids from state archives, university libraries, and museums across Texas.
  • Texas State Library — Collections Overview Access to genealogical records, maps, newspapers, oral histories, and photographs.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Portal to Texas History Largest statewide digitized collection: photographs, maps, letters, documents, newspapers, and artifacts from hundreds of institutions.
  • Texas Digital Newspaper Program (Portal to Texas History) 10+ million newspaper pages from Texas (1855–1930), the largest open-access state newspaper collection in the U.S.
  • Chronicling America — Texas Newspapers 77 digitized Texas newspapers through 1963 via the Library of Congress.
  • Dolph Briscoe Center for American History — Collections Texas Newspaper Collection (3,100+ titles), rare books, manuscripts, maps, and photographs.
  • GLO Historic County Maps (Portal to Texas History) Cadastral maps showing original land surveys and county development (1839–1917).
  • Texas General Land Office — Map Store and Database 80,000+ digitized historical maps, sketches, and property records with searchable database.
  • Sam Houston State University — Texas Primary Source Collections Alphabetical index of regional primary source collections available online across Texas.
  • Briscoe Center — Texas History Collections Largest Texas rare book collection, manuscripts, newspapers, photographs, and archival materials.
