Native Peoples: Crow, Blackfeet, Salish-Kootenai, Northern Cheyenne & Others

  • University of Montana — Natives of Montana Archival Project (NOMAP) 65,714 digitized pages from 126 boxes of Bureau of Indian Affairs records (Record Group 75) covering the Blackfeet, Crow, Flathead, Fort Belknap, Fort Shaw, Rocky Boy, and Tongue River agencies.
  • Library of Congress — Montana Folklife (American Folklife Center) Recordings of Blackfeet, Crow, Flathead, and Northern Cheyenne songs and spoken texts, including the earliest ethnographic recordings of Blackfeet songs by Walter McClintock (1898).
  • Montana State University — Native American Studies Resource Guide Curated guide to databases and online resources for Native American studies research, with Montana-specific materials.

Lewis & Clark Expedition

  • University of Nebraska — Journals of the Lewis and Clark Expedition Full text of nearly 5,000 pages of expedition journals with images, supplemental texts, audio files, and Native American perspectives. The expedition spent more time in Montana than any other region.
  • National Archives — Lewis & Clark’s Expedition to the Complex West Interactive educational resource with primary source documents demonstrating various political interests encountered by the expedition, with historic mapping tools.

Fur Trade Era

  • Virtual Library of Fur Trade Historical Source Documents Transcribed primary documents from printed and manuscript sources about the fur trade (1800–1850), including accounts by James P. Beckwourth, Charles Larpenteur, and Peter Skene Ogden.

Mining Boom: Gold Rush & Copper Country

  • Archives West — K. Ross Toole Papers (University of Montana) Research materials and correspondence relating to the Anaconda Copper Mining Company, plus extensive Montana business and industrial history materials.
  • Story of Butte — Butte Miners Union Hall Community history project documenting the Butte Miners’ Union and the city’s copper mining heritage.

Battle of Little Bighorn (1876)

  • National Archives (DocsTeach) — First Report of the Battle of Little Bighorn General Alfred H. Terry’s official report to the Adjutant General on the battle, one of the most famous engagements of the Indian Wars.
  • National Archives — Native Warriors on Both Sides of the Battle Educational resource examining Native American perspectives in the battle records and the broader context of the Great Sioux War.
  • Internet Archive — The Little Big Horn, 1876 Official communications, documents, and reports with rosters of officers and troops of the campaign.

Cattle Ranching & the Open Range

  • Digital Public Library of America — Ranching: Home on the Range Curated exhibition of ranching-related primary sources from multiple Montana institutions, documenting the open range era and the devastating winter of 1886–1887.
  • National Park Service — Grant-Kohrs Ranch National Historic Site Historical exhibits on Montana’s ranching heritage at one of the best-preserved open range cattle ranches in the country.

Homesteading Era (1900–1920s)

  • Montana Memory — Homesteading in Montana Homesteading-related items from dozens of institutions across Montana, including photographs, documents, and diaries from the era when thousands settled Montana’s plains.
  • Digital Public Library of America — The Homestead Acts Curated primary sources including a “Montana Free Homestead Land” pamphlet (Great Northern Railway, 1914) and homesteader photographs.

Butte Labor History

  • Butte-Silver Bow Public Archives — Frank Little Archival materials on Frank Little, the IWW organizer who was lynched by vigilantes in Butte in 1917 during a miners’ strike.
  • University of Montana — Copper Opera: The Butte Miners’ Strike of 1917 Open-access graduate thesis documenting the 1917 Butte miners’ strike in detail.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Montana Memory (Montana History Portal) Statewide digital collection with 66,000+ items—photographs, documents, maps, audio files, and yearbooks—from 80+ Montana institutions.
  • Montana Historical Society — Library & Archives Official state archives with the most comprehensive collection of Montana research materials: histories, government documents, diaries, business records, oral histories, and photographs.
  • University of Montana — Archives and Special Collections 3,000+ oral history interviews, 1,000+ archival collections, nearly 100,000 photographs, 7,000+ historic maps, and regional newspapers.
  • Montana State University — Digital Collections Digitized collections including Indian Peoples of the Northern Great Plains, Montana WPA records, and Thomas Brook photographs.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Montana Newspapers (Library of Congress) 118+ digitized Montana newspaper titles with 314,700+ pages from 1864–1963, fully searchable.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Montana Aggregated search across Montana cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA via the Big Sky Country Digital Network.
