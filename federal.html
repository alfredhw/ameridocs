Major National Archives & Libraries

  • National Archives — America's Historical Documents Featured founding documents and milestone documents in American government and history.
  • National Archives — Milestone Documents 100 pivotal documents in American history, from the Declaration of Independence to the Voting Rights Act.
  • DocsTeach (National Archives) Thousands of primary source documents including letters, photographs, speeches, posters, maps, and videos.
  • Library of Congress — Digital Collections Over 9 million items: books, manuscripts, prints, photographs, maps, films, and sound recordings across 100+ thematic collections.
  • Library of Congress — Primary Source Sets Curated sets on major topics including the Revolution, Civil War, and Immigration.
  • Library of Congress — Free to Use and Reuse Millions of items designated free to use with no copyright restrictions.
  • Smithsonian Open Access Over 5.1 million digital items from 21 museums and 9 research centers, free for download and reuse.
  • SOVA — Smithsonian Online Virtual Archives Searchable archive across the Smithsonian Institution covering art, culture, science, and technology.
  • Smithsonian Institution Archives Selected images, documents, oral histories, and video histories from Smithsonian archives.

Government Documents & Congressional Records

  • Congress.gov Official federal legislative information including current and historical congressional records, bills, and documents.
  • Congressional Record Official proceedings and debates of Congress, published daily since 1873.
  • GovInfo (Government Publishing Office) Free public access to official publications from all three branches of the federal government.
  • A Century of Lawmaking for a New Nation Congressional documents and debates from 1774–1875, including Continental Congress records and Constitutional Convention.
  • United States Statutes at Large Every federal law enacted by Congress, published chronologically, 1789–1950.
  • Presidential Papers (Library of Congress) Digitized papers of 23 presidents from Washington to Coolidge, comprising 3.3 million images.
  • Founders Online Over 184,000 searchable documents from Washington, Jefferson, Madison, Franklin, Hamilton, Jay, and Adams.

Military History

  • National Archives — Military Records Research Military service records, unit records, and war-related documents from the Revolution through modern conflicts.
  • Veterans History Project (Library of Congress) Firsthand recollections and oral histories of U.S. military veterans from World War I through recent conflicts.
  • National Archives — Civil War Records Searchable indexes and guides to Civil War military service records, Confederate and Union.
  • Selected Civil War Photographs (Library of Congress) Photographs and documents from the American Civil War period.

Immigration & Naturalization

  • National Archives — Immigration Records Passenger lists, naturalization documents, and border crossing records.
  • Statue of Liberty & Ellis Island Foundation — Passenger Search Approximately 65 million passenger records and ship manifests, 1820–1957, for the Port of New York.
  • National Archives — Naturalization Records Guide to locating naturalization records held at the National Archives.
  • FamilySearch — Immigration & Naturalization Records Free access to millions of digitized passenger lists, naturalization records, and border crossing documents.

Native Nations & Indigenous History

  • National Archives — American Indian Treaties Original treaties between the United States and American Indian nations, 1722–1869, digitized and searchable.
  • Kappler's Indian Affairs: Laws and Treaties (Oklahoma State) Seven-volume compilation of Indian treaties, statutes, and executive documents organized chronologically.
  • American Indian Law & Treaties Guide (Library of Congress) Research guide to American Indian treaties and related documents.
  • Bureau of Indian Affairs Annual Reports Annual reports of the Commissioner of Indian Affairs documenting federal interaction with Native populations.

Slavery, Abolition & Civil Rights

  • Born in Slavery: Slave Narratives (Library of Congress) Over 2,300 first-person accounts of slavery and 500 photographs of formerly enslaved people, collected in the 1930s.
  • Freedmen's Bureau (National Archives) Records of the Bureau established to assist freed slaves during Reconstruction: marriage records, relief applications, hospital records.
  • Freedmen's Bureau Online Searchable database of Freedmen's Bureau records with indexed names, places, and dates.
  • Freedmen and Southern Society Project (University of Maryland) Selected primary source documents on emancipation and the Freedmen's Bureau era.
  • NMAAHC — Freedmen's Bureau Records Over 1.7 million transcribed images from Freedmen's Bureau records.
  • Documenting the American South — North American Slave Narratives (UNC) All known autobiographical narratives of enslaved people published through 1920.

Census & Demographic Records

  • National Archives — Census Records Federal census schedules from 1790–1950.
  • U.S. Census Bureau — Historical Census Records Official Census Bureau resources for searching historical census data.
  • National Historical GIS (NHGIS) Historical census data and GIS-compatible demographic information from 1790 forward.
  • FamilySearch — Census Records Free access to digitized census records from 1790 onward.

Maps & Cartography

  • Library of Congress — Map Collections Extensive collection of historical and contemporary maps documenting American geography.
  • USGS Historical Topographic Maps Over 185,000 georeferenced topographic maps from 1884–2006, free for download.
  • National Archives — Cartographic Records Maps, charts, and architectural records documenting federal activities and national development.

Newspapers & Periodicals

  • Chronicling America: Historic American Newspapers Over 16 million pages from nearly every state and territory, 1798–1963, fully searchable.
  • Library of Congress — Newspaper Collections General access to historic newspapers and newspaper research materials.
