Native Peoples: Navajo, Hopi, Apache & Oʻodham

  • University of Arizona — Arizona History Primary Sources Curated guide to primary source collections including materials on Apache, Navajo, Hopi, Akimel O’odham (Pima), and Tohono O’odham peoples.
  • University of Arizona — Indigenous History in the Borderlands Research guides with extensive materials on Apache, Navajo, Hopi, and O’odham peoples, including modern political history, land disputes, and cultural documentation.
  • Arizona State Museum — Doris Duke Native American Oral History Project 417 interviews from 55 cultural groups (615+ reels), with bulk recordings from Tohono O’odham, Apache, Navajo, Pima, and Yaqui communities covering personal histories, culture, education, and storytelling.

Spanish Colonial Era & Father Kino

  • University of Arizona — Father Eusebio Kino Report (1699) Digital version of Father Kino’s modified field diary manuscript from 1699, a primary source on Spanish colonial missions in the Pimería Alta.
  • University of Arizona — Borderlands Collections One of the nation’s finest collections of archival materials on the Southwest and Northwest Mexico borderlands, documenting culture and history from the colonial period to the present.

Mining History: Tombstone, Bisbee & Copper Country

  • University of Arizona — History of Mining in Arizona Comprehensive collection on Arizona mining history (1521–1929), with detailed histories of mining districts including Bisbee, Jerome, and Tombstone, and production statistics for copper, lead, silver, and gold.
  • University of Arizona — Arizona & Southwestern Photograph Collection Photographs of Apache communities, Hopi ceremonies, Tucson history, and exploratory expeditions to the Southwest (1884–1886), including mining-era images.

Territorial Period & Statehood (1863–1912)

  • Yuma Library — Arizona Territorial Prison Collection Prison operation documents (1875–1903) with a complete alphabetical prisoner list (#1–#1100) and microfilm of prisoner registers.
  • Arizona Archives Online Searchable descriptions of 500+ archival collections from libraries, museums, and historical societies across Arizona, with 15,000+ photographs and historic territorial maps.

Japanese American Incarceration: Poston & Gila River

  • Densho — Poston (Colorado River) Camp Photographs, documents, and narratives from the Poston camp in the Arizona desert—the largest War Relocation Authority camp with a peak population of 18,000.
  • Densho — Gila River Camp Materials on the Gila River camp on the Gila River Indian Reservation, which held 13,000+ inmates, including a photograph album by Tazuko “Tazu” Kawamoto taken inside the camp.
  • Arizona Historical Society — Japanese-American Relocation Camps Exhibit Digital exhibit “A Celebration of the Human Spirit” documenting Japanese American incarceration in Arizona camps.
  • Densho Digital Repository Searchable archive of Japanese American incarceration materials including camp newsletters, photographs, personal documents, and oral histories from Arizona camps.

Glen Canyon Dam & Colorado River Development

  • Northern Arizona University — Colorado Plateau Digital Collections Digitized materials on the Colorado Plateau and Northern Arizona, including Glen Canyon pre- and post-dam photographs, Bureau of Reclamation construction records, and environmental documentation.
  • Arizona Memory Project — Colorado River Development 192+ Bureau of Reclamation photographs showing Glen Canyon construction (pre-, during, and post-dam completion), plus audit reports on Colorado River Basin water development.

Barry Goldwater

  • Arizona State University — Barry M. Goldwater Papers 1,180 linear feet spanning 100+ years of personal and political papers, including legislative files, correspondence, 1964 presidential campaign materials, photographs, audiovisual materials, and memorabilia.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Arizona Memory Project Nearly 280,000 items from 100+ contributing institutions: government documents, photographs, maps, manuscripts, oral histories, and streaming media on Arizona history.
  • Arizona State Library, Archives and Public Records Gateway to state archives collections with 180,000+ archival photographs (33,000+ digitized) and government records.
  • Arizona State University — PRISM Digital Repository Historic photographs, maps, audio recordings, manuscripts, and the State and Local Arizona Documents (SALAD) collection of government publications.
  • Northern Arizona University — Special Collections Research materials documenting the Colorado Plateau, including 2+ million photographs, oral history interviews, films, letters, diaries, and maps.
  • Arizona Historical Society — Digital Collections Curated digital exhibits and access to 400+ photography collections totaling over 1 million images on Arizona history.
  • Library of Congress — Arizona Digital Collections Guide Comprehensive guide to Arizona materials across Library of Congress holdings.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Arizona Newspapers (Library of Congress) 118+ digitized Arizona newspaper titles including mining camp papers, Spanish-language publications, and Black community newspapers, fully searchable.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Arizona Aggregated search across Arizona cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
