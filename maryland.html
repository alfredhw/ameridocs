Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • Maryland Center for History and Culture — Guide to Indigenous Peoples Resources Comprehensive guide to Piscataway, Nanticoke, and other Maryland Native nations, with references to colonial records, church records, and tribal consultations (1921–2021).

Colonial Maryland & Religious Toleration (1634–1776)

  • Avalon Project (Yale Law School) — Maryland Toleration Act (1649) Full text of the Act Concerning Religion with original spelling, a landmark document in American religious freedom history.
  • American Battlefield Trust — Maryland Toleration Act Annotated full text of the 1649 act establishing religious tolerance in Lord Baltimore’s colony.
  • Library of Congress — Colonial Maryland Commerce Primary sources on colonial Maryland’s tobacco economy, including the Charles Carroll papers (1702–1782) with correspondence, deeds, and financial records.
  • Maryland State Archives — Colonial Government Records Over 471,000 historical documents forming the constitutional, legal, legislative, judicial, and administrative basis of Maryland government from the colonial era onward.

War of 1812 & Fort McHenry

  • Library of Congress — Star-Spangled Banner Research Guide Comprehensive guide to primary sources on Francis Scott Key’s poem, the 1814 bombardment of Fort McHenry, and the first broadside printing (Baltimore, September 17, 1814).
  • Library of Congress — Francis Scott Key Manuscript Digital facsimile of Key’s original “Defence of Fort McHenry” manuscript.
  • American Battlefield Trust — Star-Spangled Banner Primary Source Full text and context for the poem that became the national anthem, with War of 1812 supporting documents.

Slavery, Freedom & the Underground Railroad

  • Maryland State Archives — Legacy of Slavery in Maryland Over 300,000 searchable names across 26+ record categories (1776–1880), including runaway advertisements, chattel records, manumissions, slave jail records, and freedom petitions.
  • Library of Congress — Frederick Douglass Papers Approximately 7,400 items (38,000 images) spanning 1841–1964, including correspondence, speeches, writings, and photographs of the Maryland-born abolitionist.
  • Frederick Douglass Papers Project (Yale) Over 800 digitized documents including speeches, autobiographies, correspondence, and unpublished writings from the multi-volume Yale University Press edition.
  • Library of Congress — Harriet Tubman Digital Resources Primary sources, photographs, and documents on Maryland-born Harriet Tubman, including Civil War pension records and digitized texts.
  • National Archives — Harriet Tubman Records Civil War pension claim documents, military service records, and materials related to Tubman’s Underground Railroad operations from Maryland’s Eastern Shore.
  • Maryland State Archives — African American Research Guide Guide to researching free and enslaved African Americans in Maryland land records, assessment records, court records, and other primary source collections.

Civil War Border State (1861–1865)

  • University of Maryland — Civil War Resources in Special Collections Manuscripts, maps, photographs, and letters documenting Maryland’s divided loyalties as a border state, including the Boyd Family Collection of Civil War photographs.
  • University of Maryland — Women on the Border Digital exhibition on women’s experiences during the Civil War in Maryland, with primary source documents and personal accounts.

Chesapeake Bay Maritime & Environmental History

  • Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum — Digital Collections Over 4,000 boat, sail, and ship plans, plus 150+ manuscript collections including boatbuilding records, maritime shipping documents, yacht club records, and ship record books.
  • University of Maryland — Chesapeake Bay Resources Guide Curated research guide to primary sources on Chesapeake Bay ecology, environmental history, navigation, and maritime heritage.

Civil Rights & Thurgood Marshall

  • Library of Congress — Thurgood Marshall Papers (1949–1991) 150,000 items spanning the Baltimore-born jurist’s 60-year career, including case files, legal papers from his Supreme Court tenure (1967–1991), and key civil rights case documentation.
  • Enoch Pratt Free Library — Thurgood Marshall Primary Sources Brown v. Board of Education brief and draft pleadings, Murray v. University of Maryland case documents, and African American Department collections with slave documents and views of Black life in Maryland.

U.S. Naval Academy

  • U.S. Naval Academy — Special Collections Digital Collections Record Group 405 with 8,500+ historic photographic prints of academy buildings, grounds, monuments, and aerial views. Naval Academy History Collection with founding documents, early histories, and personal correspondence.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Maryland State Archives Over 471,000 historical documents and 110+ digitized series including birth records, death records, estate dockets, wills, maps, photographs, and private records.
  • Maryland State Archives — Guide to Special Collections Searchable guide to manuscript, photograph, newspaper, business, and religious record collections held at the state archives.
  • Maryland Center for History and Culture — Online Resources Digitized museum and library collections including paintings, photographs, prints, and archival materials from holdings of 60,000 books, 800,000 photographs, and 5 million manuscripts.
  • University of Maryland — Digital Collections Digitized archival documents, photographs, and manuscripts documenting Maryland history from the territorial era through the modern period.
  • Library of Congress — Maryland Digital Collections Guide Comprehensive guide to Maryland materials across LOC holdings.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Maryland Newspapers (Library of Congress) 65 digitized Maryland newspaper titles covering 1840–1951, fully searchable.
  • Digital Maryland Aggregated digitized documents, images, audio, and video from multiple Maryland institutions, including Baltimore fire photographs, civil rights materials, and neighborhood histories.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Maryland Aggregated search across Maryland cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
