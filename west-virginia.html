Appalachian Native Peoples & Early Settlement

  • Digital Library of Appalachia Materials from 28 Appalachian College Association member libraries, including diaries, journals, narratives of Native Americans, explorers, and emigrants spanning 3.5 centuries.
  • Library of Congress — West Virginia Digital Collections Guide Guide to LOC’s West Virginia materials including 140+ maps, photographs, manuscripts, and government documents related to frontier settlement.

Civil War & Creation of West Virginia (1863)

  • National Archives — West Virginia Statehood Congressional documents, Lincoln’s proclamation of statehood (June 20, 1863), constitutional ratification records, and the Willey Amendment documents on the creation of a new state from Virginia during the Civil War.
  • National Archives — West Virginia Constitution The Constitution of the State of West Virginia (November 26, 1861 draft; February 18, 1863 ratification).

Coal Mining & the Mine Wars

  • West Virginia Mine Wars Museum — Research Resources Period photographs, Senate testimony, maps, trial transcripts, Baldwin-Felts Agency documents, and interviews on the Paint Creek–Cabin Creek strike (1912–13) and the Battle of Blair Mountain (1921).
  • Digital Public Library of America — When Miners Strike Primary source set including Mother Jones’s 1912 speech from Paint Creek, photographs of arrested miners, company cabin images, and federal investigative reports.
  • Library of Congress — Battle of Blair Mountain in Chronicling America Contemporary newspaper coverage of the 1921 Battle of Blair Mountain, the largest armed labor uprising in American history.

Labor History: Mother Jones & the UMW

  • Penn State University — United Mine Workers of America Archives Complete archives of the UMW with photographic, graphic, and artifact collections browsable online.
  • West Virginia University — Mine Wars Newspaper Sources Primary source materials from digitized historic newspapers with extensive coverage of labor conflicts.

Appalachian Culture: Music, Crafts & Folklore

  • West Virginia University — Folk Music Digital Collection Digitized selections from the WVRHC’s extensive folk music collection, including 1930s–1940s field recordings.
  • West Virginia University — Appalachian Collection 12,000+ volumes on Appalachian coal miners, music, crafts, folk customs, and environmental issues.
  • Berea College — Sound Archives & Appalachian Materials The Weatherford-Hammond Mountain Collection (oldest Appalachian collection in the world), with audio/video focusing on traditional music, spoken lore, and oral history.
  • Library of Congress — West Virginia Folklife American Folklife Center resources documenting expressive culture in West Virginia.

New Deal in Appalachia: Arthurdale & the CCC

  • National Park Service — Arthurdale: A New Deal Community Experiment Teaching materials with primary and secondary sources on Eleanor Roosevelt’s homestead community near Reedsville—the first New Deal subsistence homestead project.
  • Arthurdale Heritage Inc. — History Community archives with Eleanor Roosevelt correspondence and letters maintaining relationships with Arthurdale residents.
  • The Living New Deal (UC Berkeley) Searchable database of New Deal projects by state, including West Virginia CCC camps, WPA projects, and Arthurdale.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • West Virginia University — West Virginia and Regional History Center Largest repository for West Virginia and Appalachian materials: 4,000+ archival collections, 12,000+ volume Appalachian Collection, historical newspapers from 1791, and gubernatorial papers.
  • West Virginia History OnView Largest searchable database of digitized West Virginia photographs (1850–present), with 50,000+ images.
  • West Virginia University — ArchivesSpace Searchable finding aids for 4,000+ archives and manuscript collections with nearly 20,000 linear feet of primary materials.
  • Marshall University — Special Collections Digital 860+ collections including 800+ oral history interview transcripts (1980s–1990s) and the Regional Photograph Collection.
  • West Virginia Encyclopedia Comprehensive reference covering all aspects of West Virginia history, culture, and geography.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — West Virginia Newspapers (Library of Congress) 107 digitized West Virginia newspaper titles, fully searchable, with coverage of Civil War, labor strikes, and mining history.
  • Digital Virginias (DPLA Service Hub) 58,000+ items from Virginia and West Virginia discoverable through DPLA, including WVU Libraries materials.
  • Digital Public Library of America — West Virginia Aggregated search across West Virginia cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
