Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • Wisconsin Historical Society — American Indian Language Materials Online Freely accessible Native American language materials including manuscript Potawatomi vocabularies (1920–1932), Ho-Chunk materials, and other Indigenous linguistic resources.
  • Wisconsin DPI — American Indian Resources Digital resources including videos, interactive maps, and media on Ho-Chunk Nation, Ojibwe, Menominee, Potawatomi, Oneida, and Mohican histories and contemporary cultures.

French Fur Trade & Voyageur Era (1650s–1850s)

  • Wisconsin Historical Society — The French Fur Trade Articles and primary materials documenting the fur trade era, including profiles of key figures like Nicolas Perrot and Stephen Bonga, with information on voyageur life at Prairie du Chien, Green Bay, and Fort Winnebago.
  • Milwaukee Public Museum — Wisconsin Fur Trade Digital collections documenting French and French-Canadian voyageurs, fur traders, and the material culture of Wisconsin’s fur trade communities.

Lead Mining & Territorial Era (1820s–1848)

  • Wisconsin Historical Society — Lead Mining in Southwestern Wisconsin Primary sources on the lead mining rush (1820s–1830s) that brought thousands of miners to southwestern Wisconsin, producing 13 million pounds of lead annually and earning the state its “Badger” nickname.

Immigration: German, Scandinavian & Polish Settlement

  • University of Wisconsin — Wisconsin Office of Emigration Materials Digitized primary sources on Wisconsin’s 1852–1855 Office of Emigration and the impact of German immigration on the state.
  • Wisconsin Historical Society — Immigration & Growth Materials on 19th-century immigration waves including Germans (16% of immigrants), Scandinavians (5%), Irish, and others who shaped Wisconsin settlement patterns.
  • Wisconsin Historical Society — Poles in Wisconsin Materials documenting Polish settlement, from early communities like Polonia (1850s) to Milwaukee’s Polish community growing from 30,000 in 1890 to 100,000 by 1915.

Progressive Era & Robert La Follette

  • Wisconsin Historical Society — Progressivism and the Wisconsin Idea Materials documenting the Wisconsin Progressive movement and the “Wisconsin Idea” of good government, education reform, and political innovation under La Follette and successors.
  • Wisconsin Historical Society — Speeches of Robert M. La Follette Compiled speeches and historical materials from Wisconsin’s leading Progressive Era political figure.
  • University of Wisconsin — Robert M. La Follette Sr. Papers Finding Aid Detailed finding aid to the La Follette Papers (1879–1910, 1924–1929), including correspondence, speeches, and documents on Wisconsin’s direct primary and railroad regulation battles.

Labor History & Milwaukee Socialism

  • University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee — Milwaukee Socialism: The Emil Seidel Era Emil Seidel’s personal papers, mayoral papers, and unpublished autobiography documenting Milwaukee’s historic role as “America’s first Socialist city” and the “Sewer Socialists” movement.
  • UWM — Milwaukee Socialism Timeline Interactive timeline of Milwaukee socialism with linked primary sources and contextual materials.
  • Wisconsin Historical Society — Milwaukee Sewer Socialism Materials on Milwaukee’s socialist urban reform movement focused on sanitation, municipal water and power, parks, and education.
  • Milwaukee Public Library — Socialist Party Collection Photographs and publications documenting Milwaukee socialism, primarily from the 1920s–1940s.

McCarthy Era

  • Wisconsin Historical Society — McCarthy: A Documented Record Wisconsin Citizens’ Committee on McCarthy’s Record (1952) and The Progressive magazine documentation (1954) providing analysis of Senator Joseph McCarthy’s conduct and the Red Scare in Wisconsin politics.

Dairy Industry & Agricultural History

  • Wisconsin Historical Society — Dairy Industry in Wisconsin Historical materials on Wisconsin’s dairy industry development, including the Wisconsin Dairyman’s Association (founded 1872) and William Dempster Hoard’s role in making Wisconsin “America’s Dairyland.”
  • University of Wisconsin — Agricultural History Digital Collections Guide Comprehensive guide to digital collections on Wisconsin agricultural history, dairy farming, and rural life.
  • Wisconsin Historical Society — McCormick–International Harvester Collection Over 12 million pages and items (1753–1985) documenting agricultural equipment manufacturing and its impact on Wisconsin and Midwest farming.

Civil Rights in Milwaukee

  • University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee — March on Milwaukee Comprehensive primary source collection with photographs, unedited news film footage, text documents, and oral history interviews on 1960s Milwaukee civil rights protests against housing discrimination and school segregation.
  • Recollection Wisconsin — Milwaukee’s Struggle for Civil Rights Curated collection of Milwaukee civil rights materials from multiple institutions, including Alderman Vel Phillips’s Open Housing campaigns.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Wisconsin Historical Society — Digital Collections One of the largest freely accessible state historical collections: over 110,000 digitized images, 90,000+ archival images, 25,000+ historical maps, plus audio, film, and manuscripts spanning all periods of Wisconsin history.
  • Wisconsin Historical Society — Content Portal Search interface for digitized photographs, art, maps, diaries, letters, books, and oral histories from the state’s collections.
  • University of Wisconsin — Digital Collections Over two million digitized objects including books, manuscripts, photographs, maps, prints, audio, and video across multiple subject areas.
  • Recollection Wisconsin Aggregated portal providing access to digital collections from 58+ Wisconsin cultural heritage institutions.
  • Archival Resources in Wisconsin Finding aids and descriptions for archival collections held across 19 Wisconsin repositories.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Wisconsin Newspapers (Library of Congress) 67 digitized Wisconsin newspaper titles (1836–1963), fully searchable.
  • BadgerLink — Archive of Wisconsin Newspapers 600,000+ pages from 40 newspaper titles in 26 Wisconsin communities, scanned from microfilm by Wisconsin Historical Society (2015–2022). Free to Wisconsin residents.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Wisconsin Aggregated search across Wisconsin cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
