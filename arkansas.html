Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • Arkansas Archeological Survey — Toltec Mounds Research Research documentation on Plum Bayou Culture and 1,000+ year-old mound-building communities near Scott, Arkansas.
  • Encyclopedia of Arkansas — Native Americans Comprehensive essays and linked primary sources about Quapaw, Caddo, and Osage peoples.
  • University of Arkansas — Indians of Arkansas Educational resources and archaeological documentation about indigenous Arkansas nations.
  • University of Arkansas — Quapaw Nation Research Guide Subject guide connecting to primary sources and archival materials on Quapaw history and culture.

French & Spanish Colonial Era

  • Arkansas Post National Memorial — Spanish Domain (NPS) Primary source documentation on Spanish colonial governance and settlement at Arkansas Post (1763–1804).
  • Encyclopedia of Arkansas — European Exploration & Settlement Comprehensive essays connecting to colonial documents, maps, and exploration accounts from France and Spain.
  • Encyclopedia of Arkansas — Arkansas Post Detailed essay with linked primary sources about the earliest European settlement and colonial outpost.

Territory & Early Statehood (1819–1860)

  • Arkansas Territory General Assembly Records (Arkansas Digital Heritage) Digitized enrolled bills, legislative records, and government proceedings from Arkansas Territory (1819–1835).
  • 1836 Arkansas State Constitution (Arkansas Digital Archives) Complete digitized pages of the original 1836 Arkansas Constitution adopted at statehood.
  • Arkansas Territory Online Exhibits (State Archives) Curated digital exhibition with primary documents, maps, and materials on the territorial period.

Slavery & the Antebellum Period

  • University of Arkansas — Materials Related to Enslaved Persons Research guide connecting to enslaved people's experiences including WPA narratives and plantation records.
  • Encyclopedia of Arkansas — WPA Slave Narratives Overview and links to Arkansas slave narrative collections, representing one of the largest state contributions to the WPA project.
  • University of Arkansas — WPA Special Collections Archival materials from WPA oral history projects including enslaved persons' interviews.
  • Hollywood/Valley Plantation Oral History Project (Pryor Center) Oral history documentation of African American life on a 12,000-acre plantation in southeast Arkansas.
  • Encyclopedia of Arkansas — Slavery Essay with links to plantation records, slave bills of sale, and antebellum economic documentation.

Civil War & Reconstruction

  • Battle of Prairie Grove: Civilian Recollections (NPS) Primary source accounts and civilian recollections from the Prairie Grove battlefield.
  • Charles Hillman Brough Scrapbooks (Arkansas State Archives) Digitized scrapbooks containing Civil War documents, newspaper clippings, and photographs related to Arkansas conflicts.
  • Eisenhower Presidential Library — Little Rock Integration Documents Presidential correspondence, executive orders, and federal intervention documents (also relevant to Civil Rights section below).

Elaine Race Massacre of 1919

  • UALR — Elaine Race Massacre Exhibition Comprehensive digital exhibition with primary sources, newspapers, and documents on the 1919 Elaine Massacre.
  • University of Arkansas — Elaine Race Massacre Research Materials Archival collection including newspaper clippings, interviews, primary documents, and photographs.
  • Encyclopedia of Arkansas — Elaine Massacre Scholarly essay with linked primary sources documenting race relations, labor organizing, and violent suppression.

Civil Rights: Little Rock Nine & Desegregation

  • Eisenhower Presidential Library — Little Rock School Integration Crisis Presidential correspondence, telegrams, Executive Order 10730, and federal intervention records from 1957.
  • Primary Source Nexus — Little Rock Nine Curated collection of primary documents from the 1957 Central High School desegregation crisis.
  • Library of Congress — Arkansas Primary Source Sets Library of Congress primary source collection including Little Rock Nine photographs and historical documents.
  • Clinton Digital Library Digitized collections from the William J. Clinton Presidential Library, including Arkansas-related materials.

Arkansas Government & Law

  • Arkansas State Archives — Digital Heritage Central portal for thousands of digitized government records, documents, maps, and photographs.
  • Arkansas Digital Archives Comprehensive searchable collection of state archives materials including government records and manuscripts.
  • Arkansas State Archives — Finding Aids Searchable repository of detailed finding aids to government collections and archival records.
  • Arkansas Documents Digital Collections (Preservica) Digitized official state documents and records available for free viewing and download.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Encyclopedia of Arkansas Free online state encyclopedia with 8,000 entries and 17,000 pieces of media: photographs, maps, documents, videos, and audio.
  • Chronicling America — Arkansas Newspapers 104 digitized Arkansas newspaper titles with 300,000+ pages via the Library of Congress.
  • Arkansas Digital Newspaper Project Digitized Arkansas historical newspapers as part of the National Digital Newspaper Program.
  • Arkansas State Archives — Historic Maps Over 4,000 historic maps of Arkansas with selected examples digitized and publicly available.
  • University of Arkansas — Digital Collections Civil rights materials, Fayetteville history, Senator Fulbright materials, and regional collections.
  • University of Arkansas — Arkansas Collection Over 70,000 titles on Arkansas history, culture, and regional studies with finding aids online.
  • Butler Center for Arkansas Studies — Roberts Library Arkansas history archives with digitized books, periodicals, and photographs.
  • Library of Congress — Arkansas Guide Comprehensive guide to digital collections and archives relating to Arkansas.
