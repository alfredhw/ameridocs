Puritans & Colonial Religion

  • Digital Puritan Press — Jonathan Edwards Collection Sermons and writings of the leading theologian of the First Great Awakening, including “Sinners in the Hands of an Angry God” and “A Faithful Narrative of the Surprising Work of God,” available in multiple formats.
  • Digital Puritan Press — George Whitefield Collection Complete sermon collections from the itinerant evangelist whose preaching tours across the colonies (1739–1770) ignited the transatlantic Great Awakening revival.
  • Christian Classics Ethereal Library — Selected Sermons of George Whitefield Whitefield’s sermons in PDF, HTML, and ePub formats from CCEL’s curated library of classic Christian texts.
  • Princeton Theological Seminary — Puritan Materials (via Internet Archive) Selections from Princeton’s 78,000+ digitized theological works, filtered for Puritan sermons, treatises, and devotional literature from colonial New England.
  • The Puritan Library Aggregated links to freely available Puritan texts, sermons, confessions of faith, and theological works across multiple digital repositories.

Founding Era & Religious Liberty

  • Founders Online — Thomas Jefferson’s Bill for Establishing Religious Freedom (1779) Full transcription and annotation of Jefferson’s Virginia statute, one of three achievements he chose for his epitaph—the foundational American document on church-state separation.
  • Founders Online — James Madison’s Memorial and Remonstrance Against Religious Assessments (1785) Madison’s influential petition against state-supported religion, which helped defeat a Virginia tax for Christian ministers and paved the way for the First Amendment.
  • Founders Online — Benjamin Franklin’s Articles of Belief and Acts of Religion (1728) Franklin’s personal creed reflecting Enlightenment-influenced deism and religious tolerance among the founding generation.
  • Monticello — Thomas Jefferson and the Virginia Statute for Religious Freedom Scholarly context and primary materials on Jefferson’s drafting of the statute, its legislative journey, and its influence on the First Amendment.
  • Montpelier — James Madison and Religious Freedom Primary and interpretive materials on Madison’s philosophical contributions to religious liberty, from the Virginia Declaration of Rights through the Bill of Rights.

The Second Great Awakening & Revivalism

  • Christian Classics Ethereal Library — Charles Finney, Lectures on Revivals of Religion (1835) Full text of the definitive manual of Second Great Awakening revival methods—Finney’s “New Measures” that systematized and professionalized American evangelicalism.
  • Oberlin College Archives — Charles Grandison Finney Presidential Papers (1814–1878) Finding aid for the Finney Papers at Oberlin, where he served as president: correspondence, sermons, and institutional records bridging revivalism and abolitionism.
  • American Antiquarian Society — Emerging American Religions (1821–1895) Digitized periodicals from new religious movements spawned by the Second Great Awakening, plus “Alternative Faith and Philosophy Periodicals” (1789–1878) and “General Interest Christian Periodicals” (1743–1889).

African American Religious History

  • University of North Carolina — Documenting the American South: Church History Extensive digitized materials on Black church history: slave religious life, AME and AME Zion founding documents, post-Emancipation institutional development, and theological writings by African American scholars.
  • DocSouth — John Jamison Moore, History of the A.M.E. Zion Church (1884) Digitized founding history of the African Methodist Episcopal Zion Church (est. 1796) with articles of incorporation and conference minutes.
  • Library of Congress — African American Spirituals: American Folklife Center Digitized field recordings, sheet music, and scholarly essays documenting the slave spiritual tradition—the foundational form of African American sacred music.
  • Smithsonian Libraries — Slave Songs of the United States (1867) Digitized first edition of the earliest published collection of African American spirituals, with musical notation, lyrics, and field notes from the Sea Islands and beyond.
  • Stanford University — Martin Luther King, Jr. Research and Education Institute Over 1 million digitized documents including King’s sermons, theological writings, and papers connecting Black church theology to civil rights activism. (See also Slavery & Abolition.)
  • Umbra Search African American History Federated search across hundreds of thousands of digitized materials from 1,000+ libraries and archives, including Black church records, religious publications, and institutional histories.

Catholicism in America

  • Marquette University Archives — Dorothy Day–Catholic Worker Collection Comprehensive archive of the Catholic Worker Movement: Dorothy Day’s diaries, letters, and published writings, plus 300+ oral history interviews with Catholic Worker volunteers and Peter Maurin’s papers on Catholic social thought.
  • Catholic University of America — American Catholic History Research Center Digital Collections Digitized photographic archives, online exhibits, and finding aids from the largest archival collection of American Catholic history (nearly 14,000 linear feet of records).
  • DPLA — Spanish Missions in California Primary sources on the 21 California missions (1769–1833): documents, photographs, and artwork documenting Franciscan evangelization and its impact on indigenous peoples.

Judaism in America

  • American Jewish Historical Society — Digital Collections Thousands of digitized pages documenting Jewish American life from 1654 to the present: oral histories, photographs, organizational records (Hebrew Immigrant Aid Society, American Jewish Congress), and Emma Lazarus’s manuscript of “The New Colossus.”
  • Center for Jewish History — ArchivesSpace Integrated catalog providing access to finding aids and digitized materials from the five partner institutions of the Center for Jewish History in New York.
  • American Jewish Archives — Digital Collections Digital collections from Hebrew Union College in Cincinnati, documenting Jewish religious institutional development, rabbinical leadership, and Reform Judaism in America.

Mormonism & the Latter-day Saint Tradition

  • The Joseph Smith Papers The definitive scholarly edition of every document created by or under direction of Joseph Smith (1805–1844): revelations, correspondence, historical narratives, and organizational records, fully digitized with transcriptions and annotations.
  • Joseph Smith Papers — Book of Mormon Manuscript Resources Digitized original manuscript and printer’s manuscript of the Book of Mormon, plus the 1830 first edition—the primary textual evidence for the foundational LDS scripture.
  • Brigham Young University — Overland Trails Database Emigrant diaries and journals including Mormon pioneer accounts of the trek to the Great Salt Lake Valley (1846–1869), with maps and biographical data. (See also Westward Expansion.)

Indigenous Spirituality & Christian Missions

  • DPLA — Spanish Missions in California Documents, photographs, and artwork on the mission system’s intersection of Catholic evangelization with indigenous spiritual traditions and forced labor. (See also Native Nations.)
  • Library of Congress — California First-Person Narratives: The Missions First-hand accounts of mission life from 1849–1900, documenting the religious and cultural dimensions of the California mission experience.
  • Internet Archive — Jesuit Relations and Allied Documents Digitized volumes of the annual reports sent by Jesuit missionaries in New France (1610–1791), documenting encounters with Native American peoples, religious practices, and conversion efforts.
  • DPLA — Wounded Knee Massacre (1890) Primary sources including documentation of the Ghost Dance spiritual movement among the Lakota, which the U.S. government violently suppressed at Wounded Knee.

The Social Gospel & Religious Reform

  • Internet Archive — Walter Rauschenbusch, A Theology for the Social Gospel (1917) Complete digitized text of the foundational work systematizing Social Gospel theology: the argument that Christianity demands not only individual salvation but transformation of unjust social structures.
  • Stanford King Institute — The Social Gospel Essays and primary resources tracing the Social Gospel’s influence on Martin Luther King Jr.’s theology, connecting Progressive-era religious reform to civil rights activism.
  • Ramapo College — Jane Addams Digital Edition Scholarly edition of Addams’s writings on Hull House, the settlement movement, and the intersection of religious conviction with Progressive-era social reform. (See also Women’s History.)
  • Historical Society of Pennsylvania — Pennsylvania Abolition Society Papers Digitized records of the oldest abolition society in America (est. 1775 by Quakers), documenting the religious motivations for antislavery organizing.
  • DPLA — The Temperance Movement and Prohibition Primary sources on temperance as a religious reform crusade: WCTU records, Carry Nation’s activism, camp meeting temperance pledges, and the road to the 18th Amendment.

Modern Evangelicalism

  • Wheaton College — Billy Graham Center Archives Over 1 million items documenting twentieth-century American evangelicalism: 1,600+ Billy Graham sermon recordings spanning 60 years, missionary records, and the papers of major evangelical organizations and leaders.
  • Duke Divinity School — Divinity Archive Cross-searchable archive of religious primary sources created in partnership with local churches and Wesleyan societies: 1,400+ chapel recordings and 1,300+ sermon manuscripts.

Major Research Collections

  • Founders Online — National Archives Fully searchable papers of Jefferson, Madison, Franklin, Hamilton, Adams, and Washington, with extensive holdings on religious liberty, disestablishment, and the First Amendment.
  • Harvard Divinity School Library — Digital Collections Digitized Unitarian/Universalist journals, over 8,000 images for early Christianity studies, Civil War–era religious pamphlets, and Theosophical Society materials.
  • Yale Divinity School Library — Special Collections Over 6,000 linear feet of archival material: missionary records, papers of American clergy and evangelists, and materials on Protestant theological education.
  • American Antiquarian Society — Religious Periodicals Digitized collections of “General Interest Christian Periodicals” (1743–1889), “Alternative Faith and Philosophy Periodicals” (1789–1878), and “Religious Periodicals for Women, Children, and Families” (1804–1878).
  • Christian Classics Ethereal Library Curated digital library of classic Christian texts in multiple formats: sermons by Edwards, Whitefield, Finney, and hundreds of other authors spanning the history of Christian thought.
  • University of North Carolina — Documenting the American South Sixteen thematic collections of digitized Southern history with extensive African American church materials, advised by UNC historians of Southern religion.
