Treaties & Diplomacy

  • Oklahoma State University — Tribal Treaties Database Comprehensive portal providing access to treaties, agreements, and historical documents shaping tribal–U.S. relationships, continuously expanded with digitized content from “Indian Affairs: Laws and Treaties” (Kappler’s).
  • Yale Law School — Avalon Project: Treaties with Native Americans Full texts of major treaties including the 1784 Treaty with the Six Nations, 1791 Treaty with the Cherokee, Treaty of Fort Laramie, and others.
  • National Archives — Bureau of Indian Affairs Records (RG 75) Geographic guide to locating treaty and diplomatic records organized by state and tribe within the 6+ million digitized scans of BIA records.

Indian Removal & the Trail of Tears

  • Digital Public Library of America — Cherokee Removal and the Trail of Tears Documents, images, and music revealing the story of Cherokee removal, including letters from wagon masters and correspondence from tribal leaders.
  • University of Tennessee — Papers of Andrew Jackson: Indian Removal Documents Digitized manuscripts with transcriptions documenting Choctaw removal, including treaties, petitions, and letters, with planned expansions for Chickasaw, Creek, Seminole, and Cherokee removal.
  • National Archives — Jackson’s Message to Congress on Indian Removal (1830) Full text of Jackson’s pivotal message to Congress advocating for the Indian Removal Act.
  • Digital Library of Georgia — Southeastern Native American Documents (1730–1842) Over 2,000 documents including letters, legal proceedings, and military orders relating to Cherokee, Creek, and other Southeastern Native Americans prior to removal.
  • Museum of the Cherokee Indian — Online Collections Over 18,000 images of records, letters, and diaries from the 1830s onward pertaining to the Trail of Tears and Eastern Band Cherokee history.
  • Library of Congress — Indian Removal Act: Primary Documents Digitized government documents, manuscripts, ephemera, and maps related to the Indian Removal Act and its aftermath.

Reservation Era & Allotment

  • National Archives — Dawes Records of the Five Civilized Tribes Guide to Final Dawes Rolls, Census Cards, Enrollment Applications, and Land Allotment Jackets documenting the forced allotment of tribal lands under the 1887 Dawes Act.
  • Oklahoma Historical Society — Dawes Rolls Access to Dawes Final Rolls showing name, age, blood degree, census card number, tribe, and roll number for the Five Civilized Tribes.
  • National Archives — Indian Census Rolls (1885–1940) Guide to locating and accessing annual Indian Census Rolls from 1885–1940 across reservations and agencies.
  • DPLA — Reservations, Resistance, and the Indian Reorganization Act Photographs and documents related to tribal responses to the 1934 Indian Reorganization Act and the reservation system.

Boarding Schools

  • Dickinson College — Carlisle Indian School Digital Resource Center Most comprehensive digitized collection from the Carlisle Indian Industrial School (1879–1918): 300,000+ documents including student records, industrial certificates, correspondence, graduation pamphlets, and school newspapers.
  • University of Minnesota — National Indian Boarding School Digital Archive (NIBSDA) National digital repository for boarding school archival collections featuring student case files, administrative records, enrollment registers, health records, and student indexes organized by school.
  • National Native American Boarding School Healing Coalition — Resource Database Comprehensive repository of bibliographic entries, media resources, academic studies, and educational content on the history and impacts of Indian boarding schools.
  • Haskell Indian Nations University — Collections and Archives Faculty and student papers, the Frank A. Rinehart photograph collection (809 glass negatives and 5,000 images), plus 10,000 additional historical photographs, films, and oral history interviews.

Wars & Resistance

  • DPLA — The Wounded Knee Massacre (1890) Documents, photographs, government records, and newspaper accounts exploring the 1890 massacre of Lakota people at Wounded Knee Creek.
  • National Park Service — Sand Creek Massacre National Historic Site Historical documentation and primary sources on the 1864 Sand Creek Massacre of Cheyenne and Arapaho people by Colorado militia.
  • American Archive of Public Broadcasting — AIM Occupation of Wounded Knee (1973) Primary source set with audio, video, and documents on the 71-day American Indian Movement occupation at Wounded Knee, South Dakota.
  • Library of Congress — Wounded Knee in Chronicling America Newspaper coverage of the 1890 Wounded Knee Massacre from digitized historic newspapers.

Language & Culture Preservation

  • Endangered Languages Archive (ELAR) Digital archive with audio and video recordings of 500+ endangered languages worldwide, including numerous Native American languages. Free access after registration.
  • University of Texas — Archive of the Indigenous Languages of Latin America (AILLA) Digital repository of recordings, texts, grammars, dictionaries, and revitalization materials for indigenous languages. Free access in English and Spanish.
  • Smithsonian Center for Folklife and Cultural Heritage Extensive Native American music, performance, and cultural documentation from the Ralph Rinzler Folklife Archives (72,000+ audio/film/video items, 1 million still images).

Legal & Sovereignty

  • Native American Rights Fund — National Indian Law Library Comprehensive collection of federal Indian and tribal law resources, with the Tribal Law Gateway providing access to tribal constitutions, codes, and ordinances.
  • Library of Congress — Indigenous Law Web Archive Collections of primary law sources from Indigenous nations including constitutions of the Navajo, Muscogee, Cherokee, Comanche, and Hopi nations.
  • National Archives — Indian Reorganization Act (1934) Digitized records documenting the Wheeler-Howard Act and its implementation, which ended allotment and supported tribal self-governance.

Native Voices & Oral Histories

  • National Museum of the American Indian — Archive Center 1,500 linear feet of manuscripts, 500,000+ photographs, and thousands of sound and audiovisual recordings from the late nineteenth century to the present.
  • University of Oklahoma — Indian-Pioneer Papers Collection Approximately 80,000 indexed oral history entries from 1930s WPA interviews spanning 1861–1936, searchable by name, date, county, and subject.
  • Newberry Library — D’Arcy McNickle Center for American Indian and Indigenous Studies Research center housing the Edward E. Ayer Collection, one of the largest collections of American Indian and Indigenous Studies materials in the world.
  • American Archive of Public Broadcasting — Native Narratives Online exhibit on Indigenous representation in public broadcasting covering stereotypes, termination/relocation, AIM, contemporary media, and Native-created programming.

Major Research Collections

  • National Archives — Native Americans Research Guide Comprehensive gateway to all NARA holdings related to Native Americans, including Bureau of Indian Affairs records, military service records, and census rolls.
  • Library of Congress — Native American Collections Guide to LOC’s Native American resources including rare materials, digital collections, and manuscript divisions.
  • Library of Congress — Edward Curtis Photographs Over 2,400 silver-gelatin prints (1900–1930) documenting Native American peoples, with 1,000+ digitized and searchable.
  • Northwestern University — Edward S. Curtis’s The North American Indian Digitized complete 20-volume work with photographs and ethnographic documentation of Native American cultures across the continent.
  • National Museum of the American Indian — Collections Search Access to 5,500+ digitized photographs from the 800,000+ item collection at the Smithsonian’s NMAI.
  • Yale University — New England Indian Papers Series Scholarly critical edition of New England Native American primary sources from multiple institutions, digitized, transcribed, and annotated.
