Native Peoples: Pawnee, Omaha, Ponca & Lakota

  • NebraskStudies.org — Emergence of Historic Tribes Primary sources and educational materials on the Pawnee, Lakota Sioux, Omaha, Ponca, and Otoe-Missouria peoples of Nebraska.

Lewis & Clark and Westward Migration

  • University of Nebraska — Journals of the Lewis and Clark Expedition Full text of nearly 5,000 pages of the definitive Nebraska edition, with images, supplemental texts, audio files, and Native American perspectives.
  • NebraskStudies.org — The Oregon Trail Primary source materials on overland migration along the Platte River corridor through Nebraska.

Homestead Act & Pioneer Settlement

  • National Park Service — Homestead National Historical Park Museum collection of 5,000+ objects from the 1862–1890 settlement period at the site of Daniel Freeman’s homestead, the first filed under the Homestead Act.
  • National Archives — Homestead Act The 1862 Homestead Act and related primary documents.
  • NebraskStudies.org — Daniel Freeman and the Settlers Primary sources and context on the first homesteaders and the process of claiming free land on the Nebraska prairie.

Railroad Era: Union Pacific & the Transcontinental Railroad

  • Nebraska State Historical Society — Union Pacific Railroad Archives 750 linear feet of railroad materials documenting the Union Pacific’s role in building the transcontinental railroad from Omaha westward.
  • NebraskStudies.org — Railroads and Settlement Primary sources on railroad land grants, promotion of settlement, and the railroad’s economic impact on Nebraska.

Standing Bear v. Crook (1879)

  • Teaching American History — Standing Bear v. Crook Full text of the landmark 1879 federal court decision recognizing that Native Americans are “persons within the meaning of the law” entitled to habeas corpus.
  • Nebraska State Historical Society — Standing Bear Collection Archival materials on Ponca chief Standing Bear and the historic civil rights case heard in Omaha.
  • Library of Congress — Chief Standing Bear and His Landmark Case Legal history essay with links to primary sources on the case and its broader implications for Native American rights.

Populist Movement & William Jennings Bryan

  • Nebraska State Historical Society — William Jennings Bryan Collection The “Cross of Gold” speech (1896), acceptance speech to the Populists, correspondence, scrapbooks, and manuscripts (1883–1930) of Nebraska’s three-time presidential candidate.
  • NebraskStudies.org — William Jennings Bryan Primary sources and context on Bryan and the Populist-Progressive movements in Nebraska.

Willa Cather & Nebraska Literary Heritage

  • University of Nebraska — Willa Cather Archive Digital humanities project with digitized short fiction, interviews, speeches, public letters, and a chronology of Cather’s life—the world’s largest Cather archive.
  • National Willa Cather Center (Red Cloud, Nebraska) Letters, manuscripts, photographs (~2,000 historical images), and Cather family materials from the 1880s–1940s.

Dust Bowl & Farm Crisis

  • Library of Congress — Dust Bowl Migration Photographs by Dorothea Lange and Farm Security Administration photographers documenting the agricultural devastation of the 1930s.
  • NebraskStudies.org — Drought and the Dust Bowl Primary sources on the Dust Bowl’s impact on Nebraska farms and communities.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Nebraska State Historical Society — Digital Archives Government records, images, manuscripts, maps, photographs, and digitized newspapers, including the Solomon D. Butcher photographs and Oblinger family letters (1862–1912).
  • NebraskStudies.org Over 7,000 free primary sources (documents, photos, letters, maps, video) organized by historical period from pre-1500 to the present.
  • Nebraska Archives Online (University of Nebraska) Central portal to archival repositories across four university campuses (UNL, UNO, UNK, UNMC).

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Nebraska Newspapers (Library of Congress) Digitized Nebraska newspaper titles, fully searchable.
  • University of Nebraska — Nebraska Newspapers Searchable full-text of Nebraska newspapers from 1854 to the present, a collaborative project of UNL and History Nebraska.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Nebraska Aggregated search across Nebraska cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
