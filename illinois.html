Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • Cahokia Mounds State Historic Site — Explore Official resource on the largest pre-Columbian settlement north of Mexico with interpretive and archaeological materials.
  • Mythic Mississippi Project (University of Illinois) Interactive digital exhibit and primary source materials on Native Illinois peoples and their Mississippi River Valley heritage.
  • Illinois History and Lincoln Collections (IHLC) Comprehensive manuscript collections documenting Native American history in Illinois, including material on the Illini Confederacy.
  • SIU — Native American Heritage Resources Research guides and primary sources on Illinois Native American peoples including Illini, Potawatomi, and Kickapoo.

French Colonial & Frontier Era

  • Kaskaskia Manuscripts (French Illinois Colonial Archives) Digitized French notarial records (1741–1782) including marriage contracts, wills, deeds, and legal documents from colonial Illinois.
  • FamilySearch — Illinois Colonial Records Searchable index to French colonial records and church vital records from early Illinois settlements.
  • French Heritage Corridor — Illinois Resources Curated primary source links and documents on French colonial presence in the Illinois Country.

Territorial Period & Early Statehood (1787–1840s)

  • Black Hawk War Veterans Database (Illinois State Archives) Indexed muster rolls and service records of Illinois soldiers in the 1831–1832 Black Hawk War.
  • Record of Services in the Black Hawk War (Internet Archive) Full text of the 1882 official roster of Illinois militia officers and enlisted men.
  • Library of Congress — Illinois State Guide Gateway to federal and territorial documents related to Illinois formation and early statehood.

Lincoln & the Civil War

  • Abraham Lincoln Papers (Library of Congress) Over 20,000 documents including the Emancipation Proclamation draft, inaugural addresses, and Gettysburg Address copies.
  • Papers of Abraham Lincoln Over 20,000 documents with 62,000 images and 10,000 transcriptions documenting Lincoln's Illinois years (1830–1861).
  • Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library — Digital Resources Official Illinois state collection with Lincoln papers, manuscripts, and Civil War Illinois materials.
  • Camp Douglas Restoration Foundation Resources on the Civil War POW camp in Chicago including photographs, letters, journals, and camp records.
  • Register of Confederate Soldiers at Camp Douglas (Library of Congress) Digitized 1892 register of Confederate soldiers who died at Camp Douglas and were buried in Oakwoods Cemetery.

Gilded Age & Labor (1870s–1900s)

  • The Great Chicago Fire & the Web of Memory Eyewitness accounts, contemporary journalism, illustrations, photographs, diaries, maps, and letters from the 1871 fire.
  • Chronicling America — Great Chicago Fire Coverage Historic newspaper articles from Illinois papers covering the 1871 disaster.
  • The Haymarket Affair Digital Collection (Chicago History Museum) Over 3,800 images including manuscripts, broadsides, photographs, and a searchable 3,323-page trial transcript from 1886.
  • Pullman Strike Digital Collection (NIU Digital Library) Comprehensive primary sources on the 1894 Pullman Strike including labor documents and company records.
  • Jane Addams Digital Edition Searchable collection of published and unpublished works by and about Jane Addams and Hull House social reform.

Early 20th Century

  • 1919 Chicago Race Riot (Newberry Library) Primary sources from The Negro in Chicago report including documents on housing discrimination, labor policies, and newspaper accounts.
  • Coroner’s Official Report of 1919 Race Riot (Illinois State Archives) Official Cook County Coroner Peter Hoffman's report investigating the 1919 riot.
  • Red Summer Archive Over 700 documents including newspaper articles, photographs, comics, reports, letters, and telegrams on the 1919 violence.
  • Chicago History Museum — Online Resources Digital primary sources including photographs, documents, and artifacts from early 20th-century Chicago.

Modern Illinois

  • Illinois Digital Archives Includes the Doc Helm Collection (21,000 digitized photographs of Illinois government, politics, and society 1943–1992).
  • University of Illinois Chicago — Special Collections Materials on Hull House, immigrant communities, urban neighborhoods, and Chicago's civil rights history.
  • Chicago Public Library — History Resources Archival collections, photographs, Civil War artifacts, and Chicago neighborhood histories.

Illinois Government & Law

  • Illinois State Archives Repository of 75,000+ cubic feet of state government records with online databases for vital records, land, military, and census data.
  • Illinois State Archives — Online Databases Searchable databases including Black Hawk War veterans, land records, and historical government documents.
  • Illinois Regional Archives Depository (IRAD) System Network of regional archives at Illinois universities holding local, state, and historical government records.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Illinois Digital Newspaper Collections 3.2+ million digitized pages from 427 Illinois newspapers including Black and ethnic press titles.
  • Chronicling America — Illinois Newspapers 86 digitized Illinois newspapers via the Library of Congress.
  • Illinois Digital Archives Photographs, oral histories, manuscripts, maps, posters, videos, and postcards from Illinois institutions.
  • Newberry Library Digital Collections Digitized manuscripts, maps, books, photographs, and rare materials with emphasis on Chicago and Illinois history.
  • CARLI Digital Collections Federated search across digitized primary sources from academic and research libraries throughout Illinois.
  • University of Illinois Digital Collections Digitized Illinois newspapers, books, maps, photographs, and manuscripts with emphasis on state history.
  • Chicago History Museum — Collections Searchable archive of photographs, manuscripts, and artifacts documenting Chicago's history.
