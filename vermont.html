Abenaki Peoples

  • Vermont History Explorer — The Abenaki and the Europeans Educational materials, maps (including a 1762 Abenaki territory map), and primary sources on the Abenaki peoples of Vermont from pre-contact through the colonial period.
  • Digital Vermont (Vermont Historical Society) Over 1,700 digitized items across 30+ collections including photographs, broadsides, and oral histories documenting Vermont history from its earliest inhabitants.

New Hampshire Grants & Colonial Conflict

  • Vermont History Explorer — The New Hampshire Grants Maps (including a 1780 boundary map), documents, and context on the land-grant dispute between New Hampshire and New York that shaped Vermont’s path to independence.
  • Vermont Historical Society — Benning Wentworth’s Claims Article on the New Hampshire governor’s land-grant claims in the Vermont territory, freely available as PDF.

Green Mountain Boys & the Revolutionary War

  • Vermont History Explorer — The Green Mountain Boys Primary sources, maps, and documents on Ethan Allen’s militia, from the resistance to New York authority through the capture of Fort Ticonderoga (May 1775).
  • Library of Congress — Battle of Bennington Map Manuscript map of the Battle of Bennington battlefield (August 1777), a decisive American victory that helped set the stage for the British defeat at Saratoga.
  • New York State Parks — Bennington Battlefield Historical site materials with primary source documents including soldier diaries and official correspondence from the 1777 battle.

The Vermont Republic (1777–1791)

  • Vermont History Explorer — Vermont State Constitution (1777) The 1777 Vermont Constitution—the first in the Americas to ban adult slavery and establish universal male suffrage regardless of property ownership—with historical context and analysis.
  • Internet Archive — Records of the Governor and Council of Vermont Official governmental records including Council of Safety records (1777–1782) from the period when Vermont functioned as an independent republic.
  • Vermont Historical Society — Making the Vermont Constitution PDF document tracing the creation and development of Vermont’s founding constitutional documents (1777–1824).

Early Abolition: The First Ban on Slavery (1777)

  • Smithsonian NMAAHC — Vermont 1777: Early Steps Against Slavery Essay and primary sources from the National Museum of African American History and Culture on Vermont’s historic abolition of slavery in its 1777 Constitution.
  • Vermont State Archives (VT Re·tain) Digital repository preserving and providing public access to Vermont’s archival state records, including constitutional documents and early governance materials.

Calvin Coolidge

  • Library of Congress — Calvin Coolidge Papers 179,000+ digitized documents (218,513 images) from the largest Coolidge manuscript collection, including letters, speeches, and government documents from the Plymouth Notch–born president.
  • Calvin Coolidge Presidential Foundation — Virtual Library Digitized Coolidge materials including letters, veto messages, proclamations, and press conferences.
  • Digital Vermont — Calvin Coolidge Presidential Photographs Photographs of Calvin Coolidge during his presidency (1923–1929), held by the Vermont Historical Society.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Vermont History Explorer Interactive platform from the Vermont Historical Society with 200+ articles, primary sources, historic photographs, maps, and documents covering all eras of Vermont history.
  • Digital Vermont Aggregator of digitized items from across Vermont institutions, including historic photographs, broadsides, oral histories, and Civil War flags.
  • University of Vermont — Digital Collections Civil War letters and diaries, Vermont photographs, maps, Congressional correspondence, broadsides, music, newspapers, and university publications.
  • Green Mountain Digital Archive (DPLA Hub) 57,000+ digitized items from Vermont institutions, including town photographs, Civil War letters, eighteenth-century broadsides, and local history materials. Vermont’s hub for the Digital Public Library of America.
  • Library of Congress — Vermont State Resource Guide Comprehensive guide to digital collections and resources for Vermont history research.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Vermont Newspapers (Library of Congress) 70 digitized Vermont newspaper titles (1836–1922), fully searchable.
  • University of Vermont — Vermont Digital Newspaper Project Digitized Vermont newspapers from 1836–1922 in 70 titles, funded by NEH as part of the National Digital Newspaper Program.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Vermont Aggregated search across Vermont cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
