Native Nations & Indian Territory

  • National Archives — Dawes Records of the Five Civilized Tribes 64,177 digitized applications (1898–1914) for enrollment in Cherokee, Choctaw, Chickasaw, Creek, and Seminole tribes, plus census cards, land allotment jackets, and maps.
  • Oklahoma Historical Society — American Indian Records Databases of American Indian records, Dawes Final Roll indexes, and the 1890 Oklahoma Territorial Census.
  • University of Oklahoma — Doris Duke Collection of American Indian Oral Histories Transcripts and recordings of interviews (1967–1972) with hundreds of Native Americans in Oklahoma regarding tribal histories, cultures, and community life.
  • University of Oklahoma — Western History Collections Over 2,000 manuscript collections including diaries, journals, correspondence, and business records with strong holdings on Native American tribes, pioneer life, and missionary activities in Indian Territory.
  • Archive of Native American Recorded History Community-guided repository of Native American oral histories, with access determined through community review and tribal protocols.

Trail of Tears Terminus

  • DPLA — Cherokee Removal and the Trail of Tears Primary source set documenting Cherokee removal and its terminus in Indian Territory (present-day Oklahoma).
  • Library of Congress — Indian Removal Act Digital Collections Government documents, manuscripts, maps, and primary sources related to the forced removal of southeastern tribes to Indian Territory.

Land Runs (1889 & 1893)

  • Gateway to Oklahoma History — Land Run Photographs Historic photographs including “Guthrie ten days old, 1889,” “Woman on her claim in 1889,” and Cherokee Outlet Opening images from the Oklahoma Historical Society’s collections.
  • Oklahoma Digital Prairie — Cherokee Strip Land Run Digitized materials on the Cherokee Strip Land Run of September 16, 1893, the largest land run in U.S. history.

All-Black Towns

  • Encyclopedia of Oklahoma History — All-Black Towns Comprehensive article on Oklahoma’s 13 historic all-Black towns: Boley, Brooksville, Clearview, Grayson, Langston, Lima, Red Bird, Rentiesville, Summit, Taft, Tatums, Tullahassee, and Vernon.
  • Oklahoma Digital Prairie — Boley Collection Digital collection on Boley, the largest and most renowned all-Black town in Oklahoma, with over 4,000 residents by 1911.

Tulsa Race Massacre (1921)

  • Oklahoma State University — Tulsa Race Massacre Collection Ruth Sigler Avery Collection with research notes, photocopied documents, videos, audio tapes, transcripts, and interviews documenting the destruction of Greenwood (“Black Wall Street”).
  • Library of Congress — Tulsa Race Massacre in Chronicling America Research guide to contemporary newspaper coverage of the May 31–June 1, 1921 massacre.
  • Yale Beinecke Library — 1921 Tulsa Race Massacre Resources Special collections documenting the massacre from the Beinecke Rare Book & Manuscript Library.

Dust Bowl & Great Depression

  • Library of Congress — Dust Bowl Migration Primary source materials on the Dust Bowl and resulting migration from Oklahoma, including “Okie” migration to California.
  • Photogrammar — FSA/OWI Photographs 170,000 Farm Security Administration photographs (1935–1945), many documenting Oklahoma during the Depression and Dust Bowl, searchable by location.
  • Voices of Oklahoma Oral histories capturing stories of Oklahomans who lived through the Dust Bowl, oil boom, and other transformative periods.

Route 66

  • Oklahoma Historical Society — Route 66 Collection Oral histories, photographs, and manuscript collections on the “Mother Road” at the John & Eleanor Kirkpatrick Research Center.
  • National Park Service — Route 66 Oklahoma Digital Collections Aggregated Route 66 digital collections and repositories for the Oklahoma segment.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Gateway to Oklahoma History Over 600,000 digitized items including historic newspapers (1840s–1920s), photographs, maps, and documents from the Oklahoma Historical Society.
  • Oklahoma Digital Prairie State digital project with government publications, historic photographs, postcards, territorial records, and legal materials. No library card required.
  • OKHUB (Oklahoma Hub of DPLA) Over 100,000 digitized records from Oklahoma institutions discoverable through the Digital Public Library of America.
  • University of Oklahoma — Oklahoma Primary Sources Guide Research guide curating freely accessible primary sources for Oklahoma history.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Oklahoma Newspapers (Library of Congress) Digitized Oklahoma newspaper titles including Indian Territory papers (from 1836), fully searchable through 1963.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Oklahoma Aggregated search across Oklahoma cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
