Native Peoples: Nez Perce, Shoshone-Bannock & Coeur d’Alene

  • Idaho State Historical Society — Digital Collections 800,000+ photographs from the territorial period to present, searchable by subject, including images of Nez Perce, Shoshone-Bannock, and Coeur d’Alene peoples.
  • Library of Congress — Idaho Primary Source Set Curated primary sources documenting Idaho history, including Native American materials and western expansion.

Lewis & Clark Through Idaho

  • University of Nebraska — Journals of the Lewis and Clark Expedition Complete, freely accessible text of the expedition journals, including the arduous crossing of the Bitterroot Mountains and encounters with the Nez Perce.
  • Library of Congress — Lewis and Clark Exhibition Historic maps and exploration documents from the expedition, including materials related to the Idaho portion of the route.

Fur Trade & Mountain Men

  • Mountain Men and the Fur Trade — Online Research Center Digital library, virtual museum, and bibliographic resources on mountain men, trappers, and the fur trade in the Rocky Mountain West.
  • Idaho State Historical Society — Idaho Fur Trade (Reference Series) Reference document on the fur trade in Idaho, freely available as PDF.

Mining: Idaho Gold Rush & Silver Valley

  • Idaho State University — Northern Idaho Mining History Digital resources on northern Idaho mining with maps, diagrams, and educational materials on the Silver Valley and Coeur d’Alene mining district.
  • Idaho Geological Survey — Nineteenth-Century Mining in Central and Southern Idaho Free PDF bulletin on the history of gold and silver mining in Idaho from the 1860s rush onward.

Nez Perce War (1877) & Chief Joseph

  • Library of Congress — Nez Perce War Visual Materials Sketches by G.M. Holland depicting the defeat and surrender of Chief Joseph (October 5–6, 1877).
  • Christopher Newport University — Chief Joseph Primary Sources “An Indian’s Views of Indian Affairs” from The North American Review (April 1879), Chief Joseph’s own account of the war and his people’s flight.
  • Primary Source Nexus — Nez Percé Materials Land cession maps (1784–1894) and period articles on the Nez Perce.

Idaho Labor Wars

  • University of Minnesota Law Library — Big Bill Haywood Trial (1907) Trial transcripts and materials from the sensational murder trial of IWW leader William “Big Bill” Haywood, defended by Clarence Darrow, for the assassination of former Idaho Governor Frank Steunenberg.
  • NYU Special Collections — Big Bill Haywood and the Western Federation of Miners Research materials on Haywood and the WFM’s role in the Coeur d’Alene mining conflicts.

Japanese American Incarceration: Minidoka

  • Densho Digital Repository 900+ oral histories (1,700+ hours), photographs, documents, and newspapers documenting Japanese American incarceration, including materials from the Minidoka War Relocation Center in south-central Idaho.
  • Internet Archive — Final Accountability Roster of Minidoka October 1945 roster scanned from NARA microfilm records, listing all persons processed through the Minidoka camp.

Agriculture & Irrigation: The Snake River

  • Intermountain Histories — Irrigation of the Snake River Historical tour with primary sources on the development of irrigation in the Snake River Plain that transformed Idaho’s desert into productive farmland.
  • Idaho State University — Digital Atlas: Agriculture & Irrigation Maps and data on irrigated acreage and agricultural land use patterns across Idaho.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Digital Library of Idaho Statewide aggregator of photographs, documents, newspapers, maps, and sound recordings from libraries across Idaho.
  • Idaho State Archives (Idaho State Historical Society) Photographs, books, maps, manuscripts, oral histories, and government records at the Merle W. Wells Research Center.
  • University of Idaho — Digital Collections Tens of thousands of rare and unique items openly available, from the state’s flagship university.
  • Boise State University — Digital Collections Primary source materials on Boise, the Treasure Valley, and Idaho state history.
  • Idaho State University — Special Collections 360+ manuscript collections documenting Southeast Idaho and the Intermountain West, including maps, photographs, and oral histories.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Idaho Newspapers (Library of Congress) 87 digitized Idaho newspaper titles from 1863 onward, fully searchable.
  • Intermountain Histories (BYU) Curated histories of Intermountain West sites with primary sources, photographs, video, and audio interviews covering Idaho topics.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Idaho Aggregated search across Idaho cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
