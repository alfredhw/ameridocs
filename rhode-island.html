Narragansett Peoples & King Philip’s War

Roger Williams & the Founding of Providence (1636)

  • Rhode Island State Archives Official state records including colonial charters, the 1663 Royal Charter guaranteeing religious liberty, and early Providence Plantations governance documents.
  • Rhode Island Archival & Manuscript Collections Online (RIAMCO) Union catalog of finding aids from 20+ Rhode Island repositories, including colonial-era manuscripts and Roger Williams–related materials.
  • Internet Archive — Letters of Roger Williams (1632–1682) Digitized correspondence of Rhode Island’s founder, documenting his advocacy for religious freedom and separation of church and state.

Triangular Trade & the Slave Trade

  • Brown University — Slavery and Justice Report Landmark 2006 report documenting Brown University’s and Rhode Island’s deep ties to the transatlantic slave trade, with extensive primary source documentation of Newport’s role in the triangular trade.
  • SlaveVoyages (Emory University) Database of 36,000+ slave voyages with searchable records; Rhode Island merchants were among the most prolific slave traders in North America.

American Industrialization: Slater Mill

  • Library of Congress — HAER: Slater Mill (Pawtucket) Historic American Engineering Record documentation of the Slater Mill (1793), considered the birthplace of the American Industrial Revolution.
  • U.S. Naval War College — Archives Historical records from the Naval War College in Newport (founded 1884), including strategic studies, war gaming records, and materials on Alfred Thayer Mahan’s influential sea power theories.

Immigration & Ethnic Heritage

  • Library of Congress — Rhode Island Folklife Project 17,000+ photographs, sound recordings, and fieldwork documentation from the 1979 Rhode Island Folk Heritage Project, capturing the state’s Portuguese, Italian, Cape Verdean, and Franco-American communities.
  • Brown University — Brown Digital Repository Digital collections including oral histories, community documentation, and ethnic heritage materials from Rhode Island’s diverse immigrant populations.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Rhode Island State Archives Colonial records from 1638, legislative papers, court records, military records, vital statistics, and the Rhode Island Historical Records Survey.
  • Rhode Island Historical Society — Research Collections 350+ years of Rhode Island history in manuscripts, photographs, printed materials, and museum objects, with selected digital collections.
  • Providence Public Library — Digital Collections Digitized materials including Rhode Island sheet music, postcards, city directories, photographs, and local history publications.
  • Brown University — Brown Digital Repository Open-access digital collections from Brown’s library, including manuscripts, maps, and archival materials on Rhode Island and New England history.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Rhode Island Newspapers (Library of Congress) Digitized Rhode Island newspaper titles, fully searchable.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Rhode Island Aggregated search across Rhode Island cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
