Native Peoples & Cherokee Homeland

  • University of Tennessee — Southeastern Native American Tribes Research Guide Research guide with primary sources on Cherokee, Chickasaw, Choctaw, Creek, and Seminole peoples, including Cherokee Indian Fair photographs (1930), John Ross letters (1834–1856), and the Penelope Allen Collection on Cherokee agency records.
  • DPLA — Cherokee Removal and the Trail of Tears Primary source set including documents, letters, and records on Cherokee removal, with John Sevier’s 1803 letter to Cherokee warriors requesting permission to build a road through Cherokee territory.

Frontier Settlement & Early Statehood (1760s–1830s)

  • The Papers of Andrew Jackson (University of Tennessee) Digital editions of Andrew Jackson’s papers and manuscripts, covering his frontier career, military exploits, and political life from Tennessee.
  • University of Tennessee — Digital Collections Digitized manuscripts, photographs, diaries, and oral histories from UT Special Collections, including frontier settlement and migration materials.
  • Library of Congress — Tennessee Digital Collections Guide Comprehensive guide to Tennessee materials across LOC holdings, including early settlement records, land grants, and government documents.

Civil War in Tennessee

  • Tennessee Civil War Sourcebook (TSLA) Searchable database of over 7,000 articles chronicling the Civil War in Tennessee (1861–1865), sourced from diaries, period newspapers, official records, ship deck logs, and letters.
  • University of Tennessee — Civil War Research Guide Primary sources on battles of Shiloh, Chattanooga, Nashville, and Fort Donelson, including William H. Blake’s 1862 Pittsburg Landing letter, H. L. Bedford memoirs (Confederate perspective on Fort Donelson), and George Gates’s 1863 Lookout Mountain letter.
  • Tennessee Virtual Archive (TeVA) — Civil War Collections Photographs, documents, maps, and audio records from Tennessee State Library and Archives relating to the Civil War and Reconstruction periods.

The Scopes Trial (1925)

  • University of Tennessee — Of Monkeys and Men: The Scopes Trial Selected documents and photographs from the Sue K. Hicks Papers (Scopes prosecution lawyer) and W. C. Robinson Collection, including letters and notes from the 1925 trial in Dayton, Tennessee.
  • DPLA — The Scopes Trial Primary Source Set Photographs, texts, and accounts from the 1925 trial, including images of the Dayton high school and Rhea County Courthouse.
  • University of Tennessee — Scopes Trial Research Guide Guide to primary source materials in UT Special Collections related to State of Tennessee v. John T. Scopes.

Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA)

  • University of Tennessee — TVA Research Guides Comprehensive guides to TVA collections including photographs, documents, John L. Neely Jr. Papers (1940–1955), TVA Pamphlet Collection (1933–1965), and TVA Reports (1933–1973).
  • National Archives — Tennessee Valley Authority Act (1933) Digitized full text of the May 18, 1933 act that created TVA as a federal corporation for flood control, navigation, power generation, and regional development.
  • National Archives — Records of the TVA (Record Group 142) Federal records spanning 1917–1994 (bulk 1933–1986), with digitized primary sources accessible through DocsTeach.org.

Civil Rights Movement

Nashville Sit-Ins & the Student Movement

  • Nashville Public Library — Civil Rights Collection Over 100 oral history interviews with civil rights movement participants, plus photographs, documents, periodicals, and ephemera covering Nashville’s 1950s–1960s civil rights era.
  • Vanderbilt University — James M. Lawson Jr. Papers Fully digitized collection of 80 linear feet of manuscripts including correspondence, photographs, and memorabilia on the Nashville sit-ins (1960), nonviolence workshops, and Lawson’s civil rights career. Vanderbilt’s most-accessed online collection.
  • Civil Rights Digital Library — Nashville Sit-Ins Primary sources on the Nashville Student Movement’s sit-in campaign (1959–1960), including materials on Diane Nash and James Lawson’s nonviolence training.

Memphis Sanitation Strike & the Assassination of Dr. King

  • Civil Rights Digital Library — Memphis Sanitation Workers Strike Primary sources on the 1968 strike, including materials on the “I AM A MAN” campaign, the deaths of Echol Cole and Robert Walker, and Dr. King’s “I’ve Been to the Mountaintop” sermon (April 3, 1968).
  • University of Memphis — Benjamin Hooks Institute Collections Benjamin Lawson Hooks Papers (400 boxes of correspondence, speeches, photographs, and audio/video recordings) and approximately 1,200 photographs being digitized for online access.

Music History

  • Country Music Hall of Fame — Digital Collections Over 280,000 digitized items including photographs (500,000+ images from the 1920s to present), recorded sound, 40,000+ films and videos, printed materials, posters, and oral history interviews.
  • Dig Memphis — Memphis Public Libraries Digital Archives Digital treasures of the Memphis and Shelby County Room, including Beale Street blues heritage materials and photographs from the Withers Collection (1.8 million photos of blues, jazz, and civil rights).
  • Tennessee Historical Society — Music History Resources on Nashville’s Grand Ole Opry (broadcasting from WSM since 1925), Memphis Beale Street blues heritage, and Sun Records (founded 1952 by Sam Phillips).

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Tennessee Virtual Archive (TeVA) Open-access repository with thousands of photographs, documents, maps, postcards, film, and audio records from the Tennessee State Library and Archives.
  • Digital Tennessee (TSLA) Gateway to Tennessee state government publications, digitized newspapers, online exhibitions, and educational resources.
  • Volunteer Voices — Tennessee’s Statewide Digitization Program Primary sources from archives, historical societies, libraries, and museums statewide, covering frontier settlement, Civil War, civil rights, and more.
  • Vanderbilt University — Special Collections Digital Collections Over 100,000 digitized primary sources including rare books, manuscripts, and the Vanderbilt Photographic Archive (500,000+ photographs since 1875).
  • Tennessee Encyclopedia Online Reference resource with articles on Tennessee history, some linking to related primary source collections.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Tennessee Newspapers (Library of Congress) 134 digitized Tennessee newspaper titles covering 1756–1963, fully searchable.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Digital Library of Tennessee Aggregated search across Tennessee museums, libraries, and archives contributing maps, photographs, newspapers, postcards, oral histories, and musical performances.
  • Tennessee Electronic Library (TEL) Gateway to multiple digital resources including primary sources, historical newspapers, and collections from Tennessee institutions and the Library of Congress.
