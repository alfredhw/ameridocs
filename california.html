Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • California Indian History Primary sources documenting Euro-American violence against California Indigenous peoples, Gold Rush through late 19th century.
  • California Indian History — Collections Represented Directory of archival collections from multiple institutions with Indigenous source materials.
  • DPLA — Spanish Missions in California Primary source set with documents, photographs, and artwork on the missions and their impact on Native peoples.

Spanish Colonial & Mexican Era

  • Santa Barbara Mission Archive-Library — Mission Documents Original mission manuscripts and sacrament records from the California missions system.
  • Library of Congress — California First-Person Narratives: Missions Essays and primary narratives about missions in early California.
  • California State Archives — Spanish & Mexican Era Collections Original Spanish colonial administrative records and diseños (hand-drawn maps of ranchos).
  • FamilySearch — California Colonial Records Guide to California colonial-era records including mission and rancho documents.

Gold Rush & Early Statehood (1848–1870s)

  • California State Library — Gold Rush Online exhibition of annotated primary sources on the Gold Rush era.
  • UC Berkeley — People of the Gold Rush Guide to Bancroft Library's Gold Rush digital collections: letters, diaries, and images.
  • University of the Pacific — Gold Rush Life Western Americana collection with letters, diaries, and remembrances from Gold Rush participants.
  • Library of Congress — Searching for the Gold Mountain Primary sources on Chinese immigration during the Gold Rush period.

Railroad & Growth Era (1870s–1900s)

  • California State Railroad Museum Library & Archives Primary sources on California railroad history from the 1850s: corporate records, engineering drawings, photographs.
  • Online Archive of California — Railroad Museum Holdings Finding aids and descriptions of railroad museum archival collections.

Early 20th Century

  • 1906 San Francisco Earthquake & Fire (National Archives) Federal agency documents, eyewitness testimony, and records from the disaster.
  • Chronicling America — 1906 Earthquake Coverage Historic newspapers covering the San Francisco earthquake and fire.
  • National Archives — San Francisco Earthquake Legislative Records Congressional and federal records related to earthquake response and relief.
  • California State Archives — Labor History Records Guide to labor history records in the California State Archives.

WWII & Japanese American Internment

  • California State Library — Internment Experience Primary source materials and resource links on Japanese American internment.
  • CSU Sacramento — Japanese American Internment Guide Research guide with primary sources, oral histories, and camp newspapers.
  • National Archives — Japanese Relocation & Internment Official military and government records on internment.
  • Library of Congress — Japanese American Internment Primary source materials and classroom resources on the internment experience.
  • National Archives — Japanese American Incarceration Lessons Educational materials with original government documents.

Postwar & Modern California

  • UCLA — Chicano/a Studies Primary Sources Primary sources on the Chicano movement and civil rights in California.
  • Library of Congress — National Chicano Moratorium Primary sources on the 1970 National Chicano Moratorium in East Los Angeles.
  • Cal State LA — Chicano Movement Primary Sources Collections documenting Chicano movement activism at Cal State LA and beyond.
  • UC Merced — Central Valley Labor History Primary sources on the Central Valley agricultural labor movement.

California Government & Law

  • California State Archives Official repository for historic records of state government, legislature, and courts.
  • California State Archives — Collections Detailed descriptions of all State Archives holdings.
  • Minerva — State Archives Catalog Searchable descriptive catalog of California State Archives records.
  • California State Library — Legislative History Research tools for California legislative history, with bills back to 1903.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • California Digital Newspaper Collection (CDNC) 1.5+ million issues and 23+ million pages of California newspapers from 1846 to present, fully searchable.
  • Chronicling America — California Newspapers Historic California newspapers (1847–1922) via the Library of Congress.
  • Calisphere 2.2+ million digitized items from 300+ California institutions: photographs, documents, newspapers, and more.
  • Online Archive of California (OAC) Finding aids for 60,000+ collections from 350+ institutions, with 250,000+ digitized items.
  • UC Berkeley — Bancroft Library Digital Collections 300,000+ digital items from the largest California history collection in existence.
  • Stanford University — Special Collections 632+ online collections and thousands of physical collections on California and the American West.
  • California State Library — Online Collections State Library's digitized collections and online exhibits.
  • California Revealed Statewide digitization initiative aggregating materials from libraries, archives, and museums across California.
