Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • Clarke Historical Library — Native American Materials (Central Michigan University) Most comprehensive Michigan collection on Ojibwe (Chippewa), Odawa (Ottawa), and Potawatomi peoples, including the 40-volume Michigan Pioneer and Historical Collections, missionary records, treaties, and materials printed in Ojibway language.
  • University of Michigan — Indigenous Peoples Research Guide Research guide with digital resources for Michigan’s Three Fires Confederacy (Ojibwe, Odawa, Potawatomi), including digitized materials and archival finding aids.
  • Library of Michigan — Native Americans Research Guide Curated bibliography and guide to primary and secondary sources on Michigan’s Indigenous peoples.

French Colonial Era & Fur Trade (1600s–1760)

  • Clarke Historical Library — French Colonial Michigan Digital exhibit on French colonial life in 1700s Michigan, including primary source documents on the fur trade, missionaries, and early Detroit (founded 1701).
  • Detroit Historical Society — French Detroit (1701–1760) Primary source materials on French Detroit including maps, objects, and narratives from the colonial period.
  • Library of Congress — Pioneering the Upper Midwest Essays and materials on French colonial exploration, fur trade, and early settlement in the Upper Great Lakes region.

Territorial & Early Statehood (1787–1860)

  • Archives of Michigan — Digital Collections Over 80 million state and local government records dating to 1792, including territorial-era governance documents, land records, and early state papers. Also includes 10 million photographs, 500,000 maps, and audio/film recordings.
  • Library of Michigan — Digital Collections Digitized images, publications, scrapbooks, and multimedia content documenting Michigan from territorial era through statehood (1837) and beyond.
  • Michiganology (Archives of Michigan) Online portal for digitized vital records, military records, historical maps, and state documents from Michigan’s earliest periods.

Automotive Industry & Industrial Detroit

  • Ford Heritage Vault Over 19,000 Ford Motor Company materials including brochures, photographs, press releases, concept car designs, and 300+ issues of Ford Times spanning 1903–2003. Freely searchable and downloadable.
  • The Henry Ford — Digital Collections Ford Motor Company records, photographs, drawings, letters, reports, motion picture film, and over 200 oral history transcripts from Ford’s 50th anniversary project (1951).
  • Detroit Public Library — National Automotive History Collection Photographs and postcards of Detroit’s automobile industry during the first half of the 20th century, plus the Burton Historical Collection with 660,000+ digitized maps.
  • Detroit Institute of Arts — Diego Rivera Detroit Industry Murals High-resolution images of the 27-panel fresco series (1932–1933) depicting industrial Detroit and Ford River Rouge operations. Designated a National Historic Landmark in 2014.
  • Detroit Historical Society — Digital Collection Over 47,000 items from 250,000+ artifacts spanning 300 years of Detroit history, including industrial-era photographs, documents, and manuscripts.

Labor History & the UAW

  • Walter P. Reuther Library — Archives of Labor and Urban Affairs (Wayne State) Major labor archives with extensive UAW holdings, Flint sit-down strike organizers’ papers (Henry Kraus), and records of Michigan labor actions from the 1930s onward.
  • Wayne State University — Digital Collections Digitized archival materials including labor history photographs, manuscripts, and union records from the Reuther Library and other Wayne State collections.
  • University of Michigan-Flint — Labor History Project Presentations, sound recordings, and searchable transcriptions of interviews with Flint sit-down strike witnesses, collected in the late 1970s and early 1980s.
  • Library of Congress — Flint Sit-Down Strike Guide Research guide on the 1936–1937 Flint sit-down strike, with links to primary sources on what is considered one of the most important strikes in American labor history.

Great Migration & African American Detroit

  • Library of Congress — Great Migration Primary Sources Photographs, documents, and historical records on the Great Migration (1910–1970), with Detroit prominently featured as a major destination city.
  • National Archives — Great Migration (1910–1970) Federal records and digitized documents on the mass movement of African Americans to northern industrial cities including Detroit.
  • Eastern Michigan University — African American Digital Collections Over 16,500 pages of texts, 8,300 illustrations, and 67 maps documenting African American migration and history.

The 1967 Detroit Uprising

  • Detroit Historical Society — Detroit 67 Project Dedicated online archive for the 1967 uprising featuring oral histories, primary source documents, and community-contributed materials.
  • University of Michigan — Detroit Under Fire: Police Violence & Civil Rights Major digital archive with extensive primary sources on the 1967 uprising, organized chronologically with documents, photographs, and oral histories.
  • Wayne State University — 12th Street Detroit Project Digital project with primary sources on the July 23–August 4, 1967 events, including materials on the police raid that triggered the uprising.
  • Detroit Historical Society — 1967 Resources Curated primary sources including Kerner Commission documents, Michigan State Police records, and Mayor Jerome P. Cavanagh papers.

Upper Peninsula: Mining & Lumber

  • Michigan Tech — Keweenaw Digital Archives Over 13,000 digitized images documenting copper mining and Keweenaw history, with geological records and drilling data from 650+ holes (1899–1970).
  • Northern Michigan University — Recorded in Stone: Voices on the Marquette Iron Range Documents, photographs, and oral histories documenting immigration to the Marquette Iron Range and the iron mining communities of the Upper Peninsula.
  • Library of Congress — Pioneering the Upper Midwest Collection Digital collection with essays and materials on mining, logging, and settlement in Michigan’s Upper Peninsula.

Immigration & Ethnic Communities

  • Bentley Historical Library — Polish Americans in Michigan Subject guide to digitized collections and finding aids for Polish American materials in Michigan archives.
  • University of Michigan — Americanization of Finnish People in Houghton County Digitized historical account by Clemens Niemi of Finnish assimilation in Michigan’s copper country.
  • Michigan State University — Immigration Primary Sources Research guide with links to primary source collections on the various ethnic communities that settled in Michigan.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Michigan Newspapers (Library of Congress) 56 digitized Michigan newspaper titles, fully searchable, with coverage through 1963.
  • Bentley Historical Library — Image Bank (University of Michigan) Digitized images from diverse collections documenting Michigan history, organizational records, and university history.
  • William L. Clements Library — Image Bank (University of Michigan) Digital images of early Americana with strengths in 18th–19th century history, including the David V. Tinder collection of over 100,000 Michigan photographs.
  • University of Michigan Library — Digital Collections Portal Unified search across nine history image databases related to Michigan and university history.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Michigan Aggregated search across Michigan libraries, archives, and museums contributing to DPLA.
