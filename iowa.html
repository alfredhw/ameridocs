Native Peoples: Meskwaki, Sauk & Ioway

  • Teaching Iowa History — Meskwaki Resources Curated primary sources on Meskwaki (Fox) Native Americans, including materials on their unique status as the only tribe in Iowa to purchase their own land rather than accept a reservation.
  • Iowa PBS — Black Hawk Treaty (1832) Digital access to the treaty signed September 21, 1832, at present-day Davenport, ceding Sauk and Meskwaki lands following the Black Hawk War.

Black Hawk War (1832)

  • Northern Illinois University — Black Hawk War Collection Primary source documents, introductory essays, and historical accounts of the 1832 conflict between U.S. forces and Black Hawk’s confederation.
  • Wisconsin Historical Society — Black Hawk War Sources Primary documents including accounts of the Battle of Wisconsin Heights and Bad Axe, with perspectives from both Henry Dodge and Black Hawk.

Pioneer Settlement & Prairie Life

  • University of Iowa — Pioneer Lives Letters, diaries, and photographs documenting the lives of Iowa’s first white settlers from the mid- to late 19th century.
  • State Historical Society of Iowa — Primary Source Sets Downloadable primary source sets addressing Iowa-specific and national history topics, developed with Library of Congress support.

Iowa & the Civil War

  • University of Iowa — Civil War Diaries and Letters Diaries, correspondence, and photographs from Iowa soldiers and their families (1861–1865), with scanned documents and transcriptions of daily activities, battle experiences, and views on the war.

Immigration: Czech, German & Scandinavian Communities

  • Library of Congress — American Folklife Center: Iowa Collections Recordings from the Ethnic Heritage and Language Schools Project (1982) documenting Czech, German, Norwegian, and other immigrant cultural traditions and festivals in Iowa.
  • Amana Heritage Society Resources on the Amana Colonies, a German Pietist communal settlement (1855–1932) in the Iowa River Valley that operated as one of the longest-lasting utopian communities in the United States.
  • Library of Congress — Amana Colonies (HABS) Historic American Buildings Survey documentation and photographs of Amana Colonies structures.

Agriculture & Farm Life

  • State Historical Society of Iowa — Agricultural History Collections Materials from multiple state partners highlighting over a century of writing, research, and collecting on Iowa agriculture.
  • Iowa State University — Agriculture and Rural Life Collections Over 700 archival collections documenting Midwest agriculture, with particular strength in the Garst family papers and farm life from the late 19th century forward.
  • Iowa State University — Digital Collections Digitized materials focused on agriculture, rural life, design, engineering, and technology from ISU’s extensive special collections.

Grant Wood & Iowa Arts

  • University of Iowa — Grant Wood Collections Over 12 digitized scrapbooks and albums assembled by Wood’s sister Nan Wood Graham, including news clippings, photographs, postcards, letters, and ephemera. Plus Grant Wood Papers with articles, correspondence, and exhibition materials.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • State Historical Society of Iowa — Digital Resources Free access to over 400,000 pages of digitized Iowa newspapers, government documents, maps, photographs, and manuscripts.
  • University of Iowa — Iowa Digital Library Over 300,000 freely accessible digital objects including photographs, maps, sound recordings, documents, and historic newspapers.
  • Iowa Heritage Digital Collections Statewide portal bringing together digital collections from multiple Iowa cultural institutions.
  • Teaching Iowa History — Online Collections Over 1,000 primary sources from 100+ participating museums with grade-specific lessons and Iowa history narratives.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Iowa Newspapers (Library of Congress) Digitized Iowa newspaper titles, fully searchable through 1963.
  • Library of Congress — Iowa Digital Collections Guide Guide to Iowa materials across LOC holdings.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Iowa Aggregated search across Iowa cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
