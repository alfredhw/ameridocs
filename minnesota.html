Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • Minnesota Historical Society — Native Minnesota Primary Sources Comprehensive primary source materials on Dakota and Ojibwe peoples, including archaeological artifacts and historical documents.
  • Minnesota Digital Library — Stella Stocker and the Ojibwe of Minnesota Early 20th-century Ojibwe photography collection documenting daily life, music, and culture.
  • MNHS — Dakota and Ojibwe Homeland Research Guide Curated guide for research into Dakota and Ojibwe history with primary sources and archival materials.
  • MNHS — Dakota Family History Manuscripts Manuscript collections, genealogical records, and archival materials documenting Dakota peoples.

Exploration & Fur Trade Era

  • MNHS — Fur Trade Primary Source Guide La Vérendrye journals (1738–1739), voyageur correspondence (1782–1804), business documents, and trade agreements.
  • MNHS — Fur Trade at Fort Snelling Primary documents on French and British fur trade operations, Northwest Company records, and trading post activities.
  • MNHS — Great Lakes Indigenous People and the French Documents exploring French-Indigenous relationships during the fur trade period, including diplomatic correspondence.
  • French Heritage Corridor — Minnesota Resources Primary documents from French colonial and fur trade exploration across Minnesota territory.

Territory & Early Statehood (1849–1860s)

  • MNHS — State Archives Collection Legislative documents, territorial records, constitutional proceedings, and statehood documentation (1849–1858).
  • MNHS Library — Territorial Records Territorial censuses (1849, 1853, 1855, 1857), county records, land surveys, and early settlement documents.
  • Minnesota Legislative Reference Library — Historical Resources Original legislative materials, session laws, journals, and committee records from the territorial period through statehood.

U.S.–Dakota War of 1862

  • MNHS — U.S.-Dakota War Primary Sources Comprehensive guide to Dakota Conflict manuscripts, trial transcripts, letters, and official military records.
  • MNHS — U.S.-Dakota War Resources Hub Trial transcripts, presidential documents, official reports, and educational primary source materials.
  • MNHS — Dakota Conflict of 1862 Manuscripts Finding aid to letters, reminiscences, diaries, and reports documenting personal experiences of participants.

Immigration & Settlement (1860s–1920s)

  • Immigration History Research Center Archives (University of Minnesota) Comprehensive collection of Scandinavian, German, German-Russian, Polish, and other immigrant documents and materials.
  • MNHS — Naturalization & Immigration Records Naturalization records, passenger lists, and immigration documents for Scandinavian and European settlers.
  • Minnesota Digital Library — Mining on the Iron Range Photographs and documents detailing immigrant miners on the Mesabi, Vermilion, and Cuyuna ranges (1880s–1920s).
  • FamilySearch — Minnesota Emigration and Immigration Passenger lists, naturalization records, and settlement documentation for multiple immigrant groups.

20th Century: Labor, Industry & War

  • Minnesota Digital Library — Minneapolis Teamsters Strike of 1934 The Organizer strike bulletin (June–October 1934), photographs, and labor movement documents.
  • MNHS — Truckers’ Strike of 1934 Primary Sources Correspondence, telegrams, speeches, broadsides, and legal documents from Local 574 strike archives.
  • Minnesota Digital Library — Flour Milling in Minnesota Photographs of Minneapolis flour mills, Washburn ‘A’ Mill explosion materials, and milling operations documentation.
  • MNHS — General Mills Primary Sources Company records, advertisements, cookbooks, and oral histories of mill workers and home economists.
  • MNHS — Iron Range Miners’ Strikes (1907 & 1916) Labor strike records, testimony transcripts, and correspondence documenting disputes on the Iron Range.
  • MNHS — Taconite Mining Collections Edward W. Davis Papers, Mesabi Iron Company Records, and mining development documentation.
  • MNHS — Women in the Military (WWII) WAC/WAAC service records, scrapbooks, photographs, and military documents from female service members.
  • MNHS — Home Front During WWII Victory bond drive materials, defense council records, home front work documentation, and civilian wartime activities.

Minnesota Government & Law

  • Minnesota State Archives Official state government records, executive office papers, and administrative documents since the territorial period.
  • MNHS — Collections Search Searchable database of photographs, artifacts, records, maps, oral histories, and manuscripts across all collections.
  • Minnesota Legislative Reference Library Legislative records, bills, session laws, journals, and committee minutes.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Minnesota Digital Library 61,000+ items from 212 libraries, archives, museums, and historical societies: photographs, maps, documents, and oral histories.
  • Minnesota Digital Newspaper Hub Millions of pages of Minnesota newspapers (1849–present) searchable by date, region, and keyword; includes labor, ethnic, and reservation papers.
  • Chronicling America — Minnesota Newspapers 119 digitized Minnesota newspapers via the Library of Congress (1849–1920s).
  • Library of Congress — Minnesota Guide Curated guide to Minnesota resources in Library of Congress collections including maps, manuscripts, and photographs.
  • University of Minnesota — Primary Sources A–Z Interdisciplinary guide to primary source databases and collections accessible through UMN libraries.
