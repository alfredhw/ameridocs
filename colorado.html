Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • Utah American Indian Digital Archive — Ute Materials Digital collections including Ute language, history, and cultural materials relevant to the Ute people whose territory encompassed much of present-day Colorado.
  • University of Colorado Boulder — Indigenous Languages Guide Language learning resources for Cheyenne, Ute, and Arapaho, with links to archival collections on Native American history in Colorado and the Four Corners region.

Pike’s Peak Gold Rush & Mining Era (1858–1900)

  • Denver Public Library — Digital Collections Over 1.1 million items including 600,000+ photographs, 250,000+ books and pamphlets, and 4,000+ manuscript archives on Western history. Extensive mining-era photographs, maps, and personal papers.
  • Denver Public Library — Western History Subject Index 650,000 digitized citation cards from Colorado and Western periodicals (1850s–1990s), providing indexed access to gold rush, silver boom, and mining-town coverage.
  • Colorado Encyclopedia — Colorado Gold Rush Contextual article with primary source references on the 1858–1861 Pike’s Peak gold rush and the founding of Denver.
  • History Colorado — Mines & Mining Bibliography Research bibliography for Colorado mining history with references to primary sources across state collections.

Sand Creek Massacre (1864)

  • National Park Service — Sand Creek Massacre National Historic Site Interpretive materials, congressional investigation records, and primary documents on the November 29, 1864 massacre of Cheyenne and Arapaho people by U.S. troops under Colonel John Chivington.
  • University of Denver — Sand Creek Massacre Research Guide Curated guide to primary sources including the letters of Captain Silas S. Soule and Lieutenant Joseph A. Cramer, who condemned the massacre and testified before Congress.
  • Sand Creek Massacre Documents Digitized primary documents including congressional testimony, military correspondence, and eyewitness accounts.

Railroad Era & Settlement

  • Denver Public Library — Denver & Rio Grande Western Railroad Collection 197 linear feet of railroad company records (1870–1974) including maps, photographs, and operational documents from the narrow-gauge railroad that opened Colorado’s mountain interior.
  • Archives West — D&RGW Railroad Finding Aid Finding aid for Denver & Rio Grande Western Railroad materials held across western institutions.

Women’s Suffrage (1893)

  • Library of Congress — History of Equal Suffrage in Colorado, 1868–1898 Digitized full text of the history of Colorado’s suffrage movement, from the National American Woman Suffrage Association collection.
  • Colorado Virtual Library — Women’s Suffrage in Historic Newspapers Newspaper coverage of the 1893 campaign that made Colorado the first state to grant women the vote by popular referendum.
  • NewseumED — Colorado Women’s Suffrage Bill (1893) Digitized image and context for the original 1893 suffrage legislation.

Ludlow Massacre & the Colorado Labor Wars (1903–1914)

  • CSU Pueblo — Ludlow Massacre Research Guide Primary source guide to the April 20, 1914 attack on striking coal miners and their families at Ludlow, including archival collections, photographs, and oral histories.
  • University of Denver — Colorado Coalfield War Archaeological Project Archaeological and historical research on the 1913–1914 southern Colorado coal strike, combining material culture with documentary evidence.
  • Library of Congress — Ludlow Massacre in Chronicling America Guide to newspaper coverage of the massacre in Chronicling America, with search strategies for locating primary accounts.

Japanese American Incarceration: Amache (Granada)

  • Amache Preservation Society Historical overview, photographs, and resources on the Granada War Relocation Center (Camp Amache), which held over 7,300 Japanese Americans from 1942 to 1945. Designated a National Historic Site in 2022.
  • History Colorado — Amache Primary Resource Set PDF collection of primary source documents for teaching about Amache, including camp photographs and government records.
  • National Park Service — Amache National Historic Site NPS resources including historical photographs, the Granada Pioneer camp newspaper (October 1942–September 1945), and interpretive materials.
  • Densho Digital Repository — Amache Collections Oral histories, photographs, and documents related to Amache within Densho’s broader Japanese American incarceration archive.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • History Colorado — Online Collections Searchable holdings of the state historical society including manuscripts, maps, and photographs. Over 1,700 microfilmed newspaper titles (1859–present) and 2,500+ historical maps.
  • Colorado State Archives Searchable database of state and local government records including naturalization records, vital records, and historical public documents.
  • University of Colorado Boulder — ArchivesSpace Finding aids for over 1,800 archival collections, 150,000+ digitized items, 4,600+ reels of moving image film, and 25,000+ historical glacier photographs.
  • Colorado Encyclopedia Free reference resource with articles on Colorado history, many linking to related primary source collections.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Colorado Newspapers (Library of Congress) Nearly 300,000 pages of digitized Colorado newspapers, fully searchable, with coverage through 1963.
  • Colorado Historic Newspapers Collection (CHNC) Over 5.3 million digitized pages from 840+ newspaper titles (1859–2024), maintained by History Colorado and Colorado State Library. Extends well beyond Chronicling America holdings.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Colorado Aggregated search across Colorado cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
