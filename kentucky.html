Native Peoples & Early Exploration

  • Project Gutenberg — Daniel Boone, Pioneer of Kentucky (John S. C. Abbott) Full text of the historical account of Daniel Boone’s explorations through Shawnee and Cherokee territory and the settlement of Kentucky’s Bluegrass region.
  • ExploreUK (University of Kentucky) Gateway to rare and unique research materials from UK Libraries, including early exploration manuscripts, frontier-era documents, and Kentuckiana collections of county histories, biographies, photographs, and pamphlets.

Henry Clay & Antebellum Politics

  • University of Kentucky — Henry Clay Papers Finding Aid Finding aid for Clay papers (1813–1852) held at the Special Collections Research Center.
  • Internet Archive — Papers of Henry Clay Freely available digital copies of compiled volumes of the “Great Compromiser’s” papers, letters, and speeches.

Slavery & the Border State Experience

  • Library of Congress — Born in Slavery: Slave Narratives Over 2,300 first-person accounts with 500 photographs from the Federal Writers’ Project; Volume 7 covers Kentucky narratives.
  • University of Kentucky — Researching African American Slavery in Kentucky Research guide covering archives, newspapers, census records, and court documents related to slavery in Kentucky as a border state.

Civil War: A Divided Border State (1861–1865)

  • Civil War Governors of Kentucky — Digital Documentary Edition Thousands of freely accessible historical documents from Union governors (Magoffin, Robinson, Bramlette) and Confederate provisional governors (Johnson, Hawes), exploring Kentucky’s agonizing divided loyalties during the war.

Appalachian Coal Mining & the Harlan County Wars

  • University of Kentucky — Coal Strike Collections “A Strike Against Starvation and Terror”—primary sources from the 1931–1939 Harlan County coal miners’ strikes that drew national attention to Appalachian labor conditions.
  • Kentucky Historical Society — Pass the Word Oral History Database Searchable database of over 30,000 oral history interviews including extensive materials on Harlan County labor conflicts and Appalachian mining communities.

Appalachian Culture & Eastern Kentucky

  • Berea College — Digital Collections 24/7 online access to digitized collections documenting Berea College history, local history, and Southern Appalachian cultures.
  • Berea College — Appalachian Oral Histories Searchable oral history collections with transcripts covering Appalachian life, culture, music, and community history.
  • Berea College — Digital Library of Appalachia Archival materials from Appalachian College Association member libraries documenting the region’s culture and history.
  • Eastern Kentucky University — Special Collections Browseable online collections including “Appalachian Kentucky Ephemera” (1888–2000).

Louisville & Muhammad Ali

  • Muhammad Ali Digital Museum Interactive online platform with digitized archives documenting Ali’s life as athlete, humanitarian, and public figure, featuring quarterly digital exhibits and recorded oral histories.
  • Muhammad Ali Center — Collections and Archive Premier repository preserving Ali’s legacy with historical records, photographs, film, audio, and video materials in Louisville.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Kentucky Department for Libraries and Archives — Digital Collections Digitized public records of state and local government agencies with free online searchable access.
  • Kentucky Digital Library Shared digital archival collections from multiple Kentucky institutions including universities, historical societies, and libraries, featuring oral histories, photographs, maps, and primary documents.
  • Kentucky Historical Society — Digital Collections Over 20,000 images of manuscripts, maps, and photographs, plus 85,000+ museum artifact images and finding aids for archival collections.
  • Filson Historical Society — Digital Collections Digitized manuscripts, photographs, audiovisual materials, and museum items related to Ohio Valley region history, including back issues of the Filson Quarterly.
  • Library of Congress — Kentucky Digital Collections Guide Comprehensive guide to Kentucky materials across LOC holdings.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Kentucky Newspapers (Library of Congress) 80 digitized Kentucky newspaper titles representing six geographic regions, fully searchable.
  • Kentucky Digital Newspaper Program 78 historic newspaper titles digitized (2005–2013) as part of the National Digital Newspaper Program.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Kentucky Aggregated search across Kentucky cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
