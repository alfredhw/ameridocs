Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • University of Oregon — Native American & Alaska Native Materials Research guide linking to archival materials on Oregon tribes including Chinook, Klamath, Nez Perce, and Warm Springs.
  • Oregon Multicultural Archives (Oregon State University) Photographs, manuscripts, and cultural materials documenting Oregon's Native American communities.
  • Confluence Project — Tribes of the Columbia River System Interactive resource documenting Columbia River Basin tribes with maps, oral histories, and cultural information.
  • National Archives — Pacific Northwest Native Communities Guide Federal records guide identifying archival resources on Pacific Northwest tribes including Oregon nations.

Exploration & Fur Trade

  • Journals of the Lewis and Clark Expedition (University of Nebraska) Nearly 5,000 pages of expedition journals with images and supplemental texts; covers the Oregon portion of the expedition.
  • OHS Digital Collections — Hudson’s Bay Company Fur trade records, correspondence, maps, and operational documents (1840s–1850s) from Oregon Historical Society.
  • Hudson’s Bay Company Records (Archives West) Finding aids to HBC records including fur importation ledgers, letters, and Fort Vancouver chaplain correspondence (1830–1934).
  • Oregon Encyclopedia — Fur Trade in Oregon Country Scholarly article with embedded primary source documents on the fur trade era (1805–1840s).

Oregon Trail & Settlement (1840s–1860s)

  • State Library of Oregon — Oregon Trail Resources Emigrant resources, Oregon Trail maps, and pictorial maps of overland routes and the Donation Land Claim era.
  • Oregon Provisional & Territorial Government Records (Archives West) Original and transcribed records (1843–1859): laws, court notices, and land claim documents.
  • Early Oregon Census & Tax Records (Archives West) Census and tax records from Provisional/Territorial government and early statehood (1842–1880).
  • State Library of Oregon — Territorial Government Act (1848) Full text of the act establishing the territorial government of Oregon, digitized and searchable.

Statehood & Growth (1859–1900s)

  • Oregon Encyclopedia — Chinese Exclusion Act in Oregon Primary source packet with digitized legal documents including Oregon's racial intermarriage act (1866) and Chinese restriction correspondence.
  • Oregon Chinese Disinterment Documents (Oregon State University) Exhibit documenting discriminatory laws against Chinese immigrants with digitized legislative records.
  • Library of Congress — Chinese Exclusion Act Digital Collections Case files, immigration applications, affidavits, and interrogation transcripts from Portland (1890–1914).
  • National Archives at Seattle — Chinese Immigration Case Files INS case files for Chinese immigrants in Portland (1890–1914): forms, photographs, and court records.

20th Century

Vanport Flood & WWII

  • Vanport Flood 1948 Collections (Archives West) Multiple photograph collections documenting the Vanport flood disaster including aerial views of damage.
  • Oregon State Archives — Japanese Americans in WWII Digital exhibit on the Portland Assembly Center with primary documents and historical photographs.
  • Japanese American Museum of Oregon — Collections Photographs, government documents, family letters, and 74 videotaped oral history interviews on the internment experience.
  • Oregon History Project — Japanese Evacuees Digitized primary sources and annotated documents on internment from OHS collections.

Labor & Environment

  • Oregon Encyclopedia — Labor Movement Peer-reviewed articles with embedded primary sources on Oregon's 20th-century labor history and unions.

Oregon Government & Law

  • Oregon State Archives Official repository of Oregon government records; searchable online archives and historical records dating to the territorial period.
  • State Library of Oregon — Digital Collections Government publications, territorial and statehood records, maps, and Oregoniana; 2.3+ million pages of historical documents.
  • Archives West — Oregon Institutions Search Unified access to finding aids from Oregon State University, University of Oregon, OHS, and other institutional archives.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Oregon Digital 500,000+ digitized works from University of Oregon and Oregon State University: photographs, manuscripts, publications, and maps.
  • Oregon Historical Society — Digital Collections 36,000+ items: photographs, manuscripts, maps, publications, oral histories, and films.
  • Oregon History Project OHS-curated 1,000+ digitized and annotated primary sources organized by theme, region, and chronology.
  • Chronicling America — Oregon Newspapers 67 digitized Oregon newspapers (1846–2017), 800,000+ pages fully searchable.
  • OHS Maps Collection Digitized maps from a 25,000+ item collection documenting exploration, territorial development, mining, forestry, and land claims.
  • Oregon Encyclopedia 1,800+ peer-reviewed essays on Oregon history with embedded digitized primary sources.
  • Library of Congress — Oregon Guide Comprehensive guide to Library of Congress resources on Oregon including digitized maps, documents, and photographs.
