Alaska Native Peoples & Languages

  • Alaska Native Language Archive (University of Alaska Fairbanks) Premier repository for Alaska Native languages (Tlingit, Haida, Yup’ik, Inupiat, Aleut/Unangan, and more), housing 15,000+ documents and 5,000+ audio recordings dating to 1943.
  • Sealaska Heritage Institute — Archives & Collections 3,100 linear feet of materials documenting Tlingit, Haida, and Tsimshian languages, culture, and history, including 60,000+ photographs and 5,000+ audio/audiovisual recordings.
  • Project Jukebox (University of Alaska Fairbanks) Digital branch of UAF’s Oral History Program with 13,000+ recorded interviews, synchronized audio/video with transcripts, maps, historic photographs, and films covering all major Alaska history topics.

Russian America (1733–1867)

  • Library of Congress — Yudin Collection of Russian-American Company Records 166 items (1,369 images) of Russian-American Company records (1783–1830) documenting colonial operations, fur trade, missionary efforts, and Russian settlement in Alaska.
  • Library of Congress — Meeting of Frontiers: Russian Colonization Essays and digital collections on Russian colonization of Alaska within the broader Meeting of Frontiers project.

Gold Rush Era (1897–1900s)

  • University of Washington — Eric A. Hegg Photographs (1897–1901) 730 digitized photographs by Swedish-born photographer Eric A. Hegg documenting the Klondike and Alaska gold rushes, including frontier life in Skagway, Dawson, and Nome.
  • UAA/APU Consortium Library — Gold Rush Archival Collections Guide to gold rush collections including photographs, diaries, and documents at Alaska libraries and archives.
  • Library of Congress — Alaska Gold Rush in Chronicling America Research guide to gold rush–era newspaper coverage available through Chronicling America, with search strategies for locating primary accounts.

Salmon, Whaling & Maritime Industries

  • Alaska Historical Society — Alaska Fisheries History Resources Comprehensive guide to fisheries history resources including cannery records and oral histories documenting Alaska’s salmon industry.
  • Alaska State Library — Whaling in Alaska Curated collection of whaling-related primary sources in Alaska collections.

World War II: Aleutian Campaign & Aleut Relocation

Aleutian Islands Campaign (1942–1943)

  • National Park Service — Aleutian Islands World War II National Historic Area Interpretive materials, congressional investigation records, photographs, and primary documents on the Japanese invasion and U.S. recapture of Attu and Kiska.
  • Alaska State Library — WWII in Alaska Comprehensive guide to WWII Alaska materials including Aleutian Campaign documents, photographs, and related collections.

Unangax̂ (Aleut) Relocation

  • Alaska State Archives — Aleut Relocation During WWII Primary source materials on the forced removal of Alaska Natives from Aleutian and Pribilof Islands and their internment in camps in Southeast Alaska.
  • National Park Service — WWII Aleut Relocation Camps in Southeast Alaska Detailed articles on Aleut relocation camps with primary source documentation and photographs.

Alaska Statehood (1959)

  • Eisenhower Presidential Library — Alaska Statehood Documents including Proclamation #3269 for Alaska’s admission (January 3, 1959), memos, press releases, and presidential statements.
  • University of Alaska Fairbanks — Alaska Statehood Research Guide Research guide to statehood-era materials with links to primary sources at UAF and across Alaska institutions.
  • Library of Congress — Territories to Statehood in Chronicling America Research guide to newspaper coverage of Alaska and Hawaii statehood movements through Chronicling America.

Trans-Alaska Pipeline

  • Alaska State Library — Petroleum & Natural Gas Collections Guide to pipeline construction and oil industry materials in state library collections, including photographs and operational documents.
  • Alaska State Archives — Pipeline Records State archives materials on the Trans-Alaska Pipeline including aerial photographs, environmental reports, and field documents (1974–1979).

Exxon Valdez Oil Spill (1989)

  • Alaska State Library — Exxon Valdez Oil Spill Primary Sources Comprehensive guide to EVOS materials across Alaska institutions, with digitized items available through VILDA.
  • UAA/APU Consortium Library — Exxon Valdez Archival Collections Detailed research guide to oil spill collections including oral histories, documents, and photographs.
  • Valdez Museum — Exxon Valdez Collection Newspaper clippings, U.S. Coast Guard reports, field notes, letters, photographs, art, poetry, and songs documenting the spill and its aftermath.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Alaska’s Digital Archives (VILDA) Over 1 million items including historical photographs, oral histories, moving images, maps, and documents from libraries, museums, and archives throughout Alaska.
  • Alaska State Archives Approximately 26TB of digital materials including digitized territorial records (1884–1959) and state agency records.
  • University of Alaska Fairbanks — Alaska and Polar Regions Collections and Archives Collections focused on Alaska history, subsistence, Indigenous cultures, and polar regions, with finding aids for over 1,800 archival collections.
  • UAA/APU Consortium Library — Archives and Special Collections Collections on Alaska history, anthropology, public policy, and more from the University of Alaska Anchorage and Alaska Pacific University.
  • Anchorage Museum — Digital Collections Access to 26,000+ objects and 700,000+ photographs and archival materials on Alaska history and culture.
  • Library of Congress — Alaska Purchase in Chronicling America Research guide to newspaper coverage of the 1867 Alaska Purchase.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Alaska Newspapers (Library of Congress) Digitized Alaska newspaper titles, fully searchable.
  • Alaska Digital Newspaper Program Official program preserving Alaska’s historical newspapers, with over 800 microfilm titles in the state collection and new digitizations added regularly.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Alaska Aggregated search across Alaska cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
