Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • Dawnland Voices — Indigenous New England Digital Collections Collaborative archive of New England Indigenous writings, including Mohegan-Pequot materials and Fidelia Fielding’s Mohegan-Pequot diary (1904).
  • Connecticut State Library — Native American Research Guide Guide to resources on the Pequot, Mohegan, Schaghticoke, Golden Hill Paugussett, and other Connecticut nations.
  • National Park Service — Connecticut Indigenous & Early Contact NPS materials covering Native peoples of Connecticut and early colonial encounters.

Colonial Connecticut & the Fundamental Orders (1630s–1776)

  • Online Library of Liberty — Fundamental Orders of Connecticut (1639) Full text of the Fundamental Orders with original spelling, often considered the first written constitution in the Western tradition.
  • Avalon Project (Yale Law School) — New Haven Colony Documents Fundamental Agreement and Original Constitution of the Colony of New Haven (June 4, 1639).
  • Teaching American History — Fundamental Orders Full text and educational context for the 1639 Fundamental Orders.
  • Connecticut History (Connecticut Humanities) — Fundamental Orders Interpretive article with historical context on the significance of the Fundamental Orders.
  • Connecticut State Library — Connecticut Archives: Colonial Records Digitized papers of the Connecticut General Assembly from its beginnings through 1820, including colonial governance documents.
  • Early American Sources — Connecticut Collections Directory linking to colonial-era materials across Connecticut Historical Society, Beinecke Library, State Library, and Mystic Seaport.

Revolutionary War (1763–1789)

  • Connecticut State Library — Revolutionary War Archives 37 volumes and index of “Connecticut Archives: Revolutionary War, 1763–1789,” covering military operations, supply records, and legislative wartime proceedings.
  • Connecticut State Library — Revolutionary War Military Records Guide Research guide to Connecticut service records, pension files, and published rosters from the American Revolution.
  • TeachIt Connecticut — American Revolution Primary Sources Gallery of Connecticut-specific primary source materials from the Revolutionary era.

Industrial Revolution & Manufacturing (1790s–1900)

  • Connecticut State Library — Colt Patent Fire Arms Collection Factory records, manuscripts, and photographs from Colt’s Manufacturing Company, received by the state library in 1957.
  • Connecticut History — Connecticut Arms the Union Resources on Connecticut firearms manufacturing during the Civil War, including Whitney Arms, Colt, and Savage Revolving Firearms companies.
  • Connecticut History Illustrated — Industry Collections Featured collections on Connecticut’s manufacturing heritage in firearms, brass, textiles, and precision machinery.

Abolition & Underground Railroad

  • Connecticut History — James Lindsey Smith & the Underground Railroad Account of an enslaved man’s escape to freedom through Connecticut via the Underground Railroad network.
  • Wesleyan University — Cross Street AME Zion Church History Materials on Middletown’s abolitionist community, colonization debates, and Underground Railroad activity.
  • Internet Archive — The Underground Railroad in Connecticut (1962) Full text of Horatio Strother’s comprehensive study of Connecticut’s Underground Railroad routes and safe houses.
  • Connecticut State Library — Research Guide on Slavery in Connecticut PDF guide to manuscript collections, court records, and legislative documents related to slavery and abolition in Connecticut.

Literary Heritage: Twain & Stowe in Hartford

  • Mark Twain House & Museum Digital resources from the Hartford home where Twain lived 1874–1891, writing Adventures of Huckleberry Finn, The Adventures of Tom Sawyer, and A Connecticut Yankee in King Arthur’s Court. Over 50,000 items in the collection.
  • Harriet Beecher Stowe Center — Collections 228,000 items including first editions of Stowe’s works, Beecher-Stowe family correspondence, diaries, journals, and the Uncle Tom’s Cabin collection of American and foreign editions.
  • Stowe Center — Harriet Beecher Stowe Papers Finding Aid Detailed finding aid to manuscript collections including personal correspondence, literary manuscripts, and family papers.

Maritime History

  • Mystic Seaport Museum — Research Collections Connecticut Ship Database (1789–1939), American Offshore Whaling Voyages data, over one million photographs (including the Rosenfeld Collection), ship plans, oral histories, and maritime manuscripts.
  • Connecticut Digital Archive — Mystic Seaport Materials Digitized photographs, documents, and materials from Mystic Seaport’s collections accessible through the statewide digital archive.

Twentieth Century

  • Connecticut Digital Archive (CTDA) Statewide digital repository with WPA Census of Old Buildings (1934–1937) survey forms and photographs, Federal Art Project Depression-era artwork database, 20th-century newspapers, and historical telephone directories.
  • University of Connecticut — Oral History Center Transcripts and recordings from UConn oral history interviews (established 1968) documenting 20th-century Connecticut life and communities.
  • Ellis Island Foundation — Passenger Search Nearly 65 million passenger records (1820–1957) from ships arriving in New York, including immigrants bound for Connecticut’s industrial cities.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Connecticut State Archives Finding aids and digitized materials from the state’s public records, government manuscripts, and historical collections.
  • Connecticut’s Archives Online (CAO) Aggregated finding aids to archival resources across Connecticut institutions, hosted by Western Connecticut State University.
  • Connecticut Historical Society — Digital Reproductions Thousands of digitized images, documents, sound recordings, oral histories, maps, paintings, and artifacts from the 17th through 21st centuries.
  • Yale Beinecke Library — Digital Collections Over one million digitized images including the Jonathan Edwards Collection (60,000+ pages of sermons, notebooks, and correspondence) and early American manuscripts.
  • Yale Collections Discovery (LUX) Unified search across Yale University’s millions of library, archive, and museum items, including extensive Connecticut-related holdings.
  • ConnecticutHistory.org (Connecticut Humanities) Educational resource with articles, primary source links, and biographical profiles spanning all periods of Connecticut history.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Connecticut Newspapers (Library of Congress) Over 50 digitized Connecticut newspaper titles, fully searchable, spanning through 1963.
  • Connecticut State Library — Freely Available Newspapers Curated guide to freely accessible Connecticut newspapers online, including titles beyond Chronicling America.
  • Library of Congress — Connecticut Digital Collections Guide Comprehensive guide to Connecticut materials across LOC holdings, including maps, photographs, manuscripts, and sound recordings.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Connecticut Aggregated search across Connecticut libraries, museums, and archives contributing to DPLA.
