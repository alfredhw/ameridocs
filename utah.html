Native Peoples: Ute, Paiute, Goshute, Shoshone & Navajo

  • Utah American Indian Digital Archive Gateway to resources on Northwestern Shoshone, Goshute, Paiute, Utah Navajo, White Mesa Ute, and Northern Ute tribes, including articles, oral histories, photographs, maps, and government documents.
  • University of Utah — J. Willard Marriott Digital Library Over 2 million digitized items including photographs, newspapers, maps, and historical documents from 500+ collections focused on Utah and Western history, with materials on Native peoples.

Spanish Exploration & the Old Spanish Trail

  • Archives West — Old Spanish Trail Collection Correspondence and articles (1914–1987) concerning the location and documentation of the Old Spanish Trail through Utah and Arizona.
  • Intermountain Histories (BYU) Curated histories of Intermountain West sites with primary sources, photographs, and GPS-enabled maps covering Spanish exploration and early trails through Utah.

Mormon Pioneers & Settlement (1847)

  • Church History Library — Online Sources Primary archive of Church records including materials on 61,000+ pioneers (1847–1868), 44,000+ missionaries (1830–1940), Mormon Battalion records, and Brigham Young correspondence.
  • BYU — L. Tom Perry Special Collections 11,000+ manuscript collections including nineteenth- and twentieth-century Mormon and Western manuscripts, pioneer diaries, photographs, and correspondence from key figures in Utah settlement.
  • BYU Harold B. Lee Library — Digital Collections Over 100 digital collections with Utah materials including historical photographs (1878–1928), newspapers, Brigham Young correspondence, and pioneer documentation.

Utah War (1857–1858)

  • History to Go — Utah War Historical overview with primary source documents on the 1857–1858 confrontation between the federal government and the Latter-day Saints in Utah Territory.
  • BYU Special Collections — Utah Expedition Records Documents, correspondence, and records related to the Utah War, including military reports and official communications.

Mountain Meadows Massacre (1857)

  • Mountain Meadows Massacre Collection Andrew Jenson and David H. Morris collections with digitized original documents (images and transcriptions) including letters, confessions, and trial records related to the 1857 massacre.
  • Utah Division of Archives — John D. Lee Case Files Trial case files (1875–1876) from the Mountain Meadows Massacre proceedings, digitized and freely accessible online.
  • Library of Congress — Mountain Meadows Massacre (1877) Digitized 1877 publication documenting the massacre, trials, and execution of John D. Lee, with illustrations.

Transcontinental Railroad: The Golden Spike (1869)

  • Library of Congress — Golden Spike Topic Guide Chronicling America guide to newspaper coverage of the Golden Spike completion at Promontory, Utah (May 10, 1869), with links to primary source newspaper articles.
  • University of Utah — Golden Spike Exhibit Digital exhibit on transcontinental railroad history with primary source images and historical context on the joining of the Central Pacific and Union Pacific railroads.
  • University of Utah — Golden Spike Ceremony Photographs Digitized photographs of the Golden Spike ceremony at Promontory Summit, Utah on May 10, 1869.

Mining: Park City & Bingham Canyon

  • Utah Division of Archives — Bingham Canyon Photographs Historic photographs of Bingham Canyon mining operations, the community, buildings, and people, fully digitized and freely available.
  • University of Utah — Mining the West Exhibit Digital exhibit on Utah mining history including Park City silver mining and the Bingham Canyon copper mine, with primary source materials.

Japanese American Incarceration: Topaz

  • Utah State University — Topaz Digital Collection Digitized yearbooks, literary magazines, school publications, and camp materials from the Topaz War Relocation Center (1942–1945), including the Topaz Times newspaper and Trek literary magazine.
  • Densho Digital Repository Oral histories, photographs, and documents on Japanese American incarceration, including materials from the Topaz camp in south-central Utah.
  • Topaz Museum — Digital Resources Digital resources from the Topaz Museum in Delta, Utah, including research materials and museum collections on camp life.

Nuclear Testing & Downwinders

  • University of Utah — Downwinders of Utah Archive Interactive geospatial timeline with fallout maps, statistical data, government documentation, and 40+ oral history interviews documenting the effects of Nevada Test Site fallout on Utah communities.
  • History to Go — Downwinders Educational resources and primary sources on nuclear testing and its effects on Utah’s downwind communities.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Utah Division of Archives — Digital Archives Official repository of Utah governmental records dating from 1850, including curated primary source sets on major Utah historical events.
  • University of Utah — J. Willard Marriott Digital Library Over 2 million digitized items from 500+ collections covering Utah and Western history.
  • Utah Digital Newspapers Over 9 million freely searchable pages from 400 Utah newspapers published 1850–2023, organized by county and title.
  • Utah State University — Special Collections Rare books, manuscripts, and papers including Northwestern Shoshone oral history interviews and Intermountain Indian School materials.
  • Mountain West Digital Library Free portal providing access to 1+ million digitized items from 80+ institutions in Utah, Nevada, Oregon, Idaho, and Hawaii.
