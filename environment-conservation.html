The Conservation Movement & National Parks

  • Theodore Roosevelt Center — Digital Library (Dickinson State University) Over 100,000 digitized items including correspondence, speeches, photographs, and manuscripts documenting Roosevelt’s conservation legacy: the creation of 5 national parks, 18 national monuments, 150 national forests, and 51 federal bird reservations (1858–1919).
  • Library of Congress — John Muir Papers Correspondence, journals, and manuscripts of the naturalist and Sierra Club founder, including letters to Theodore Roosevelt and writings that helped establish Yosemite and Sequoia National Parks (1860s–1914).
  • University of the Pacific — John Muir Digital Collections The primary repository of Muir’s personal papers: over 20,000 items including journals, correspondence, drawings, photographs, and unpublished manuscripts documenting his explorations of the Sierra Nevada, Alaska, and the American West.
  • National Park Service — Antiquities Act of 1906 Full text, legislative history, and documentation of the landmark preservation law that gave presidents authority to designate national monuments, with records of all monument proclamations.
  • Library of Congress — Theodore Roosevelt Papers Approximately 276,000 digitized items including presidential correspondence on forest reserves, reclamation policy, and the Newlands Act, plus exchanges with Gifford Pinchot and John Muir (1759–1920).
  • Sierra Club — Library & Archives Historical records of the nation’s oldest grassroots environmental organization (founded 1892), including the Sierra Club Bulletin, expedition records, and conservation campaign documents.

Environmental Protection & Regulation

  • U.S. Environmental Protection Agency — EPA History Official archive of EPA’s institutional history from its creation in 1970 to the present, including administrator speeches, policy documents, annual reports, oral histories, and milestone legislation timelines.
  • National Archives — Records of the Environmental Protection Agency (RG 412) Finding aid to the official federal records of the EPA including regulatory proceedings, enforcement actions, Superfund site documentation, and interagency correspondence from 1970 forward.
  • Rachel Carson Council — Digital Archive Resources related to the life and legacy of Rachel Carson, including biographical materials, photographs, and documentation of the impact of Silent Spring (1962) on environmental policy.
  • Library of Congress — Rachel Carson Papers (Finding Aid) Guide to the Carson papers held at the Beinecke Library (Yale), with biographical information, correspondence listings, and research notes related to Silent Spring and her marine biology writings.
  • University at Buffalo — Love Canal Collections Digitized primary sources on the Love Canal environmental disaster (1978): government reports, community activism records, health studies, newspaper clippings, photographs, and EPA documentation that led to the creation of the Superfund program.
  • EPA — History of the Clean Water Act Legislative history, original text, and key amendments of the Federal Water Pollution Control Act (1972) and Clean Water Act, with regulatory milestones and enforcement data.
  • EPA — Evolution of the Clean Air Act Documentary history of air pollution regulation from the Air Pollution Control Act (1955) through the Clean Air Act (1963, 1970, 1990), with legislative texts, amendment histories, and implementation records.

Public Lands & the American Landscape

  • Bureau of Land Management — General Land Office Records Over 12 million federal land title records (1788–present) including homestead patents, cash entries, military bounty land warrants, and original survey plats—the definitive record of American public land disposition.
  • U.S. Geological Survey — National Geologic Map Database Historic and current geologic maps of the United States, including survey reports from the great western surveys of the 1870s (Hayden, King, Powell, and Wheeler) that shaped understanding of the American landscape.
  • National Archives — Records of the Forest Service (RG 95) Finding aid to U.S. Forest Service records including Gifford Pinchot’s correspondence, national forest establishment documents, timber management plans, and fire suppression records from 1891 forward.
  • Forest History Society — U.S. Forest Service History Collection Photographs, oral histories, and documents tracing the Forest Service from its founding (1905) through major policy shifts in wilderness preservation, multiple-use management, and wildfire policy.
  • National Archives — Records of the Civilian Conservation Corps (RG 35) Official records of the CCC (1933–1942) including enrollment records, camp newspapers, photographs, project reports, and correspondence documenting the New Deal program that employed 3 million young men in conservation work.
  • National Park Service — Civilian Conservation Corps Legacy Photographs, camp histories, and project documentation from CCC work in national parks, including trail construction, lodge building, erosion control, and landscape restoration projects across the park system.
  • Library of Congress — John Wesley Powell Survey Photographs Photographs from Powell’s surveys of the Colorado River and the American West (1871–1879), documenting the landscapes, geology, and Indigenous peoples of the Colorado Plateau before major development.

Environmental Photography & Art

  • Library of Congress — Ansel Adams Photographs of Japanese American Internment at Manzanar Over 200 photographs by Adams at Manzanar (1943), with additional landscape work; part of the broader Adams collections documenting the American West and national park landscapes.
  • National Archives — Ansel Adams Photographs Over 200 photographs taken by Adams for the Department of the Interior (1941–1942) documenting national parks and monuments including Yellowstone, Grand Canyon, Grand Teton, and Glacier—in the public domain.
  • Library of Congress — FSA/OWI Collection Over 175,000 black-and-white film negatives and prints (1935–1945) from the Farm Security Administration and Office of War Information, documenting Dust Bowl devastation, soil erosion, rural landscapes, and New Deal conservation programs. Photographers include Dorothea Lange, Walker Evans, Arthur Rothstein, and Russell Lee.
  • Library of Congress — FSA/OWI Color Photographs Approximately 1,600 color photographs (1939–1944) documenting American landscapes, agriculture, and rural life during the transition from Depression to wartime—among the earliest color documentary photographs.
  • U.S. Geological Survey — Photographic Library Over 400,000 photographs documenting American geology, landscapes, and natural hazards from the 1870s to the present, including images from the Hayden, King, Powell, and Wheeler surveys of the American West.
  • Smithsonian American Art Museum — American Landscape Art Digital collection of American landscape paintings and photographs including works by the Hudson River School, Albert Bierstadt, Thomas Moran, and other artists who shaped public perception of the American wilderness.
  • Library of Congress — Historic American Landscapes Survey (HALS) Measured drawings, photographs, and written histories documenting significant American landscapes, gardens, and designed outdoor spaces as part of the broader HABS/HAER/HALS program.

Climate & Weather Records

  • NOAA — National Centers for Environmental Information (NCEI) The world’s largest archive of weather and climate data, with historical records dating to the early nineteenth century: temperature, precipitation, severe weather, ocean data, and paleoclimatology spanning millions of years.
  • NOAA — U.S. Climate Normals 30-year climate averages for thousands of weather stations across the United States, with historical normals data sets enabling comparison across decades back to 1901.
  • NOAA — Photo Library Over 40,000 historical and contemporary photographs documenting weather events, climate phenomena, marine environments, and the history of American weather observation from the Signal Corps era to the present.
  • National Archives — Records of the Weather Bureau (RG 27) Finding aid to the historical records of the U.S. Weather Bureau (1870–1970), including station observation logs, storm reports, weather maps, and administrative records from the nation’s weather observation network.
  • NOAA — Climate at a Glance Interactive tool providing historical temperature and precipitation time series for the United States from 1895 to the present, at national, state, regional, and divisional scales.
  • NOAA — World Data Service for Paleoclimatology Archive of paleoclimate data including tree rings, ice cores, cave deposits, corals, and lake sediments, with records spanning hundreds of thousands of years used to reconstruct past climate conditions.

Environmental Justice

  • EPA — EJScreen: Environmental Justice Screening Tool Interactive mapping tool combining environmental and demographic data to identify communities with disproportionate environmental burdens, with data on air quality, hazardous waste proximity, lead paint exposure, and socioeconomic indicators at the census block group level.
  • EPA — Environmental Justice Program Historical records of the federal environmental justice movement from Executive Order 12898 (1994) forward, including program histories, community engagement documents, and interagency working group records.
  • National Archives — Toxic Waste and Environmental Records Research guide to federal records on toxic contamination, Superfund sites, and environmental health investigations, including records from the EPA, Army Corps of Engineers, and Department of Energy.
  • Cornell University — National Crop Residue Processing (Warren County PCB Landfill Documentation) Documentation of the Warren County, North Carolina PCB landfill protests (1982), widely recognized as the catalyst for the modern environmental justice movement, including photographs, oral histories, and community records.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Environmental Justice Curated primary source set with photographs, documents, maps, and media exploring the intersection of environmental degradation and racial and economic inequality from the twentieth century forward.

Water & River Systems

  • Bureau of Reclamation — History Program Historical records of the Bureau of Reclamation from the Newlands Reclamation Act (1902) forward, including dam construction photographs, engineering reports, project histories, and oral histories documenting major water projects across the 17 western states.
  • National Archives — Records of the Tennessee Valley Authority (RG 142) Finding aid to TVA records (1933–present) including dam construction photographs, regional planning documents, maps, flood control studies, and rural electrification records from the New Deal’s most ambitious regional development program.
  • National Archives — Records of the Office of the Chief of Engineers (RG 77) Finding aid to Army Corps of Engineers records including navigation improvement plans, flood control project documents, survey maps, and engineering reports for major American river systems from the early nineteenth century forward.
  • Bureau of Reclamation — Colorado River Documents Historical documents related to the Colorado River Compact (1922), Boulder Canyon Project Act (1928), and the complex legal and engineering history of the most contested river system in the American West.
  • U.S. Geological Survey — National Water Information System Comprehensive database of water resources data: streamflow measurements, groundwater levels, and water-quality data from over 1.5 million sites across the United States, with some records extending back over a century.
  • Library of Congress — Panoramic Photographs (Dam & Waterway Views) Within this collection of over 4,000 panoramic photographs (1851–1991) are images of major dam construction projects, river systems, irrigation works, and waterway engineering across the United States.

Wildlife & Species Conservation

  • U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service — National Digital Library Historical photographs, reports, and publications from the Fish & Wildlife Service documenting wildlife management, refuge establishment, species surveys, and conservation programs from the early twentieth century forward.
  • U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service — National Digital Library (Media) Tens of thousands of photographs, videos, and audio recordings of American wildlife, habitats, and conservation activities across the National Wildlife Refuge System.
  • National Archives — Records of the Fish and Wildlife Service (RG 22) Finding aid to federal wildlife records including Bureau of Biological Survey records, predator control correspondence, wildlife refuge establishment documents, and endangered species files from the 1880s forward.
  • Biodiversity Heritage Library Over 60 million pages of digitized biodiversity literature from the Smithsonian and partner institutions, including historic natural history publications, species descriptions, expedition reports, and scientific journals from the eighteenth century to the present.
  • National Audubon Society — Birds of America High-resolution digital reproductions of John James Audubon’s 435 hand-colored plates from The Birds of America (1827–1838), the landmark work of American natural history illustration.
  • Smithsonian National Museum of Natural History — Collections Search Searchable database of specimens, photographs, and field notes from the nation’s largest natural history collection, with over 146 million specimens including extensive American wildlife holdings.

Nuclear Testing & Environmental Impact

  • Department of Energy — OpenNet (Declassified Documents) Database of over 500,000 declassified Department of Energy documents including nuclear weapons testing reports, environmental monitoring data, radiation exposure studies, and contamination assessments from the Manhattan Project era to the present.
  • Atomic Heritage Foundation — Oral Histories & Document Collections Over 600 oral history interviews with Manhattan Project veterans, nuclear test participants, and community members, plus documents, photographs, and multimedia resources on the history and environmental legacy of nuclear weapons development.
  • National Archives — Records of the Atomic Energy Commission (RG 326) Finding aid to AEC records (1946–1975) including nuclear test documentation, environmental monitoring reports, fallout studies, and records of the weapons production complex at sites including Hanford, Oak Ridge, and Savannah River.
  • University of Nevada, Las Vegas — Nuclear Testing Archive Digitized documents, photographs, films, and oral histories from the Nevada Test Site, including over 12,000 declassified documents on atmospheric and underground nuclear tests and their environmental and health effects.
  • Hanford Site — Historical Documents Records of the Hanford nuclear reservation in Washington State, including declassified production records, environmental contamination data, and cleanup documentation for the most contaminated nuclear site in the Western Hemisphere.
  • Library of Congress — Atomic Testing Photography Photographs of American nuclear tests from the Prints and Photographs Division, documenting atmospheric testing in Nevada and the Pacific from the 1940s through the 1960s.

Reference & Research Collections

  • Library of Congress — Environmental History Research Guide Comprehensive research guide to environmental history primary sources at the Library of Congress, including manuscripts, maps, photographs, government documents, and finding aids across dozens of collections.
  • National Archives — Environmental Records Research Guide Central finding aid for federal environmental records across all agencies, including the EPA, Interior Department, Agriculture Department, Army Corps of Engineers, and Department of Energy.
  • Digital Public Library of America (DPLA) Aggregated access to over 45 million items from libraries, archives, and museums nationwide; searching for environmental, conservation, and natural history topics yields primary sources from hundreds of institutions.
  • Smithsonian Institution — Collections Search (Environment) Cross-institutional search of Smithsonian collections spanning the National Museum of Natural History, National Museum of American History, Archives of American Art, and other units, with millions of objects related to natural and environmental history.
  • HathiTrust Digital Library Over 17 million digitized volumes from major research libraries, including full runs of government publications from the USGS, Forest Service, Bureau of Reclamation, and other agencies central to American environmental history. Public-domain works are freely accessible.
  • National Archives Catalog Search portal for the complete holdings of the National Archives, including record groups for the EPA (RG 412), National Park Service (RG 79), Forest Service (RG 95), Fish and Wildlife Service (RG 22), Bureau of Reclamation (RG 115), and other environmental agencies.
