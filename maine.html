Wabanaki Peoples

  • Abbe Museum — Online Collections Searchable database of 70,000+ items including contemporary and historic Wabanaki art, archaeological artifacts, ash-splint baskets, and birchbark containers spanning 12,000 years of Penobscot, Passamaquoddy, Maliseet, and Micmac culture.
  • University of Maine Hudson Museum — Online Collections Searchable database of Wabanaki artifacts and materials from Maine’s four federally recognized tribes, including anthropological collections and photographs.
  • University of Maine — Wabanaki Studies Guide Research guide curating primary and secondary sources on Wabanaki peoples, history, and culture.

Colonial Period: French & English Settlement

  • Maine Memory Network — Contact & Conflict (1500–1667) Primary source materials on early European contact with Wabanaki peoples, French settlement at St. Croix Island, and English colonial conflicts.
  • Internet Archive — York Deeds (1642–1737) Complete digitized records of York County deeds covering early colonial property transactions and settlements in Maine.
  • Internet Archive — Documentary History of Maine Digitized historical documents including Trelawny Papers, Baxter manuscripts, and colonial discovery records.

Maritime History: Shipbuilding, Fishing & Lighthouses

  • Maine Memory Network — Trade & Transport Curated collections on Maine’s fishing trade, merchant shipping, and maritime commerce from the colonial era through the 19th century.
  • Maine Maritime Museum Bath, Maine museum with 20,000+ objects and millions of rare documents and manuscripts on Maine maritime heritage, including shipbuilding records.

Statehood & the Missouri Compromise (1820)

  • Maine Memory Network — Bicentennial: Road to Statehood Comprehensive project documenting Maine’s 1820 statehood, including William King’s proclamation and legislative documents tied to the Missouri Compromise.
  • Library of Congress — Missouri Compromise Research Guide Congressional debates (Annals of Congress), presidential papers, and primary documents on the compromise that admitted Maine as a free state.
  • National Archives — Missouri Compromise Full text of the legislation with historical context on the simultaneous admission of Maine and Missouri.

Abolition & the Underground Railroad

  • Maine Historical Society — Portland Freedom Trail Guide to Portland’s freedom trail documenting antislavery activism, including William Lloyd Garrison’s 1832 speech and pro-slavery riots of 1836 and 1847.
  • University of Southern Maine — The Underground Railroad in New England Digitized primary source document on Underground Railroad networks in New England, including Maine routes and safe houses.

Lumber Industry & the Forest Economy

  • Maine Memory Network — Living Off the Land & Sea Primary sources on Maine’s lumber industry, logging practices, and the forest economy that shaped the state’s interior.
  • University of Maine — Maine Folklife Center Center documenting Maine’s traditional culture with 160+ collections, 3,000+ accessions, and 10,000+ photographs, including logging and lumbering oral histories.

Immigration: French-Canadian & Irish Communities

  • Franco Center (Lewiston) — Immigration History Resources on French-Canadian immigration to Maine mill towns, documenting the experience of Québécois workers in Lewiston and other industrial communities.
  • Maine MILL — Immigration & Labor Legacy Educational resources on immigration history and immigrant worker experiences in Maine textile and shoe mills.

Civil War & Joshua Chamberlain

  • Digital Maine — Chamberlain Correspondence 20th Maine Regiment Adjutant General correspondence, including Joshua Chamberlain’s Gettysburg battle report (July 6, 1863) and related military documents.
  • Maine State Library — Maine in the Civil War Guide to Maine Civil War records including 20th Maine Regiment materials and other unit histories.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • DigitalMaine Repository Partnership of the Maine State Library, State Archives, and partner institutions providing access to digitized historical materials including military records, naturalization records, court records, and Wabanaki papers (1793–1880s).
  • Maine Memory Network (Maine Historical Society) Collaborative digital museum with 70,000+ historical items, stories, and exhibits from 270+ organizations across Maine.
  • Bowdoin College — Maine History Research Guide Research guide curating primary sources on Maine history, including Oliver Otis Howard papers and institutional archives.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Maine Newspapers (Library of Congress) Digitized Maine newspaper titles from the 1700s through the early 1900s, fully searchable.
  • DigitalMaine — Map Collections Over 2,000 maps spanning the 17th–21st centuries from the Maine Cultural Building collections.
  • University of Maine — Sanborn Fire Insurance Maps Fire insurance maps of Maine communities through 1923, searchable and viewable online.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Maine Aggregated search across Maine cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
