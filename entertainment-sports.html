Recorded Sound & Music

  • Library of Congress — National Jukebox 10,000+ acoustically recorded discs (1901–1925) from the Victor Talking Machine Company and other labels: popular songs, opera, jazz, vaudeville, spoken word, and ethnic recordings from the dawn of the recording industry.
  • Library of Congress — Recorded Sound Collections Gateway to 3.5 million sound recordings: music, spoken word, radio broadcasts, and oral histories spanning the full range of American audio heritage.
  • Library of Congress — Alan Lomax Collection 17,400 sound recordings plus photographs, manuscripts, and field notes from the folklorist who documented blues, folk, gospel, Cajun, and work songs across the American South and beyond.
  • Rock & Roll Hall of Fame — Library & Archives Digital materials from one of the nation’s foremost music archives: artist correspondence, photographs, posters, handbills, and oral histories documenting the history of rock and roll and its roots in American popular music.
  • UC Santa Barbara — Discography of American Historical Recordings Database of master recordings made in the United States from 1900 to 1970, with detailed discographic information on thousands of labels, artists, and sessions.

Vaudeville, Theater & Performance

  • Library of Congress — American Variety Stage: Vaudeville and Popular Entertainment Playbills, programs, photographs, and sheet music from 1870–1920 documenting vaudeville, burlesque, minstrelsy, circus, Wild West shows, and variety theater—the dominant entertainment forms of Victorian America.
  • American Vaudeville Museum 4,321+ digitized items: photographs, sheet music, correspondence, and performance ephemera preserving the history of vaudeville as America’s most popular form of mass entertainment (1880s–1930s).
  • Library of Congress — Federal Theatre Project (1935–1939) Scripts, production bulletins, photographs, costume and set designs, and administrative records from the WPA’s ambitious New Deal arts program that employed 12,700 theater workers and produced 1,200 plays.
  • Hagley Museum — Playbills & Entertainment Ephemera Theater playbills, circus posters, and entertainment advertisements from the Hagley Library’s collections documenting popular amusements in the nineteenth and early twentieth centuries.

Chautauqua & Lyceum

  • Cleveland Public Library — Chautauqua Digital Archive Rare materials on the Chautauqua movement: programs, photographs, and publications from the traveling educational and entertainment circuits that brought lectures, music, and culture to rural America (1870s–1930s).
  • University of Iowa — Redpath Chautauqua Collection Records of the Redpath Lyceum Bureau (1868–1940): correspondence, promotional materials, performer contracts, and circuit routes documenting the business of traveling entertainment and popular education.

Early Film & Motion Pictures

  • Library of Congress — Edison Company Motion Pictures 341 motion pictures produced by Thomas Edison’s company (1891–1918): actualities, dramas, comedies, and newsreels from the birth of American cinema, including some of the earliest surviving American films.
  • Library of Congress — Early Motion Pictures (1897–1920) Pioneering American films including the first copyrighted motion picture and early works by the American Mutoscope & Biograph Company—documenting everyday life, travel, news events, and early narrative cinema.
  • UCLA Film & Television Archive One of the world’s largest university-held film archives: 400,000+ film and television holdings with selected materials digitized, including newsreels, independent films, and rare Hollywood productions.
  • Library of Congress — National Film Registry Essays and contextual information on 875+ culturally significant American films selected for preservation, with links to freely accessible titles in the Library’s collections.

Radio & Television

  • Internet Archive — Old Time Radio Collection Thousands of Golden Age radio broadcasts (1930s–1960s): dramas, comedies, mysteries, news programs, and variety shows from the era when radio was America’s dominant mass medium.
  • American Archive of Public Broadcasting 100,000+ digitized public radio and television programs (1940s–present) preserved by WGBH Boston and the Library of Congress: documentaries, news, cultural programming, and local public broadcasting.
  • Paley Center for Media — Collection 160,000 television and radio programs from the 1920s to the present: catalog searchable online, with selected materials available for on-site research at New York and Los Angeles facilities.

Baseball

  • National Baseball Hall of Fame — PASTIME Digital Archive Digitized photographs, documents, and artifacts from Cooperstown: player files, World Series programs, correspondence, contracts, and ephemera spanning the history of America’s national pastime.
  • Library of Congress — Jackie Robinson and Other Baseball Highlights 2,100 baseball cards (1887–1914) plus photographs and manuscripts documenting baseball’s early professional era and the story of integration.
  • Negro Leagues Baseball Museum — Digital Archive Photographs, documents, and multimedia resources on the history of African American baseball from the 1860s through integration: the Negro National League, Negro American League, barnstorming teams, and the players who made history.

Sports & Athletics

  • LA84 Foundation — Sports Library 250,000+ items constituting the largest free-access sports research library in North America: digitized books, journals, photographs, and Olympic materials including complete coverage of every modern Olympic Games.
  • University of Louisville — Muhammad Ali Archives Photographs, scrapbooks, correspondence, and personal papers of Muhammad Ali documenting his career, his stand against the Vietnam War draft, and his impact on American sports, race, and culture.
  • Pro Football Hall of Fame — Archives Research materials on professional football history: photographs, game programs, correspondence, and organizational records from the NFL’s founding to the present.

Hip Hop & Popular Culture

  • Cornell University — Hip Hop Collection 250,000+ items constituting the largest institutional hip hop archive: photographs, flyers, recordings, magazines, personal papers, and business records documenting hip hop from its Bronx origins to global phenomenon.
  • Harvard University — Hiphop Archive & Research Institute Research institute preserving and studying hip hop culture: fellowships, publications, digital exhibitions, and archival collections on the art form’s intersection with race, politics, and American identity.

World’s Fairs & Expositions

  • Chicago History Museum — 1893 World’s Columbian Exposition Photographs, souvenirs, maps, and ephemera from the 1893 fair that showcased American industrial and cultural ambition—the White City, the Midway Plaisance, and the birth of American consumer spectacle.
  • DPLA — 1939 New York World’s Fair Primary sources on the “World of Tomorrow” fair: photographs, promotional materials, and documents from the exposition that imagined a technological American future on the eve of World War II.
  • Library of Congress — World’s Fairs Collections Maps, photographs, prints, architectural drawings, and ephemera from American and international expositions, with substantial holdings on fairs from 1876 (Philadelphia Centennial) through the twentieth century.

Circus, Amusements & Popular Spectacle

  • Circus Historical Society — Archives Research materials on American circus history from the eighteenth century to the present: route books, programs, photographs, and business records of traveling shows.
  • Bridgeport History Center — Barnum Museum Digital Collections Digitized materials from the P. T. Barnum collection: circus posters, photographs, correspondence, and ephemera documenting America’s greatest showman and the culture of nineteenth-century popular entertainment.
  • Library of Congress — Historic American Sheet Music 3,000+ pieces of sheet music (1850–1920) with illustrated covers depicting popular entertainment, social life, and cultural attitudes—a visual and musical record of American popular culture.
