The Space Program & NASA

  • NASA — History Office Central portal for NASA’s historical resources: mission histories, technical reports, oral histories, photographs, and primary documents covering Mercury, Gemini, Apollo, Skylab, the Space Shuttle, and the International Space Station.
  • NASA — Apollo Lunar Surface Journal Complete annotated transcripts of all Apollo lunar surface operations (Apollo 11–17), with photographs, audio clips, video, mission timelines, and commentary—the definitive record of human activity on the Moon.
  • NASA Johnson Space Center — History Collection Digitized documents, photographs, and oral histories from the Johnson Space Center, including mission control records, astronaut papers, and technical manuals from the human spaceflight program.
  • NASA Image and Video Library Over 140,000 photographs, videos, and audio recordings from across NASA’s history: rocket launches, spacewalks, planetary exploration, and Earth observation, all freely available in the public domain.
  • NASA Technical Reports Server (NTRS) Over 1 million citations with full-text access to NASA technical publications, research reports, and scientific papers from the agency’s founding (1958) to the present.

Computing & the Digital Revolution

  • Computer History Museum — Collections Over 100,000 artifacts and 4,000 linear feet of archival materials documenting the history of computing, with digitized oral histories, photographs, software, hardware, and documents from the 1940s to the present.
  • Computer History Museum — Digital Archive Digitized documents, manuals, photographs, and promotional materials from pioneering computing companies and engineers, including materials on the ENIAC, UNIVAC, and the development of the personal computer.
  • National Science Foundation — The Internet: A History Documentary history of NSF’s role in developing the internet, from ARPANET (1969) through NSFNET to the commercial internet of the 1990s, with reports, photographs, and network maps.
  • Library of Congress — Copyright Office Software Collection Historical software deposits at the Copyright Office documenting the development of American software from the 1960s forward.

Medical Breakthroughs & Public Health

  • National Library of Medicine — Profiles in Science Digitized papers of pioneering biomedical researchers: Jonas Salk and the polio vaccine, Francis Collins and the Human Genome Project, Florence Sabin, C. Everett Koop, Joshua Lederberg, and others who shaped American medicine.
  • National Library of Medicine — History of Medicine Division One of the world’s largest medical history collections: 600,000+ prints, photographs, manuscripts, and rare books documenting American medicine from the colonial era to the present, with extensive digital exhibitions.
  • CDC — Stacks (Public Health Publications) Digital archive of Centers for Disease Control and Prevention publications (1946–present): the MMWR, surveillance reports, epidemic investigation records, and public health guidance spanning polio, HIV/AIDS, influenza, and other threats.
  • National Archives at Atlanta — Tuskegee Syphilis Study Records Federal records of the U.S. Public Health Service study (1932–1972): correspondence, medical records, photographs, and administrative documents. (See also African American History.)
  • National Library of Medicine — Visual Culture and Health Digital exhibitions on the visual history of American medicine: posters, advertisements, photographs, and public health campaigns from the nineteenth century to the present.

The Atomic Age & Nuclear Science

  • Department of Energy — Manhattan Project Historical Resources Declassified documents, the 36-volume Manhattan District History, and primary sources from the development of the atomic bomb at Los Alamos, Oak Ridge, and Hanford. (See also Military & Wartime.)
  • Department of Energy — OpenNet (Declassified Documents) Over 500,000 declassified documents on nuclear weapons testing, reactor development, radiation research, and arms control from the Manhattan Project era to the present. (See also Environment & Conservation.)
  • Atomic Heritage Foundation — Oral Histories & Documents Over 600 oral history interviews with Manhattan Project veterans, nuclear scientists, and community members, plus documents, photographs, and multimedia resources.
  • University of Nevada, Las Vegas — Nuclear Testing Archive Over 12,000 declassified documents, photographs, films, and oral histories from the Nevada Test Site documenting atmospheric and underground nuclear tests and their effects.
  • Alsos Digital Library for Nuclear Issues (Washington & Lee University) Curated collection of annotated resources on nuclear science, technology, and policy, including primary documents on the development and consequences of nuclear weapons and energy.

Invention & Innovation

  • National Archives — U.S. Patent Records Federal patent records from the earliest patents (1790) forward, including drawings, specifications, and correspondence documenting American innovation from Eli Whitney and Samuel Morse to modern technology.
  • Smithsonian Lemelson Center for the Study of Invention and Innovation Collections and exhibitions on American invention at the National Museum of American History: archives of inventors and innovators, patent models, and educational resources on the innovation process.
  • Rutgers University — Thomas A. Edison Papers Over 5 million pages of Edison’s documents digitized and annotated: laboratory notebooks, correspondence, legal records, and business papers documenting the phonograph, electric light, motion pictures, and other inventions (1847–1931).
  • Library of Congress — Alexander Graham Bell Papers Approximately 4,700 items (1862–1939): correspondence, scientific notebooks, journals, blueprints, and photographs documenting Bell’s invention of the telephone and work on hearing, flight, and communications technology.
  • Library of Congress — Wilbur and Orville Wright Papers Approximately 8,000 items (1809–1979): correspondence, diaries, notebooks, scrapbooks, and photographs documenting the development of powered flight, including drawings and technical records of the 1903 Flyer.

Agricultural Science & Land-Grant Universities

  • USDA — National Agricultural Library Digital Collections Digitized USDA publications, research bulletins, and extension materials from the 1860s to the present: crop reports, nutritional studies, food safety regulations, and agricultural experiment station records. (See also American Foodways.)
  • Cornell University — USDA Census of Agriculture Historical Census of Agriculture reports (1840–2017) documenting the transformation of American farming: crop production, livestock, farm size, land use, and the industrialization of the food system. (See also Economic History.)
  • USDA National Agricultural Library — George Washington Carver Papers, photographs, and publications of George Washington Carver documenting his research on peanuts, sweet potatoes, and Southern agriculture at Tuskegee Institute. (See also African American History.)
  • National Archives — Morrill Act (1862) The landmark legislation that created the land-grant university system, with full text and historical context for the law that democratized American higher education and established agricultural research institutions.

Telecommunications & Broadcasting

  • American Archive of Public Broadcasting (LOC & WGBH) Over 120,000 digitized radio and television programs from the 1940s to the present, preserving the history of American public media with full-text search across transcripts.
  • Federal Communications Commission — Historical Documents Regulatory records, annual reports, and policy documents from the FCC (established 1934), covering the licensing and regulation of radio, television, telephone, and digital communications.
  • Library of Congress — Early Radio Recordings Sound recordings from the dawn of American broadcasting (1920s–1930s), including commercial programs, news broadcasts, and experimental transmissions.
  • Engineering and Technology History Wiki (IEEE) Collaborative wiki with primary source materials, oral histories, and technical documentation on the history of electrical, electronic, and computing technology, maintained by the IEEE History Center.

Science Policy & Government Research

  • National Science Foundation — History Institutional history of the NSF from its founding (1950) to the present, including Vannevar Bush’s foundational 1945 report Science—The Endless Frontier, annual reports, and policy documents shaping American science funding.
  • National Archives — Records of the Office of Scientific Research and Development (RG 227) Finding aid to OSRD records (1940–1947) documenting wartime scientific mobilization under Vannevar Bush, including research contracts, technical reports, and the organizational model for postwar federal science agencies.
  • Department of Energy — Office of Scientific and Technical Information (OSTI) Over 3 million citations and full-text technical reports from DOE-funded research, including national laboratory publications and declassified scientific documents spanning nuclear physics, energy, and materials science.
  • Library of Congress — Science Reference Guides Research guides to the Library’s science and technology collections, including materials on the history of American scientific institutions, government research agencies, and technical innovation.

Natural History & Scientific Exploration

  • Smithsonian National Museum of Natural History — Collections Over 146 million specimens and objects with a growing digital presence: type specimens, field notes, photographs, and scientific illustrations from expeditions and research programs spanning the full history of American natural science.
  • Biodiversity Heritage Library Over 60 million pages of digitized natural history literature from the Smithsonian and partner institutions, including historic American expedition reports, species descriptions, and scientific journals. (See also Environment & Conservation.)
  • Library of Congress — John Wesley Powell Survey Photographs Photographs from Powell’s surveys of the Colorado River and the American West (1871–1879), documenting geology, landscapes, and Indigenous peoples. (See also Environment & Conservation.)
  • University of Nebraska–Lincoln — Journals of the Lewis and Clark Expedition Complete annotated digital edition of the Lewis and Clark journals (1803–1806), including scientific observations on botany, zoology, geography, and ethnography from the expedition that mapped the American West.
  • Smithsonian Institution Archives Administrative records, personal papers, photographs, and oral histories documenting the Smithsonian’s role as the nation’s premier scientific research institution since 1846, including records of scientific expeditions, museum collections, and research programs.

Reference & Research Collections

  • Smithsonian Open Access Over 5.1 million digital items from 21 museums and 9 research centers, free for download and reuse, with extensive science and technology holdings across the National Museum of American History, Air and Space Museum, and Natural History Museum.
  • Smithsonian National Air and Space Museum — Collections The world’s largest collection of historic aircraft, spacecraft, and related artifacts, with a growing digital presence: photographs, documents, and technical records spanning from the Wright Flyer to the Space Shuttle.
  • HathiTrust Digital Library Over 17 million digitized volumes including full runs of scientific journals, government research publications, and technical literature from major research universities. Public-domain works freely accessible.
  • National Archives Catalog Search portal for federal records across all agencies including NASA (RG 255), the Patent Office (RG 241), the Atomic Energy Commission (RG 326), the Weather Bureau (RG 27), and dozens of science-related record groups.
