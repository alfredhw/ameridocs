Chamorro People & Culture

  • Guampedia Comprehensive digital archive covering Chamorro history, culture, language, music, and traditional governance, with genealogy resources, oral histories from elders (manaina), and educational materials spanning the Spanish colonial period through the present.
  • University of Guam — Digital Archives (MARC) Searchable digital database from the Richard F. Taitano Micronesian Area Research Center, with digitized manuscripts, photographs, the YMLG Chamoru Language Collection, and Memoirs Pasifika materials. The center holds approximately 40,000 volumes on Guam and Micronesia.

World War II: Japanese Occupation & the Battle of Guam

  • National Park Service — War in the Pacific National Historical Park Online resources on the Japanese occupation (1941–1944) and the Battle of Guam, with stories from CHamoru survivors and soldiers, and information about the T. Stell Newman Visitor Center exhibits.
  • Library of Congress — South Pacific Islands Web Archive Archived government documents, constitutions, legislation, census data, and development plans from Pacific island governments including Guam.

Archives & Reference

  • University of Guam — Guam and Micronesia Collection Research collections focused on Guam’s history and culture, including materials on indigenous knowledge systems and island studies.
  • University of Guam — Guam Cultural Repository Dedicated to preservation and repatriation of Guam’s cultural and material heritage, artifacts, and archaeological collections.
  • National Archives — Records of the Office of Territories Federal records documenting the administration and governance of Guam and other U.S. territories.
