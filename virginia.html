Pre-Colonial & Native Peoples

  • Virginia Department of Historic Resources — State Archaeology Archaeological site records and artifacts spanning over 10,000 years of Native occupation in Virginia.
  • Encyclopedia Virginia — Virginia Indians Scholarly article on Native American history in Virginia with linked primary sources.
  • Monacan Indian Nation — Our History Community history and cultural records of the Monacan Nation.

Colonial Era (1607–1776)

  • Virtual Jamestown — First-Hand Accounts Over 60 digitized letters and firsthand accounts from 1570–1720 on early colonial settlements and Bacon's Rebellion.
  • Virtual Jamestown — Full Portal Maps, images, court records, public records, and newspaper advertisements from early Virginia.
  • Virginia Gazette (Colonial Williamsburg Digital Library) Complete run of Virginia's first newspaper (1736–1780), searchable by keyword and date.
  • Virginia Records 1606–1737 (Jefferson Papers, Library of Congress) Twenty-one volumes of colonial records collected and transcribed by Thomas Jefferson.
  • Colonial Williamsburg — Rockefeller Library Collections Research library with primary sources on colonial Virginia life, governance, and material culture.
  • Early American Sources — Virginia Archives Gateway to digitized Virginia colonial records curated from multiple institutional repositories.

Revolutionary War & Early Republic

  • Founders Online (National Archives) Over 184,000 searchable documents from Washington, Jefferson, Madison, Franklin, Hamilton, Jay, and Adams.
  • Thomas Jefferson Papers (Library of Congress) Approximately 27,000 documents (1606–1827): correspondence, notes, drafts, and the Declaration of Independence.
  • George Washington's Mount Vernon — Primary Sources Educational primary source collections on Washington's life, plantation, and presidency.
  • Virginia Declaration of Rights (National Archives) George Mason's Declaration of Rights (June 1776), foundation for the Bill of Rights.
  • Document Bank of Virginia — Revolutionary Era Searchable primary documents including Virginia's 1776 Constitution and founding-era records.

Antebellum Virginia & Slavery

  • Born in Slavery: Slave Narratives (Library of Congress) Over 2,300 first-person accounts of slavery including Virginia narratives and photographs.
  • Documenting the American South — North American Slave Narratives (UNC) All known autobiographical narratives of enslaved people published through 1920.
  • University of Virginia — American Slavery Research Guide Curated guide to digital primary sources on slavery at UVA and beyond.
  • Document Bank of Virginia — Slavery & Antebellum Era Primary source documents on plantation life, slavery, and antebellum Virginia.

Civil War

  • Valley of the Shadow (University of Virginia) Thousands of documents comparing Augusta County, VA and Franklin County, PA: letters, diaries, newspapers, maps, church records, military rosters.
  • VMI Archives — Civil War Manuscripts Over 75 digitized letters, diaries, and manuscripts from soldiers of both armies, including Battle of New Market papers.
  • Virginia Tech — Civil War Diaries Digitized Civil War diary collections from Virginia Tech Special Collections.
  • Library of Virginia — Civil War Collections Robertson Civil War Sesquicentennial Legacy Collection: letters, diaries, photographs, and manuscripts.

Reconstruction through Jim Crow

  • Freedmen's Bureau Online — Virginia Records Virginia field office records (1865–1872): reports, letters, contracts, and registers.
  • FamilySearch — Virginia Freedmen's Bureau Records Digitized Virginia Freedmen's Bureau field office records (free registration required).
  • Valley of the Shadow — Reconstruction Post-Civil War Reconstruction documents from Augusta County, Virginia.
  • Virginia Museum of History & Culture — Jim Crow to Civil Rights Scholarly overview with primary sources on Virginia's Jim Crow era.

20th Century & Modern Era

  • Virginia Museum of History & Culture — Civil Rights in Virginia Collections and essays on Virginia's civil rights movement.
  • Virginia Museum — Massive Resistance Virginia's Massive Resistance policies (1956+) opposing school desegregation.
  • Library of Virginia — School Desegregation Research Guide Primary sources on Massive Resistance and school desegregation in Virginia.
  • Encyclopedia Virginia — Massive Resistance Comprehensive article with linked primary sources on Virginia's resistance to integration.

Virginia Government & Law

  • Library of Virginia Official state archives with over 130 million items on Virginia government, history, and culture.
  • Virginia Memory — Digital Collections Over 100 digital collections: photographs, maps, Virginia Chronicle newspapers, and Chancery Court records.
  • Document Bank of Virginia Primary source documents from Virginia history organized by era and theme.
  • Virginia Department of Historic Resources — Archives State archaeological records and historic resources documentation.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Virginia Chronicle: Digital Newspaper Archive Over 6 million pages of Virginia newspapers (18th–20th centuries) with full-text search across 269+ titles.
  • Chronicling America — Virginia Newspapers 111 Virginia newspapers (1756–1963) via Library of Congress.
  • Encyclopedia Virginia Nearly 2,000 scholarly articles with primary documents, images, audio, and video.
  • University of Virginia — Special Collections Over 16 million objects: manuscripts, rare books, maps, photographs, audio and video recordings.
  • Virginia Tech Special Collections Specialized collections on Virginia history with digital access to select materials.
  • Virginia Museum of History & Culture — Collections Over 9 million items: manuscripts, maps, broadsides, and sheet music.
