Native Peoples: Miami, Potawatomi & Shawnee

  • Indiana University — Great Lakes–Ohio Valley Ethnohistory Collection Materials on Miami, Potawatomi, Shawnee, Delaware, Kickapoo, and other nations, with parts digitized in collaboration with the Citizen Potawatomi Nation, Eastern Shawnee Tribe, and Wyandotte Nation.
  • Digital Treaties Historic treaties between the U.S. and tribal nations, including treaties signed at Grouseland near Vincennes and Fort Wayne involving Miami, Potawatomi, Shawnee, and Delaware peoples.

Battle of Tippecanoe & Tecumseh (1811)

  • University of Michigan — The Battle of Tippecanoe (1889) Full digitized text of Reed Beard’s account of the November 7, 1811 battle, including historical sketches and lives of the Prophet and Tecumseh.
  • U.S. Army — Staff Ride Handbook for the Battle of Tippecanoe Detailed military and historical analysis of the battle between William Henry Harrison’s forces and Tecumseh’s confederation.

New Harmony Utopian Community (1814–1860s)

  • University of Southern Indiana — Historic New Harmony Archives documenting New Harmony’s Harmonist (1814–1824) and Owenite (1825–1827) communities, with digitization projects on utopian reform, early American education, and scientific inquiry.
  • Purdue University — New Harmony Collection Correspondence, newspapers, illustrations, maps, and pamphlets from New Harmony and the Robert Owen family papers.
  • Indiana State Library — New Harmony Subject Guide Guide to Indiana’s New Harmony collection materials across state institutions.

Underground Railroad & Levi Coffin

  • Indiana Historical Society — Levi and Catharine Coffin Essay Scholarly essay on the Quaker couple whose Fountain City home was called the “Grand Central Station of the Underground Railroad,” aiding an estimated 2,000 freedom seekers.
  • Levi and Catharine Coffin State Historic Site Official site with digital resources on Indiana’s role in the Underground Railroad network.

Civil War: Morgan’s Raid & Indiana Regiments

  • Indiana State Library — Morgan’s Raid Resources Bibliography and digital resources on the July 1863 Confederate raid into Indiana, including county-by-county damage claims with claimant names and property values lost.
  • Indiana Historical Society — Morgan’s Raid Map Digitized map tracing John Hunt Morgan’s 1863 route through Indiana.
  • Indiana Archives — Civil War Military Records Searchable muster rolls and military records for Indiana Civil War volunteer regiments.

Steel Industry: Gary, Indiana

  • IU Northwest — U.S. Steel Gary Works Photograph Collection (1906–1971) Over 2,200 digitized images documenting the creation and operation of the world’s largest steel mill, including the essay “Gary, the Magic City.”
  • IU Northwest — Calumet Regional Archives Digitized materials from the Calumet Regional Archives including Inland Steel records and steel industry photographs.

Indiana Klan Era (1920s)

  • Hoosier State Chronicles — Ku Klux Klan Newspapers Includes the Klan’s Fiery Cross newspaper (edited from Indianapolis when Indiana was the national Klan headquarters), plus anti-Klan voices: the Indianapolis Recorder (African American) and the Muncie Post-Democrat.

Indianapolis 500 & Automotive History

  • Indianapolis Motor Speedway — Digital Archive Official digital archive with photographic documentation spanning every decade of Indy 500 history since 1909.
  • IU Indianapolis — IMS Collection Digitized vital photographic history from the Speedway dating back to 1909, created in partnership with the IMS Museum.
  • The Henry Ford — The First Indianapolis 500 (1911) Photographs of 23 of 40 qualifying cars from the inaugural 1911 race.

Middletown Studies: Muncie, Indiana

  • Ball State University — What Middletown Read Database Searchable database of handwritten reading records from the 1920s–1930s Muncie research, part of the famous Middletown sociological studies by Robert and Helen Lynd.
  • Ball State — Middletown Digital Oral History Collection Audio and transcriptions of oral history interviews with African American, Jewish, Catholic, and labor union communities in Muncie.
  • Ball State — Stoeckel Archives of Local History Manuscripts on Muncie business, industry, churches, clubs, schools, genealogy, government, and photographs.

John Dillinger & the Prohibition Era

  • Indiana Archives — Dillinger Collection Mug shots and Indiana State Reformatory and State Prison records for “Public Enemy No. 1.”
  • Indiana State Police — The Pursuit of Public Enemy #1 Historical documentation of Indiana law enforcement’s response to the Dillinger gang.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Indiana State Library — Digital Collections Over 2 million searchable records including county histories, city directories, plat books, photographs, and yearbooks.
  • Indiana Memory Collaborative project connecting Indiana libraries, archives, museums, and cultural institutions with digitized books, manuscripts, photographs, and maps.
  • Indiana Historical Society — Digital Images Nearly 100,000+ freely accessible digital images including photographs, maps, letters, diaries, documents, and artifacts.
  • Indiana University — Digital Collections Growing repository of digitized special collections materials from IU campuses.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Indiana Newspapers (Library of Congress) Digitized Indiana newspaper titles, fully searchable through 1963.
  • Hoosier State Chronicles Indiana’s Digital Historic Newspaper Program with free access to digitized historic newspapers across the state.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Indiana Aggregated search across Indiana cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
