Colonial & Early Republic Commerce

  • Library of Congress — Alexander Hamilton Papers Approximately 12,000 digitized items including Hamilton’s reports on public credit, the national bank, and manufactures—the foundational documents of American fiscal policy.
  • Founders Online — Papers of Alexander Hamilton Fully searchable and annotated edition of Hamilton’s writings including Treasury Department correspondence, reports to Congress, and financial planning documents.
  • National Archives — Louisiana Purchase Treaty (1803) Full text of the treaty including the $15 million financing arrangement that doubled the national debt—a landmark in government finance and territorial commerce.
  • Library of Congress — A Century of Lawmaking for a New Nation Congressional records from 1774–1875 including debates on tariffs, the national bank, internal improvements, and the foundational economic legislation of the republic.

Banking & Financial History

  • Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis — FRASER (Federal Reserve Archival System for Economic Research) Over 180,000 digitized documents on American financial history: Federal Reserve Board publications, congressional testimonies, banking crisis records, policy speeches, and statistical releases from 1913 to the present.
  • FRASER — Annual Reports of the Comptroller of the Currency Complete digitized run of the Comptroller’s annual reports documenting the national banking system, bank failures, and financial conditions from the Civil War era forward.
  • National Archives — Records of the Federal Reserve System (RG 82) Finding aid to Federal Reserve records including Board of Governors minutes, correspondence, and policy deliberations from the system’s founding in 1913.
  • Library Company of Philadelphia — Digital Collections Digitized materials from America’s first lending library, including early American currency, Bank of the United States records (1790–1842), mercantile papers, and financial ephemera.

Historical Economic Data

  • Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis — FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data) Over 816,000 freely accessible economic time series: GDP, employment, inflation, interest rates, trade balances, and monetary aggregates—many with data extending back decades or more.
  • National Bureau of Economic Research — Macrohistory Database 3,036 economic data series covering pre–World War I and interwar economies: production, construction, employment, money supply, prices, and foreign trade, organized in 16 chapters.
  • Bureau of Economic Analysis — Historical Data Tables Official National Income and Product Accounts from 1929 to the present: GDP components, personal income, corporate profits, and government expenditures at national, state, and local levels.
  • IPUMS — National Historical Geographic Information System (NHGIS) Freely available aggregate census data (1790–present) with GIS-compatible boundary files, enabling analysis of population, economic, and demographic data by county, state, and metropolitan area.
  • IPUMS USA — Historical Census Microdata Integrated individual-level census data from 1850 to the present, including occupational classifications, income, industry codes, and labor force information for quantitative economic history.
  • USDA — Census of Agriculture (Historical Archive) Digitized agricultural census reports from 1840 to the present: farm size, crop production, livestock, farm value, tenancy rates, and mechanization data at the county level.

Trade & Tariff Policy

  • FRASER — Tariff of 1816 (Dallas Tariff) Digitized text of the first protective tariff, which launched decades of debate over protectionism, sectional conflict, and American industrial development.
  • Library of Congress — A Century of Lawmaking Congressional debates on major tariff legislation: the Tariff of Abominations (1828), Morrill Tariff (1861), McKinley Tariff (1890), and other acts that shaped American trade policy.
  • U.S. State Department — Foreign Relations of the United States (FRUS) Official diplomatic record including documents on Bretton Woods negotiations (1944), the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT), and postwar trade liberalization.
  • Office of the U.S. Trade Representative — Archives Official texts of NAFTA, bilateral trade agreements, and negotiating documents from the modern era of American trade policy.

Corporate & Industrial Development

  • Hagley Museum & Library — Digital Collections Over 300,000 digitized images from one of the nation’s premier business history archives: DuPont Company records (1802–20th c.), trade catalogs, industrial photographs, and Delaware Valley business manuscripts.
  • Harvard Business School — Baker Library Special Collections Nearly 1,600 business manuscript collections (17th–20th centuries) including the Kress Collection of Business and Economics texts (1450–1914) and manufacturing course reports.
  • Rockefeller Archive Center — DIMES Online Collections Finding aids and digitized materials from the Rockefeller family and associated institutions: Standard Oil correspondence, philanthropic records, and corporate leadership papers.
  • Library of Congress — Standard Oil Co. v. United States, 221 U.S. 1 (1911) Full Supreme Court opinion in the landmark antitrust case that dissolved Standard Oil into 34 companies and established the “rule of reason” in antitrust law.
  • Securities and Exchange Commission — EDGAR Database Freely searchable database of corporate filings (10-K, 10-Q, proxy statements) since 1994—the public record of American corporate disclosure.

Transportation & Infrastructure

  • New York State Library — Erie Canal Digital Collections Digitized maps, construction plans, drawings, and administrative records from the canal that transformed American commerce after its 1825 opening, linking the Great Lakes to the Atlantic.
  • National Archives — ICC Railroad Valuation Records Engineering field notes, detailed land maps, and property records from the 1915–1920 comprehensive federal railroad inventory covering virtually every American rail line.
  • Library of Congress — Railroads Research Guide Gateway to LOC’s railroad collections: corporate records, photographs, maps, ICC publications, and materials on railroad land grants, labor, and regulation.
  • National Archives — Pacific Railway Act (1862) Full text of the legislation authorizing the transcontinental railroad with massive land grants, transforming western development and corporate-government relations. (See also Westward Expansion.)

The Great Depression & New Deal Economics

  • Library of Congress — FSA/OWI Photographs (1935–1945) Over 164,000 photographs documenting Depression-era economic hardship, migrant labor, rural poverty, and New Deal programs by Dorothea Lange, Walker Evans, Gordon Parks, and others.
  • Yale University — Photogrammar Interactive platform for exploring 170,000 FSA/OWI photographs with geolocation, photographer, and subject metadata—a visual record of the Depression-era economy.
  • The Living New Deal Interactive map and database of over 15,000 New Deal projects across the United States: public works, infrastructure, arts programs, and conservation projects with photographs and documentation.
  • National Archives — National Labor Relations Act (1935) Full text of the Wagner Act guaranteeing collective bargaining rights—a cornerstone of New Deal labor-management reform.
  • National Archives — New Deal Records Guide Finding aids for Civilian Conservation Corps (RG 35), Works Progress Administration (RG 69), and other New Deal agency records documenting federal economic intervention.
  • Library of Congress — Great Depression and New Deal Digital Collections Curated gateway to Depression-era primary sources: WPA life histories, Federal Writers’ Project narratives, posters, and documentary photography.

The Agricultural Economy

  • USDA — Census of Agriculture (Historical) County-level agricultural data from 1840 to the present: crop acreage, livestock counts, farm values, tenancy, mechanization, and labor—essential for quantitative agricultural history.
  • USDA — National Agricultural Library Digital Collections Historical USDA publications, yearbooks, and bulletins documenting agricultural policy, research, and rural life from the Department’s founding in 1862.
  • Bracero History Archive Over 150 oral histories, photographs, and documents from the Bracero guest worker program (1942–1964), a collaboration of George Mason, the Smithsonian, Brown, and UT El Paso. (See also Labor & Industry.)
  • DPLA — The Dust Bowl Primary sources on the ecological and economic catastrophe of the 1930s: photographs, government reports, personal accounts, and New Deal soil conservation records.

The Postwar Economy

  • Harry S. Truman Library — Marshall Plan Collection 52 digitized documents (441 pages) on the European Recovery Program (1947–1953), including planning memoranda, congressional testimony, and implementation records.
  • George C. Marshall Foundation — Marshall Plan Documents Digitized speeches, policy documents, films, and oral histories on the $13 billion program that rebuilt postwar Europe and anchored American economic leadership.
  • FRUS — Bretton Woods and International Monetary Policy Diplomatic records on the 1944 Bretton Woods Conference, the creation of the IMF and World Bank, and postwar international economic architecture.
  • Eisenhower Presidential Library — Interstate Highway System Documents on the Federal-Aid Highway Act of 1956 and the creation of the Interstate system, the largest public works project in American history and a catalyst for postwar suburbanization and commerce.

Major Research Collections

  • FRASER — Federal Reserve Archival System for Economic Research The single most important digital archive for American economic history: 180,000+ documents on monetary policy, banking regulation, financial crises, and economic data from 1913 to the present.
  • FRED — Federal Reserve Economic Data 816,000+ freely downloadable time series for quantitative economic research: GDP, employment, prices, money supply, interest rates, trade, and industry-level data.
  • Hagley Museum & Library — Digital Collections Premier American business history archive with 300,000+ digitized images: corporate records, trade catalogs, industrial photographs, and business manuscripts from the colonial era to the twentieth century.
  • Harvard Business School — Baker Library Special Collections 1,600 business manuscript collections spanning four centuries, with the Kress Collection of pre-modern economic texts and extensive corporate archives.
  • Library of Congress — Business and Labor History Research Guide Comprehensive gateway to LOC holdings: company records, organizational papers, personal papers of business leaders, and government economic records.
