Lenape (Delaware) People

  • Delaware Public Archives — History of the Lenape Indian Tribe Historical overview and resources documenting the Lenape (Delaware) people’s presence and history in the region.
  • University of Pennsylvania — The Lenape: Pre-History to the 18th Century Scholarly resource on Lenape pre-contact history through colonial displacement, with primary source references.

New Sweden & Dutch Colonial Settlement (1638–1664)

  • Delaware Public Archives — Colonial & Pre-Statehood Records Miscellaneous colonial records from Swedish, Dutch, and English administrations, including original documents and microfilm copies.
  • New Netherland Institute — Delaware in New Netherland Digital exhibition exploring Delaware’s colonial period under Dutch rule (1655–1664), including Fort Christina.
  • Internet Archive — Swedish Settlements on the Delaware Amandus Johnson’s definitive scholarly work on the New Sweden colony (1638–1664), fully digitized and searchable.
  • Colonial Swedes — Chronology of Swedish Colonial Presence Timeline and documentation of Swedish colonial settlement on the Delaware, 1638–1713.

William Penn & Early Governance

  • Historical Society of Pennsylvania — Deed Between William Penn and Native Americans Primary source document of the July 15, 1682 deed granting William Penn land between the Delaware River and Neshaminy Creek.
  • Avalon Project (Yale Law School) — Delaware Constitution of 1776 Full text of Delaware’s original state constitution, the first adopted following independence.

The First State: Ratification of the Constitution (December 7, 1787)

  • Delaware Public Archives — Ratification Document Original ratification document of the U.S. Constitution signed December 7, 1787, with signatures of delegates and the state seal. Delaware was the first state to ratify.
  • Avalon Project (Yale Law School) — Delaware Ratification Text Full text of Delaware’s Constitution ratification, with complete legal language and signatories.

Slavery, Freedom & the Underground Railroad

  • University of Delaware — Delaware and the Underground Railroad Digital exhibition with primary sources, documents, and artifacts related to the Underground Railroad in Delaware.
  • Delaware Public Archives — Tracing Your African American Roots Digital collection of eBooks, documents, photographs, and audio about early African American life in Delaware.
  • University of Delaware — Underground Railroad Research Guide Research guide directing to primary sources and scholarship on the Underground Railroad in Delaware.

du Pont Family & Industrial History

  • Hagley Museum and Library — Digital Archives Portal to all Hagley digitized collections, including E.I. du Pont de Nemours & Company records, family papers, photographs, and documents from the gunpowder manufacturing era onward.
  • Hagley Digital Archives — du Pont Collections Digital access to du Pont company records, family papers, and photographs tracing the dynasty from gunpowder manufacturing to the modern chemical industry.
  • Hagley Museum and Library — Collections Overview Description of physical and digital collections including 37,000 linear feet of manuscripts, 290,000 printed volumes, and 300,000+ digital images on American business and industrial history.

Civil War: A Border State

  • University of Delaware — A State Divided: Delaware During the Civil War Digital exhibition on Delaware’s unique Civil War experience as a border slave state that remained in the Union, featuring letters, speeches, lithographs, and photographs.
  • Delaware Public Archives — Civil War Primary Sources Guide to Civil War primary sources including photographs, documents, and drawings from the era.

Desegregation & Civil Rights

  • Delaware Public Archives — Desegregation Archives Materials documenting Delaware’s school desegregation. Delaware’s Belton v. Gebhart was one of five cases consolidated into Brown v. Board of Education (1954).
  • University of Delaware — Civil Rights & African American Studies Research guide to primary sources on civil rights and African American history in Delaware.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Delaware Public Archives — Digital Collections Main portal to digitized Delaware historical records including legislative papers, colonial records, photographs, and maps.
  • University of Delaware — Digital Collections Open-access visual materials including photographs, postcards, maps, and materials supporting Delaware history and culture.
  • Winterthur Museum — Digital Collections Over 10,000 trade catalogs and digitized items from the Winterthur Library, documenting American decorative arts and material culture.
  • Delaware Public Archives — DE250 Resources Curated primary sources and eBooks celebrating Delaware’s history and Revolutionary War role.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Delaware Newspapers (Library of Congress) Digitized Delaware newspaper titles from the 1700s through the early 1900s, fully searchable.
  • Delaware Public Archives — Historic Maps Collection Historical maps of Delaware from the 1600s–2000s, with digital copies available online.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Delaware Aggregated search across Delaware cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
