Home › Midwest

Regional collections spanning Iowa, Illinois, Indiana, Michigan, Minnesota, Missouri, Ohio, and Wisconsin. See also individual state pages.

Regional Digital Portals

  • Ohio Memory 1,000,000+ digitized items from nearly 400 Ohio cultural institutions: photographs, maps, documents, archaeological artifacts, newspapers, and oral histories. A partnership of Ohio History Connection and the State Library of Ohio.
  • Recollection Wisconsin Hundreds of thousands of photos, maps, letters, diaries, oral histories, and artifacts from 250+ Wisconsin institutions, integrated with the Digital Public Library of America.
  • Explore Chicago Collections Unified search across 100,000+ maps, photographs, letters, and archival materials from 45+ Chicago-area libraries, museums, historical societies, and cultural institutions.
  • Iowa Digital Library Over 1 million digitized objects from University of Iowa Libraries: Civil War diaries, maps, historic newspapers, railroadiana, and regional cultural materials.

Northwest Territory & Early Settlement

  • Library of Congress — Pioneering the Upper Midwest (ca. 1820–1910) First-person accounts, biographies, local histories, and ethnographic texts from Michigan, Minnesota, and Wisconsin, including essays on British control (1763–1783) and early American settlement of the Old Northwest.
  • Canadian Museum of History — Virtual Museum of New France: Fur Trade Interactive maps, illustrations, and materials on French fur trade operations across the Great Lakes region from the sixteenth through eighteenth centuries, documenting the French colonial foundations of the Midwest.

Great Lakes Maritime & Commerce

  • Bowling Green State University — Historical Collections of the Great Lakes 100,000+ vessel images, a vessel database with data sheets, Lake Erie Ports Database, and Maritime Personnel database documenting shipping, shipbuilding, commercial fishing, and shipwrecks across the Great Lakes.
  • UW-Madison — Great Lakes Maritime History Project 6,400+ ship files, charts, maps, lighthouse records, vessel logbooks, blueprints, and statistics focused on Lake Superior and the Twin Ports of Duluth–Superior.
  • Wisconsin Shipwrecks Collaborative online exhibit featuring Great Lakes shipwrecks in Lakes Superior and Michigan: underwater video, historic photographs, and archaeological discoveries.
  • Cleveland Memory Project — Ohio & Erie Canal Historic photographs, maps, and period advertisements documenting the Ohio & Erie Canal (constructed 1825–1832), which connected Lake Erie to the Ohio River and transformed Midwest commerce.

Immigration & Ethnic Communities

  • University of Minnesota — Immigration History Research Center Archives Comprehensive archives for central, eastern, and southern European immigrants: 250+ digital immigrant stories, the Immigrant Letters Project (1850–1970), rare publications, and ethnic newspapers documenting the peopling of the Midwest.
  • IHRCA — Immigrant Stories 250+ digital immigration narratives with tools for creating and sharing new stories, preserving the experiences of successive waves of newcomers to the Upper Midwest.
  • Minnesota Historical Society — Collections “Becoming Minnesotan” oral histories, photography collections, maps, and records documenting Minnesota’s German, Scandinavian, Polish, and other immigrant communities.

Industrial Cities

  • Chicago History Museum — Digital Collections 330,000+ digitized images: Chicago Sun-Times photography (75+ years), 55,000 Chicago Daily News images (1902–1933), and 1,000 Hedrich-Blessing architectural photographs of Chicago buildings.
  • Detroit Public Library — Burton Historical Collection Digital index of holdings documenting Detroit from 1727–1953: genealogical resources, census records, city directories, military records, and 660,000+ historical maps.
  • Cleveland Memory Project Photographs, documents, video, and oral histories on Cleveland history, including the 500,000-image Cleveland Press photo collection and specialized materials on industrial development and women’s history.
  • Encyclopedia of Milwaukee 700 entries on Milwaukee history: industrial development, labor, business, philanthropy, and notable people, with educational resources for teaching urban Midwest history.
  • Southeast Chicago Historical Museum — Digital Archive Photographs, oral histories, and 13 exhibits on Southeast Chicago’s steel mills, labor organizing, ethnic communities, the Memorial Day Massacre, and environmental impacts of deindustrialization.

The Labor Movement

  • Wayne State University — Walter P. Reuther Library Millions of primary source documents on the American labor movement: UAW records, personal papers of major labor leaders, and the Archives of Labor and Urban Affairs. (See also Labor & Industry.)
  • Northern Illinois University — The Pullman Strike Primary sources on the 1894 nationwide railroad strike affecting 150,000 workers across 27 states, centered on the Pullman Company town south of Chicago.
  • Newberry Library — The Jungle and the Community Scholarly and reform documents on Chicago’s meatpacking industry, stockyards workers, and turn-of-the-century labor conditions (ca. 1901–1911).

The Great Migration

  • Newberry Library — Chicago and the Great Migration Primary sources on African American migration to Chicago (1915–1950): poems, maps, photographs, newspapers, travel guides, and government reports documenting the transformation of Northern cities.
  • Library of Congress — African American Mosaic: The Great Migration Photographs, maps, and manuscripts documenting African American migrations to Chicago, Detroit, Cleveland, and other Midwest industrial cities.

Major Research Collections

  • Newberry Library (Chicago) 1,200+ archival collections (15,000+ linear feet) dating from 1700 to the present, with 600+ collections focused specifically on Midwest history: literature, music, journalism, social action, labor, and settlement house movements.
  • Wisconsin Historical Society — Collections 290 million items including 110,000+ historical images online, manuscript and photograph collections, state and local government records, and audio recordings—one of the nation’s largest historical society collections.
  • State Historical Society of Missouri — Digital Collections Photographs, art, maps, diaries, letters, oral histories, and 8 million+ digitized newspaper pages through Missouri Digital Heritage (9+ million total records).
  • Indiana State Library — Digital Collections Materials from institutions across Indiana: rare books, manuscripts, maps, photographs, and documents on Indiana and regional Midwest history from the fifteenth century to the present.
