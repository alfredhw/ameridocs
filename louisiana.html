Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • LSU Special Collections — Indigenous Materials Guide to materials on Native American cultures including Caddo, Chitimacha, and other Louisiana tribes.
  • Chitimacha Tribe of Louisiana — Tribal History Official tribal government historical documentation and cultural preservation materials.
  • 64 Parishes — Caddo Culture Scholarly encyclopedia entry on Caddo culture and history of northwest Louisiana.
  • Louisiana Colonial Documents Digitization Project — Indigenous Materials Searchable database of French and Spanish colonial records documenting interactions with Native peoples.

French Colonial Era (1682–1762)

  • Louisiana Colonial Documents Digitization Project Digitized French Superior Council records (1714–1769) and related colonial documents with searchable interface.
  • Library of Congress — La Salle’s Taking Possession of Louisiana, 1682 Original French document of La Salle's expedition claiming Louisiana for France.
  • Louisiana Historical Center — Colonial Documents French Superior Council records (1714–1769) and original 1724 Code Noir signed by Louis XV.
  • Library of Congress — French Colonial Trade and Justice Rules Complete early colonial governance documents including Company of the Indies regulations.
  • 64 Parishes — French Colonial Louisiana Scholarly encyclopedia entry on the French colonial period with primary document references.

Spanish Colonial Era (1762–1800)

  • Louisiana Colonial Documents — Spanish Judicial Records Digitized Spanish Judicial Records (1769–1803) including Acadian settlement documentation.
  • Center for Louisiana Studies — Acadian Genealogy Collections Acadian family papers, settlement records, and cultural documentation from the Spanish colonial period forward.
  • University of Louisiana Lafayette — Digital Archives Digitized materials on Cajun and Creole history including Spanish colonial administrative records.
  • 64 Parishes — Acadians in Colonial Louisiana Scholarly entry on Acadian settlement and adaptation during Spanish colonial rule.

Territorial Period & Early Statehood (1800–1860)

  • Library of Congress — Louisiana Purchase: Primary Documents 119 items from Library of Congress special collections including Jefferson's messages, treaties, and negotiation documents.
  • Avalon Project (Yale) — Louisiana Purchase Documents Full texts of the Louisiana Purchase treaty, protocols, and official correspondence from 1803 negotiations.
  • Historic New Orleans Collection — Battle of New Orleans Original letters, reports, and documents including the Andrew Jackson Collection and Latour Archives.
  • Library of Congress — Jackson’s Account of the Battle of New Orleans Andrew Jackson's original letter to James Monroe describing the final battle on January 8, 1815.
  • Evergreen Plantation Slavery Database Digitized estate inventories, succession records, and sacramental records from plantation archives.
  • Free People of Color in Louisiana Digital Project 30,000+ pages of family papers, business records, and public documents from LSU, Tulane, HNOC, and Louisiana State Museum.
  • Archdiocese of New Orleans — Sacramental Records Digitized baptism, marriage, and funeral records (1718–1815) searchable online, covering diverse racial and ethnic identities.

Civil War & Reconstruction

  • Louisiana Digital Library — Civil War Collections Searchable digitized wartime documents, military records, and Reconstruction-era materials from multiple repositories.
  • Supreme Court of Louisiana Historical Archives Digitized Louisiana Supreme Court cases (1813–1921) including Reconstruction-era judicial decisions.
  • National Archives — Plessy v. Ferguson Official Supreme Court decision (1896) from the New Orleans case establishing “separate but equal.”
  • DPLA — Civil War and Reconstruction Aggregated Freedmen's Bureau records and related Louisiana Civil War documents from multiple archives.

Late 19th & Early 20th Century

  • Library of Congress — Plessy v. Ferguson: Primary Documents Supreme Court opinions, newspaper coverage, and legal briefs related to the 1896 decision.
  • Chronicling America — Plessy v. Ferguson in Newspapers Contemporary newspaper coverage of the Plessy decision and Jim Crow implementation.
  • LSU Libraries — Huey P. Long Files Original Huey Long papers including speeches, political documents, and “Share Our Wealth” program materials.
  • Louisiana Tech — Huey P. Long Newspaper Clippings Newspaper clipping collection documenting Long's political career and assassination investigation.
  • LSU — ExxonMobil Baton Rouge Historic Collection Industrial history materials including Standard Oil/Exxon newsletters (1924–1999) documenting Louisiana's oil industry.

Modern Louisiana

  • Historic New Orleans Collection — Hurricane Katrina Resources Stories, photographs, artwork, video, and primary source materials documenting Katrina's impact and recovery.
  • Hurricane Digital Memory Bank Searchable digital archive of stories, photographs, and personal documents from Hurricanes Katrina and Rita.
  • New Orleans City Archives — Hurricane Katrina Records Municipal government recovery plans, progress reports, photographs, and administrative records.
  • NAACP Legal Defense Fund Records (Library of Congress) 80,000 digitized items (1915–1968) including Louisiana regional lawyer files and federal court cases.

Louisiana Government & Law

  • Law Library of Louisiana — History of the Codes Research guides covering Code Noir, 1825 Civil Code, Napoleonic Code adaptation, and Louisiana's civil law system.
  • Law Library of Louisiana — 1825 Civil Code Origins Research guide documenting the origins of Louisiana's distinctive civil law tradition.
  • Louisiana State Library — Digital Records State government documents and official records from 1807 to present.
  • Secretary of State — Historical Resources Research Library Official government repository for historical records including legislative documents and administrative archives.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Louisiana Digital Library Central hub aggregating 400,000+ digitized items from 25 participating institutions across Louisiana.
  • Chronicling America — Louisiana Newspapers 139 digitized Louisiana newspapers (1805–1963) from the Library of Congress, fully searchable.
  • LSU — Digitizing Louisiana Newspapers Project 128 Louisiana newspaper titles with 328,900 pages digitized, covering 1836–1922.
  • Historic New Orleans Collection — Databases Collins C. Diboll Vieux Carré Digital Survey, Lost Friends database, and digital catalog of 1 million artifacts.
  • 64 Parishes — Encyclopedia Freely accessible online encyclopedia with thousands of articles on Louisiana history, culture, and institutions.
  • Tulane University — Louisiana Primary Sources Guide Curated guide to freely accessible primary source materials and digital collections for Louisiana research.
  • Library of Congress — Louisiana State Resource Guide Comprehensive guide linking to Library of Congress digital collections related to Louisiana.
