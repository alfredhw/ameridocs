Bleeding Kansas & the Border War (1854–1861)

  • Civil War on the Western Border Comprehensive digital resource with primary source sets on Bleeding Kansas, John Brown, the Pottawatomie Massacre, Quantrill’s Raiders, and Kansas Territorial records.
  • Library of Congress — Bleeding Kansas in Chronicling America Research guide connecting to contemporary newspaper coverage of the violent struggle over slavery in Kansas Territory.
  • DPLA — John Brown Primary Source Set Documents, letters, and contemporary accounts related to John Brown’s Kansas activities and subsequent raid on Harpers Ferry.

Exodusters & Nicodemus

  • DPLA — Exodusters: African American Migration to the Great Plains Maps, photographs, broadsides, testimonies, and documents on the post-Reconstruction Black migration to Kansas, including Benjamin Singleton’s broadsides and Kansas Freedmen’s Relief Association reports.
  • Kansas Memory — Nicodemus Collection Certification of incorporation, Nicodemus Western Cyclone newspaper articles, census data, and colony advertisements for the historic all-Black town founded in 1877.

Populist Movement (1890s)

  • Wichita State University — Populist Period Pamphlet Collection 29 pamphlets on Populist Party issues, Kansas government, and social reform, including speeches by William Jennings Bryan.
  • Kansas Historical Society — Kansapedia: Populism Thematic overview with references to primary sources on the People’s Party movement that swept Kansas in the 1890s.

Carry Nation & the Temperance Movement

  • Pittsburg State University — The Smasher’s Mail (1901) Thirteen digitized issues of the newspaper published by Carrie Nation, containing anti-alcohol essays, editorials, supporter and opponent letters, illustrations, cartoons, and temperance poetry.
  • Library of Congress — Carrie Nation in Chronicling America Research guide to contemporary newspaper coverage of Nation’s saloon-smashing temperance crusade.
  • Kansas Historical Society — Carry Nation Papers (1870–1919) Diary entries, letters to family members, and correspondence with public officials and temperance supporters.

Dust Bowl

  • Library of Congress — FSA/OWI Photographs Extensive pictorial record (1935–1944) including Kansas Dust Bowl photography by Arthur Rothstein and other Farm Security Administration photographers.
  • Library of Congress — American Life Histories (WPA) Approximately 2,900 documents from 300+ Federal Writers’ Project workers (1936–1940), including Kansas oral histories and life narratives from the Dust Bowl years.

Brown v. Board of Education (1954)

  • Library of Congress — Brown v. Board Research Guide Comprehensive guide to primary sources including newspapers, photographs, films, and manuscripts related to the landmark Topeka desegregation case.
  • National Archives — Brown v. Board Milestone Document Digitized Supreme Court decision and related materials from the case that declared school segregation unconstitutional.
  • Kansas Memory — Brown v. Board Materials 70-minute Kansas Bar Association video with reenactment of 1952–1953 Supreme Court oral arguments, plus photographs and documents.

Dwight D. Eisenhower

  • Eisenhower Presidential Library — Online Documents Digitized documents, speeches, photographs, and papers from the Abilene, Kansas presidential library. Materials in the public domain.
  • Johns Hopkins — Papers of Dwight David Eisenhower Searchable scholarly edition of Eisenhower’s papers with full text and historical context.

Fort Leavenworth & Military History

  • Combined Arms Research Library — Digital Collections Digitized military documents including World War I pamphlets and papers from the U.S. Army Command and General Staff College at Fort Leavenworth.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Kansas Memory (Kansas Historical Society) Nearly 600,000 archival images and state government records, including photographs, maps, documents, oral histories, and objects organized by historical period.
  • University of Kansas — Kenneth Spencer Research Library Manuscripts, photographs, maps, architectural drawings, books, newspapers, films, and videotapes documenting the Kansas experience from 1854 to present.
  • Kansas State University — Archives and Special Collections Over one million items including the Kansas Agriculture and Rural Life collection (5,000+ boxes from 1835–present).

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Kansas Newspapers (Library of Congress) Over 300,000 pages from 42 Kansas newspaper titles across 28 cities, fully searchable.
  • Kansas Historical Society — Kansas Digital Newspaper Program Nearly 12 million pages from Kansas newspapers (1854–1923), including nearly every paper from Kansas cities and towns.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Kansas Aggregated search across Kansas cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
