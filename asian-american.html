Chinese Immigration & the Exclusion Era

  • Library of Congress — Chinese Exclusion Act Digital Collections Manuscripts, government documents, newspaper articles, and broadsides related to the Chinese Exclusion Act (1882) and Chinese American immigration history: interrogation transcripts, affidavits, and photographs of detained Chinese immigrants. (See also Immigration.)
  • UC Berkeley — Chinese Inclusion Project 8,000 images and pages of primary sources from the Bancroft Library and California Historical Society on Chinese immigration to California: photographs, letters, business records, and legal documents. (See also Pacific.)
  • National Archives — Chinese Americans Research Guide Portal to federal records on Chinese American history including digitized affidavits, photographs, letters to immigration officials, and interrogation transcripts of Chinese immigrants under exclusion-era restrictions.
  • DPLA — Early Chinese Immigration to the US Curated primary sources with overview, teaching guide, and related resources on the Chinese American experience from the Gold Rush through the Exclusion era.

Angel Island Immigration Station

  • Angel Island Immigration Station Foundation — The Vault Digital archive from the “Ellis Island of the West”: records from approximately 200,000 Asian immigrant case files, photographs, documents, and poetry carved into detention barracks walls (1910–1940). (See also Pacific.)
  • DPLA — Immigration Through Angel Island Curated primary sources on the Angel Island immigration experience with overview, teaching guide, and key documents including historical letters, photographs of missionary English lessons, and Chinese poem documentation.
  • UC Berkeley — Angel Island Oral History Project Transcriptions and original recordings from the Asian American Studies Program’s oral history project (1988–1990), with immigrant voices from detainees held at Angel Island (1910–1940).

Japanese American Experience

  • Densho — Japanese American Digital Archive The largest freely accessible archive of Japanese American incarceration materials: over 100,000 items including nearly 2,000 hours of indexed video interviews, 80,000+ photographs and documents, personal letters, diaries, government papers, scrapbooks, and artwork spanning immigration through WWII incarceration and redress.
  • UC Berkeley/Calisphere — Japanese American Relocation Digital Archive Thousands of primary sources documenting WWII Japanese American internment: personal diaries, letters, photographs, drawings, War Relocation Authority camp newsletters, and 100,000+ original manuscript items from the Japanese American Evacuation and Resettlement Study.
  • Stanford/Hoover Institution — Hoji Shinbun Digital Collection The world’s largest online archive of open-access Japanese American newspapers: 500,000+ pages from 60+ titles covering prewar and internment eras, with mix of Japanese and English text reflecting the business and social life of Japanese American communities.
  • National Archives — Japanese American Internment Records Digitized internee cards, 4,000+ War Relocation Authority photographs, and Ansel Adams’s Manzanar photographs documenting the forced relocation and incarceration of 120,000 Japanese Americans. (See also Pacific.)

Filipino American History

  • CSU Dominguez Hills — Filipino American Digital Archives Digital collections documenting the vital role of Filipino Americans in US history: photographs, videos, oral histories, meeting notes, memorabilia, and family records from the colonial period through the contemporary era. (See also Pacific.)
  • University of Maryland — Philippine and Filipino American History Research guide to primary source databases and historical newspapers related to Philippine and Filipino American history, with links to 300+ published sources and the Filipino American Community Archives documenting communities in the DC Metro area.
  • University of Michigan — Philippine Photographs Digital Archive 2,100+ digitized images depicting Filipinos, buildings, monuments, political and military leaders, and landscapes from the turn-of-the-twentieth-century Philippines during the early American occupation era.

South Asian American History

  • South Asian American Digital Archive (SAADA) Nonprofit dedicated to preserving South Asian American stories since 2008: oral history interviews, photographs, newspaper clippings, community publications, and digital materials spanning the late 1600s to the present. Notable projects include the “First Days Project” collecting immigrant first-experience stories.

Korean American & Vietnamese American Communities

  • USC — Korean American Digital Collection 13,000+ pages of documents, 1,900+ photographs, and 180 sound files chronicling the Korean American community (1903–1965), plus the Korean American Digital Archive (KADA) with 25,000+ resources from the Korean American Museum Oral History Series.
  • Vietnamese in the Diaspora Digital Archive (ViDDA) Open-access primary sources and interviews providing insight into Vietnamese global diaspora experiences, supported by the Vietnamese American Heritage Foundation—capturing first-generation stories of Vietnam War displacement and resettlement.
  • UC Irvine — Southeast Asian Archive Established 1987 to preserve the social, cultural, religious, political, and economic life of Cambodian, Hmong, Laotian, and Vietnamese diaspora communities: thousands of searchable photographs, oral histories, newsletters, artifacts, and documents including Fort Chaffee refugee photographs and the Chan Troi Moi newspaper.
  • Texas Tech — Vietnam Center: Vietnamese American Collections 157 linear feet of immigration applications, correspondence, speeches, and legal records documenting 12,000+ applicants for the UN Orderly Departure Program—first-generation Vietnamese American refugee and immigration documentation from 1975 onward.

Asian American Civil Rights & Activism

  • Vincent Chin Institute — Legacy Guide Educational resource documenting the 1982 murder of Vincent Chin and its galvanizing role in Asian American civil rights activism, including historical context, legacy analysis, and materials on the American Citizens for Justice organization formed in response.
  • University of Michigan — American Citizens for Justice Records Archives of the ACJ organization (1872–2018): meeting minutes, correspondence, publications, Vincent Chin case files, and audiovisual materials documenting the movement that emerged from the Chin murder and its aftermath.
  • American Archive of Public Broadcasting — AAPI Collection 700+ public radio and television programs (1965–2021) highlighting Asian American and Pacific Islander cultures: programming from PBS Guam and PBS Hawai‘i, civil rights coverage, arts, cultural heritage, and community issues.

Pacific Islander Communities

  • Library of Congress — South Pacific Islands Web Archive Archived web content covering twenty island nations and territories: American Samoa, Guam, Marshall Islands, Northern Mariana Islands, Palau, and others—preserving digital historical and cultural materials from Pacific Islander communities.
  • He ‘Ohina Nūpepa ‘Ōlelo Hawai‘i (Hawaiian Language Newspapers) Transcriptions and translations of Hawaiian-language newspapers published 1834–1948, searchable in English or Hawaiian—preserving a vast body of Native Hawaiian journalism and cultural documentation. (See also Pacific.)
  • National Archives — Asian American and Pacific Islander Records Federal agency records documenting AAPI communities across immigration, military, social services, and administrative functions—including materials on Hawaiian history, Guam, American Samoa, and other Pacific territories.

Major Research Collections

  • Densho Seattle-based nonprofit and flagship institution for Japanese American digital humanities: over 100,000 items freely accessible, the Densho Encyclopedia as reference resource, and K–12 and higher education curricula on incarceration and immigration history.
  • Smithsonian Asian Pacific American Center Established 1997 to ensure AAPI representation across the Smithsonian: archival materials, oral histories, thematic guides, food history resources, and portraits of distinguished AAPI figures across the National Museum of American History and National Portrait Gallery.
  • UC Berkeley — Bancroft Library Asian American Collections Significant Asian American archival holdings: the Japanese American Relocation Digital Archives (100,000+ items), Him Mark Lai Papers, Margaret Chung collection, Save the International Hotel collection, and Angel Island Oral History Project materials.
  • Library of Congress — AAPI Collections The AAPI Collection (est. 2007): individual collections on notable Asian American authors, scholars, activists, and artists, with materials in English, Chinese, Korean, Japanese, Vietnamese, Tagalog, and Hawaiian.
