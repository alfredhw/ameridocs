Colonial Immigration (1607–1776)

  • Virtual Jamestown — Indentured Servants Registers Database of indentured servants sent to Virginia, including the London I Registers (1682–1692, ~1,000 records) and London II Registers (1718–1759, ~3,000 records) documenting colonial labor migration.
  • Library of Congress — Indentured Servants Research Guide Comprehensive finding aid to online indentured servant records from Maryland State Archives, Virginia Land Patents, Library of Virginia, and runaway advertisements (1736–1803).
  • Pennsylvania State Archives — Colonial Passenger Lists Ships’ passenger lists for the Port of Philadelphia (1727–1808) with over 30,000 names of German, Dutch, Swiss, and French immigrants, plus oaths of allegiance and naturalization records.

Early Republic & Antebellum (1790–1860)

  • National Archives — Immigration Records Overview Comprehensive finding aid to all NARA immigration collections including passenger lists, naturalization records, border crossing records, and immigration legislation.
  • National Archives — Access to Archival Databases (AAD) Searchable database of approximately 6 million records of nineteenth-century passengers arriving at Baltimore, Boston, New Orleans, New York, and Philadelphia.
  • National Archives — Passenger Arrival Records Detailed guide to 1820–1982 passenger arrival manifests from all major U.S. ports, with information on accessing digitized copies.

Ellis Island Era (1892–1954)

  • Statue of Liberty–Ellis Island Foundation — Passenger Search Free database of 65 million passenger records and ship manifests (1820–1957) searchable by passenger name, ship name, or destination.
  • Statue of Liberty–Ellis Island Foundation — Oral History Library Nearly 2,000 recorded interviews with passengers, families, immigration officials, military personnel, and island employees, searchable by name, country, and topic.
  • National Park Service — Ellis Island Arrival Records Comprehensive guide to finding Ellis Island passenger records through multiple free databases.
  • FamilySearch — New York Passenger Arrival Lists (1892–1924) Free searchable collection of 25 million names of immigrants with ship manifests showing destinations, sponsors, and financial details.

Angel Island & Chinese Immigration (1910–1940)

  • Angel Island Immigration Station Foundation — Poetry Finder Digitized collection of 154 Chinese poems and inscriptions carved on detention barrack walls, with translations, historical context, and audio recordings in regional dialects.
  • Angel Island Immigration Station Foundation — The Vault Primary sources including family records, administrative documents, photographs, and historical materials documenting Chinese and other Asian immigrant experiences.
  • UC Berkeley — Angel Island Oral History Project 62 oral history transcripts from Chinese immigrants detained at Angel Island (1910–1940).

Asian Exclusion & Restriction Era

  • National Archives — Chinese Exclusion Act (1882) Full text, historical context, and legislative history of the first federal law to exclude an entire ethnic group from immigration.
  • National Archives — Chinese Heritage Research Guide Comprehensive finding guide to Chinese American records across multiple record groups, including immigration, naturalization, and exclusion act case files.
  • Densho Digital Repository Thousands of digitized photographs, documents, newspapers, and 900+ visual histories (1,700+ hours) documenting Japanese American experience from early immigration through WWII incarceration and redress.

The Great Migration (African American, 1910–1970)

  • New York Public Library — In Motion: The African-American Migration Experience 16,500+ pages of texts, 8,300 illustrations, and 60+ maps documenting 13 defining African American migrations, from the domestic slave trade through the return migration to the South.
  • Library of Congress — The Great Migration Curated primary sources including oral histories, maps, photographs, newspapers, and newsreels documenting African American migration from the rural South to urban North and West.
  • Schomburg Center for Research in Black Culture (NYPL) Manuscripts, archives, rare books, and photographs documenting African descent histories, with strong holdings on the Great Migration and its impact on Northern cities.

Mexican & Latin American Immigration

  • Bracero History Archive Open-access collection of 150+ oral histories, documents, photographs, and artifacts from the Bracero guest worker program (1942–1964), a collaboration of George Mason University, the Smithsonian, Brown University, and UT El Paso.
  • National Archives — Mexican Border Crossing Records Indexed immigration records for Mexican border crossings (1903–1957) with card manifests listing name, age, sex, occupation, and destination.
  • Library of Congress — Bracero Program Research Guide Curated guide connecting the Bracero program to broader Latinx civil rights history with linked primary source materials.
  • USCIS — Bracero Program Images Digitized photographs documenting INS inspection and admission procedures at Mexican border ports of entry.

Refugee Resettlement

Holocaust Refugees & Displaced Persons

  • U.S. Holocaust Memorial Museum — Displaced Persons & Immigration Primary source documents on the Displaced Persons Act (1948) and Holocaust refugee resettlement, including letters, photographs, and official correspondence.
  • JDC Archives — Displaced Persons Camps (1945–1957) Finding guide to materials from the American Jewish Joint Distribution Committee documenting assistance to Displaced Persons in European camps.

Cuban Refugees: Operation Pedro Pan & Mariel

  • University of Miami — Operation Pedro Pan Collections Cuban Heritage Collection materials on Operation Pedro Pan (1960–1962), including records from the program that airlifted 14,000+ unaccompanied Cuban children to the United States.
  • University of Miami — Mariel Boatlift Collections Cuban Refugee Center Records and institutional archives documenting the 125,000 Cuban and 25,000 Haitian entrants during the 1980 Mariel boatlift.

Southeast Asian Refugees

  • UMass Lowell — Southeast Asian Digital Archive Collections from aid workers documenting refugee camps and border settlements in Cambodia, the Philippines, and Thailand (1970s–1980s), covering Vietnamese, Cambodian, Hmong, and Laotian displacement.

Internal Migration

  • Library of Congress — FSA/OWI Photographs 164,000 digitized Farm Security Administration photographs documenting Dust Bowl migration and rural poverty (1935–1943), including Dorothea Lange’s iconic images of migrant families.
  • Oakland Museum of California — Dorothea Lange Digital Archive Specialized Dust Bowl section of Lange’s photographs and related documentary materials from the FSA collection.

Major Research Collections

  • University of Minnesota — Immigration History Research Center Archives Founded 1965; 3.5 million items including immigrant letters, diaries, organizational records, photographs, maps, and rare books, with particular strength on 1880–1930s labor migrants and post-1975 refugee populations.
  • Historical Society of Pennsylvania — Balch Institute Collections 60+ ethnic groups documented through manuscripts, images, pamphlets, and ethnic newspapers; focus on Philadelphia’s 1.3 million immigrants (1815–1985).
  • USCIS — Historical Reference Library Agency publications, handbooks, reports, and digitized periodicals since 1892, including annual reports, statistical yearbooks, and policy documentation.
