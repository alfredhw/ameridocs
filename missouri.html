Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • State Historical Society of Missouri — Native American Research Guide Materials on the Osage, Missouria, and other nations whose territory once extended across the state, including Euro-American contact records, treaties, and ethnographic documents.

French Colonial Era & Fur Trade (1670s–1803)

  • Louisiana Colonial Documents Digitization Project French Superior Council records (1714–1769) and Spanish Judiciary records (1769–1803) covering the colonial administration that governed St. Louis (founded 1764) and Upper Louisiana.
  • Missouri History Museum — Online Collections Photographs, prints, and archival materials documenting St. Louis from its French colonial founding, with over 800 photographic collections and 90,000 books, maps, and manuscripts.

Lewis & Clark Expedition

  • University of Nebraska Press — Complete Journals of Lewis & Clark Fully searchable digital edition of the expedition journals, covering the 1804 departure from Camp Dubois (near present-day Hartford, Illinois, across from St. Louis) through the 1806 return.
  • Missouri State Archives — Lewis & Clark Collection State archival materials related to the expedition’s Missouri connections and territorial governance.
  • American Journeys — Lewis & Clark Documents Searchable digital library of early American exploration documents related to the expedition.
  • Library of Congress — Jefferson’s Secret Message (1803) Approximately 25,000 items of Thomas Jefferson papers relating to expedition planning and execution.

Westward Expansion: Gateway to the West

  • University of Oregon — Oregon Trail Special Collections Diaries from 1850–1853 overland journeys departing from Missouri jumping-off points like Independence and St. Joseph.
  • Library of Congress — Santa Fe Trail Map Historic map of the Santa Fe Trail, which originated in Missouri and served as a major commerce route to the Southwest (1821–1880).

The Dred Scott Case

  • Missouri State Archives — Dred Scott Case Collection 85 original documents from Dred and Harriet Scott’s trials in the St. Louis Circuit Court (1846–1857), including summons, depositions, writs, bonds, petitions, affidavits, and verdicts. Digital partnership with Washington University Libraries.
  • Library of Congress — Dred Scott Research Guide Primary source documents and research guide on the 1857 Supreme Court decision, with links to digitized court records, contemporary newspaper coverage, and congressional debates.

Civil War in Missouri (1861–1865)

  • Missouri Digital Heritage — Civil War Collection Digitized records documenting guerrilla warfare, border conflict, and military operations in Missouri during the Civil War.
  • Civil War on the Western Border — General Order No. 11 Digital archive documenting General Thomas Ewing Jr.’s infamous 1863 order depopulating four Missouri counties, Quantrill’s Raiders, and the violent Missouri-Kansas border conflict.
  • Civil War Missouri Resources on Missouri’s devastating Civil War experience, including battles, guerrilla actions, and civilian impact.

Mark Twain & Hannibal

  • Library of Congress — Mark Twain’s Hannibal Thousands of digitized images, maps, sheet music, newspapers, and resources related to Hannibal, Twain’s boyhood, and the Mississippi River world that inspired Huckleberry Finn and Tom Sawyer.

1904 St. Louis World’s Fair

  • St. Louis Public Library — Louisiana Purchase Exposition Glass Plate Negatives Over 6,700 black-and-white images showing fair grounds, palaces under construction, international exhibits, and visiting dignitaries from the 1904 World’s Fair.
  • St. Louis Public Library — World’s Fair Research Guide Over 7,500 images and curated guide to primary sources on the Louisiana Purchase Exposition.
  • Washington University in St. Louis — 1904 World’s Fair Guide Research guide to Fair materials in Washington University collections, including photographic plates and printed ephemera.
  • University of Missouri — World’s Fair Documents Searchable World’s Fair Bulletin (1900–1904) and James Buel’s eleven-volume Louisiana and the Fair (1905).

Kansas City Jazz & Music Heritage

  • State Historical Society of Missouri — Kansas City Jazz Oral History Collection Transcriptions and audio recordings of interviews with Kansas City jazz musicians from the “Golden Age” (1924–1942).
  • Kansas City Public Library — Missouri Valley Special Collections Digital Gallery Over 20,000 images of archival materials—letters, photographs, postcards, maps—and oral history interviews with full text transcriptions documenting Kansas City history.

Harry S. Truman

  • Harry S. Truman Presidential Library — Online Collections Digitized papers, photographs, films, and government documents from the Independence, Missouri presidential library. Student Research Files contain 500–1,000 pages on major policy topics.
  • Truman Library — Truman Papers Personal and presidential papers, policy documents, and correspondence from Truman’s political career.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Missouri Digital Heritage Over 9 million records about Missouri history from the State Archives, State Library, and institutions statewide, including rare books, photographs, maps, and government documents.
  • State Historical Society of Missouri — Digital Collections Digitized photographs, art, maps, diaries, letters, books, and oral histories. Over 8 million pages of Missouri newspapers digitized and freely searchable.
  • University of Missouri — Special Collections Digital Rare books, archival materials, and photographs including correspondence, diaries, and papers from pioneers, soldiers, workers, and politicians.
  • Library of Congress — Missouri State Guide Comprehensive guide to Missouri materials across LOC holdings.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Missouri Newspapers (Library of Congress) 96 digitized Missouri newspaper titles, fully searchable.
  • State Historical Society of Missouri — Missouri Digital Newspaper Project Over 8 million pages of Missouri newspapers digitized and keyword-searchable, complementing Chronicling America holdings.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Missouri Aggregated search across Missouri cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
