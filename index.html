About AmeriDocs. This site is a directory of openly accessible primary source collections for the history of the United States. Inspired by EuroDocs at Brigham Young University, it aims to organize links to freely available historical documents—letters, diaries, government records, photographs, maps, newspapers, and more—by state, territory, region, and theme. All linked collections are free to access online; no subscriptions or paywalls.

Thematic & Cross-Cutting Collections

Thematic Pages

  • Immigration & Migration Cross-state sources on immigration waves, internal migration, refugee resettlement.
  • Native Nations & Indigenous History Tribal histories, treaties, Bureau of Indian Affairs records, reservation-era documents.
  • Slavery, Abolition & the African American Experience Slave narratives, abolition movement, Freedmen’s Bureau, civil rights primary sources.
  • Westward Expansion & the Frontier Homesteading, overland trails, land surveys, territorial governance.
  • Labor & Industry Union records, labor disputes, industrial development, worker narratives.
  • Women’s History Suffrage movement, women’s organizations, diaries, correspondence.
  • Military & Wartime Revolution through modern conflicts: service records, letters, battle accounts.
  • Economic & Commercial History Banking, trade, tariffs, corporate development, historical economic data, Depression & New Deal.
  • American Religious History Puritans, religious liberty, revivalism, African American churches, Catholic & Jewish traditions, Social Gospel.
  • Gender & Sexuality LGBTQ archives, homophile movement, Stonewall, AIDS crisis, transgender history, marriage equality.
  • American Foodways Historic cookbooks, agricultural history, Depression-era & wartime food, Southern & regional foodways, immigrant food traditions.
  • Entertainment & Sports Recorded sound, vaudeville & theater, early film, radio & television, baseball, hip hop, world’s fairs.
  • Hispanic & Latino American Experience Spanish colonial heritage, Chicano movement, Puerto Rican & Cuban American history, borderlands, civil rights.
  • Asian American & Pacific Islander Experience Chinese exclusion, Japanese American incarceration, Angel Island, Filipino & South Asian history, Pacific Islander communities.
  • The United States & Latin America Monroe Doctrine, Spanish-American War, Panama Canal, Cold War interventions, declassified documents, FRUS.
