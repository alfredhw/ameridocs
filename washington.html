Native Peoples & Plateau Cultures

  • Plateau Peoples’ Web Portal (Washington State University) Reciprocally managed archive curated by tribal representatives from the Yakama, Nez Perce, Spokane, Colville, Umatilla, Coeur d’Alene, Warm Springs, and Salish-Kootenai nations. Materials drawn from WSU, Northwest Museum, Smithsonian NMAI, and National Anthropological Archives.
  • Northwest Museum of Arts and Culture — Spokane Largest collection of Plateau Indian art and artifacts in the world (over one million objects), with digital archives of photographs, posters, drawings, and manuscripts.
  • University of Washington — Coast Salish Collections Archival collections on Coast Salish and neighboring language materials, photographs, and manuscripts searchable through Archives West.

Exploration & Territorial Era (1790s–1889)

  • University of Nebraska Press — Lewis & Clark Journals Fully searchable digital edition of the expedition journals, documenting the 1805–1806 journey through the Columbia River region and Pacific coast.
  • Hudson’s Bay Company Archives (Archives of Manitoba) 1,052 reels of digitized microfilm covering records from nearly 500 posts, including Fort Vancouver (established 1825), the key fur-trade and administrative center of the Oregon Country.
  • Washington State Library — Research Collections Digital maps and documents covering Washington territorial and state government history from early exploration (1794) through statehood.

Labor History: Wobblies, Strikes & the Working Class

Seattle General Strike (1919)

  • University of Washington — Seattle General Strike Project Comprehensive digital archive with Central Labor Council meeting minutes, pamphlets, IWW leaflets, photographs, spy reports, and the complete Seattle Union Record (January–February 1919).
  • University of Washington — Solidarity Centennial Exhibition Over 180 digitized articles, personal biographies, and primary source documents from the five-day general strike.

Everett Massacre & Centralia Tragedy

  • University of Washington — IWW History Project: Everett Massacre (1916) Documents on the armed confrontation between Industrial Workers of the World members and Everett authorities on November 5, 1916.
  • University of Washington — Centralia Tragedy Collection (1919) Pamphlets, leaflets, letters from IWW Seattle Office, legal proceedings, prison files, and court records from the November 11, 1919 incident, including documentation of the Wesley Everest lynching.
  • Labor Archives of Washington (University of Washington) Hundreds of photographs and digitized documents on workers, strikes, and union activities, including The Dispatcher (1942–2014), Voice of the Federation (1935–1941), and Waterfront Worker (1932–1935).

Anti-Asian Violence & Chinese Exclusion

  • The Tacoma Method Project Primary documentation of the 1885 Tacoma riot and forced expulsion of Chinese residents, showing how the “Tacoma Method” of violent exclusion spread across the American West.
  • Washington State Historical Society — Chinese Exclusion in Washington Historical documentation of anti-Chinese violence in Washington Territory, including the 1886 Seattle riot and statewide expulsion campaigns.

Japanese American Incarceration (WWII)

  • Densho Digital Repository Over 900 video oral histories (1,700+ hours, fully transcribed), 10,700+ historic photographs, documents, and newspapers covering Japanese American immigration, WWII incarceration at camps including Minidoka and Puyallup, and the long aftermath. Seattle-based nonprofit founded 1996.
  • Friends of Minidoka Previously undigitized War Relocation Authority negatives (1943–1945), camp newspapers, yearbooks, newsletters, and personal documents from Minidoka incarcerees—many of whom were forcibly removed from Washington communities.

Grand Coulee Dam & Columbia River Development

  • University of Washington — Grand Coulee Dam Collection Photographs documenting the construction of the massive Columbia River dam (1933–1941), its impact on Native communities, and the transformation of the inland Northwest.
  • University of Idaho — Columbia River Basin Project Over 500 photographs documenting Columbia Basin irrigation development, Army Corps of Engineers projects, and regional transformation.
  • Washington State Archives — Grand Coulee Dam Records Government records, scrapbooks, and technical drawings spanning the dam’s construction period.

Hanford Nuclear Site

  • Hanford History Project Oral histories and archival documentation of the Hanford nuclear reservation, from the displacement of farming communities and Native peoples to Manhattan Project plutonium production and Cold War operations.
  • Washington State University — Hanford Photographs Photographs (1900–1980) showing Native Americans, pre-dam towns, and WWII/Cold War nuclear facilities at the Hanford site.

Civil Rights & Social Movements

  • University of Washington — Seattle Civil Rights & Labor History Project Multi-media archive with 300+ pages covering Seattle’s African American, Chicano, and Filipino-American civil rights movements, Black Panther activism, video oral histories, rare photographs, and personal biographies.
  • University of Washington — Pacific Northwest African American Digital Collections Guide to digitized materials on African American history and communities across the Pacific Northwest.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Washington State Digital Archives Over 92 million freely searchable records including vital records, census records, military records, and government documents from state and local agencies.
  • Washington State Historical Society — Collections Search Over 90,000 artifacts, 600,000 photographs, 50,000 ephemera items, and 7,500+ linear feet of archival material, including Coast Salish 3D digital models.
  • University of Washington — Digital Collections Over 7 million unique titles including photographs, maps, newspapers, posters, and rare materials on Pacific Northwest history.
  • HistoryLink.org Free online encyclopedia of Washington State history with over 4,000 articles covering people, places, and events, plus guides to additional primary source locations.
  • Archives West Unified access to finding aids for primary source collections across western U.S. research institutions, including extensive Washington State materials.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Washington Newspapers (Library of Congress) 89 digitized Washington newspaper titles, fully searchable, with coverage through 1963.
  • Library of Congress — Washington Digital Collections Guide Guide to Washington State materials across LOC holdings including maps, photographs, and manuscripts.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Washington Aggregated search across Washington cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
