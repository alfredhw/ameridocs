The Monroe Doctrine & Early Hemispheric Policy

  • Library of Congress — Monroe Doctrine Digital Collections Manuscripts, government documents, images, and newspaper articles related to the Monroe Doctrine (1823): the original text of President Monroe’s message to Congress, related diplomatic correspondence, and secondary analysis.
  • National Archives — Monroe Doctrine Full text of President James Monroe’s December 2, 1823 message to Congress articulating the doctrine of non-colonization and hemispheric intervention, with educational context and related materials through DocsTeach.

The Spanish-American War (1898)

  • Library of Congress — Spanish-American War in Motion Pictures 68 motion pictures produced 1898–1901 documenting the first major use of motion picture cameras in US military conflict: newsreels, combat footage, and documentation of the Cuban independence movement and US military operations.
  • Library of Congress — Spanish-American War Resource Guide Manuscripts, maps, broadsides, photographs, prints, sheet music, and films on the 1898 war encompassing military operations, diplomatic correspondence, and civilian experiences across Cuba, Puerto Rico, and the Pacific theater.
  • Library of Congress — Puerto Rico at the Dawn of the Modern Age First-person accounts, political writings, and historical narratives documenting the Spanish colonial period, the War of 1898, and US territorial administration of Puerto Rico from 1898 to the 1950s.

The Panama Canal

  • University of Florida — Panama and the Canal Digital Collection Extensive holdings from the Panama Canal Museum Collection and University of Florida Latin American & Caribbean Collection: construction documents, administrative records, maps, photographs, and personal narratives spanning 1881–2000s.
  • Hay-Bunau-Varilla Treaty (1903) Full text of the convention establishing US rights to construct and control the Panama Canal, defining the relationship between the US and Panama for 77 years until the 1977 Carter-Torrijos treaties.

Banana Wars & Early Interventions

  • Marine Corps Archives — Banana Wars Campaign Collections Finding aids, indexes, and materials on US military interventions in Central America and the Caribbean (1898–1934): campaigns in Nicaragua (1912–1933), Haiti (1915–1934), Dominican Republic (1916–1924), and Honduras, with military reports, correspondence, maps, and photographs.

Good Neighbor Policy & Alliance for Progress

  • Gilder Lehrman Institute — Roosevelt’s Good Neighbor Policy Speech (1936) Full text of President Roosevelt’s August 14, 1936 address at Chautauqua, New York, articulating Good Neighbor Policy principles of non-intervention and hemispheric cooperation.
  • JFK Library — Alliance for Progress Archival materials on the Alliance for Progress (1961–1969): the Charter of Punta del Este, presidential speeches, administrative records, and correspondence of Kennedy administration officials overseeing hemispheric economic development.

Cold War in Latin America

Guatemala 1954

  • National Security Archive — CIA and Guatemala 1954 Declassified CIA documents on Operation PBSuccess, the 1954 covert operation overthrowing President Jacobo Arbenz: CIA briefings, operational plans, intelligence assessments, and assassination proposals released in response to FOIA requests.
  • CIA FOIA Reading Room — Guatemala Collection Publicly released CIA documents on Guatemala operations declassified under the Freedom of Information Act: formerly classified materials on Operation PBSuccess, intelligence assessments, and covert operations.

Bay of Pigs & Cuban Missile Crisis

  • JFK Library — Cuban Missile Crisis: The World on the Brink Comprehensive interactive digital archive: day-by-day timeline, formerly classified ExComm meeting transcripts, audio recordings of Kennedy and advisors’ discussions, declassified cables, and photographic intelligence from the October 1962 nuclear crisis.
  • JFK Library — Bay of Pigs Collection Declassified operational documents, after-action reports, intelligence assessments, and Top Secret materials on the planning and execution of the April 1961 invasion, from McGeorge Bundy’s National Security files and President Kennedy’s personal papers.
  • JFK Library — Address During the Cuban Missile Crisis Digitized text and audio of President Kennedy’s October 22, 1962 televised address announcing Soviet missile presence in Cuba—the speech that defined the nuclear brinkmanship strategy and brought the world to the edge of nuclear war.

Chile, Operation Condor & the Southern Cone

  • National Security Archive — Chile and the United States Declassified documents on the September 11, 1973 military coup overthrowing Salvador Allende: CIA assessments, State Department cables, intelligence briefings, and evidence of US contacts with the Chilean military and support for the Pinochet regime.
  • National Security Archive — Chile: 16,000 Secret Documents Declassified Over 50,000 pages of declassified records (1973–1990) from the State Department, CIA, White House, and Defense and Justice Departments on the Pinochet dictatorship, including CIA briefings on Operation Condor assassination plots.
  • National Security Archive — Operation Condor Declassified documents on the multinational security cooperation among Southern Cone dictatorships (Chile, Argentina, Paraguay, Brazil, Uruguay) coordinating surveillance, torture, kidnapping, and assassination of political opponents, with CIA intelligence assessments of US awareness.

Central American Conflicts (1980s)

  • Office of the Historian — Central America, 1981–1988 State Department documentation of US Central American policy during the 1980s: the Sandinista government, Contra support, El Salvador civil war, and Reagan administration strategy, with links to Foreign Relations of the United States volumes.
  • National Security Archive — Guatemala Project Declassified documents on Guatemala from the 1954 coup through the 1990s civil war: 2,071 documents from the State Department, CIA, Defense Department, and NSC covering death squads, guerrilla war, genocide, and US military assistance.
  • Guatemala National Police Historical Archive (AHPN) Digital archive of over 10 million scanned images from Guatemala’s National Police: intelligence files, personnel dossiers, and operational records documenting counterinsurgency, human rights violations, and state violence during the civil war (1960s–1990s).

US-Cuba Relations

  • National Security Archive — Cuba Embargoed Declassified documents on the origins and evolution of the US economic embargo against Cuba (1960–present): State Department memos on implementation, Treasury Department sanctions directives, and policy rationales across multiple administrations.
  • National Security Archive — Carter and Cuba Declassified documents on the Carter administration’s efforts toward US-Cuba diplomatic normalization (1977–1981): secret communications, NSC decision documents, and diplomatic correspondence documenting the partial thaw before Reagan-era renewed confrontation.

The Drug War & Colombia

  • National Security Archive — Colombia Project 2,000+ declassified US documents (1948–2010) covering Colombian political violence, guerrilla war, paramilitary abuses, and US policy evolution from Cold War counterinsurgency through drug war escalation and Plan Colombia implementation.

Foreign Relations of the United States (FRUS)

  • Office of the Historian — FRUS Online The official documentary record of US foreign policy: all post-1960 FRUS volumes freely accessible online, covering diplomatic relations with every Latin American and Caribbean nation. The most authoritative primary source collection for US foreign policy.
  • University of Wisconsin–Madison — FRUS Digital Volumes (1861–1988) Full-text searchable scans of all published FRUS volumes (1861–1988) in partnership with University of Illinois Chicago, providing free public access to the complete run of diplomatic documents without registration.

Declassified Documents

  • National Security Archive (George Washington University) The world’s largest nongovernmental collection of declassified US government documents, with 50+ topical briefing books on Latin America covering Guatemala, Chile, Cuba, Colombia, Central America, Panama, and Cold War interventions—all freely accessible.
  • CIA FOIA Electronic Reading Room 8,000+ frequently requested declassified CIA records searchable by keyword, with Latin America materials including Guatemala 1954, Chile coup documents, Cuba operations, and other Cold War intelligence files.

Major Research Collections

  • University of Texas at Austin — LLILAS Benson Latin American Collection One of the world’s premier Latin American research libraries with centuries of freely accessible digital materials: human rights documentation, colonial-era documents, the Guatemala National Police Archive (10+ million images), and extensive visual and manuscript collections.
  • Tulane University — Latin American Library Digital Collections 1,800+ images in “Early Images of Latin America” (1850–1910), Mexican incunabula (earliest Mexican imprints, 1539–1600), and 110,500+ images from the Latin American Image Archive, with specialization in Mesoamerican manuscripts and colonial history.
  • Yale University — Latin American & Caribbean Collections 535,000+ volume Latin American collection with digital access to SUR magazine (40,000+ pages), Cuban cinema history materials, and Central American Federal Republic documents (1825–1840).
