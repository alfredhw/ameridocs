Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • Indigenous Law Web Archive (Library of Congress) Constitutions, ordinances, Supreme Court papers, and legislation for Cherokee Nation and Muscogee (Creek) Nation.
  • Museum of the Cherokee Indian — Online Collections Over 18,000 digitized records, letters, and diaries from the 1830s onward related to the Eastern Band Cherokee.
  • University of Alabama — Native American Primary Sources Research guide focusing on Native American history including southeastern Georgia tribes.
  • New Georgia Encyclopedia — Native Peoples Scholarly articles with primary source references on Cherokee, Creek/Muscogee, and other Georgia nations.

Colonial Georgia (1733–1776)

  • Georgia Archives — Trustees Records Official records of the Georgia Trustees for Establishing the Colony, including Oglethorpe correspondence.
  • Colonial Records of the State of Georgia (UGA Press) Original papers and correspondence to Trustees and Oglethorpe (1732–1735), digitized and readable online.
  • Library of Congress — Georgia Colony 1732–1750 Account of the establishment of the colony under General James Oglethorpe and related documents.
  • Salzburgers and Their Descendants (Digital Library of Georgia) Documents on the German Lutheran Protestant settlers who arrived in Georgia in 1734.

Revolution & Early Republic

  • Georgia Archives — Revolutionary War Documents Official Declaration of Independence copy (March 2, 1777), proclamations, and evacuation records.
  • Georgia’s Roster of the Revolution (Internet Archive) Compiled list of Georgia's officers, soldiers, and sailors with service certificates and land grants.
  • Library of Congress — American Revolution Digital Collections Manuscripts, broadsides, government documents, and maps from 1763–1783 including Georgia materials.
  • New Georgia Encyclopedia — Revolution & Early Statehood Scholarly articles and linked primary sources on the Revolution and early governance in Georgia.

Antebellum & Slavery

  • Digital Library of Georgia — Slave Records Legal documents, estate appraisals, and inventories documenting slavery in Georgia (1831–1863).
  • Digital Library of Georgia — Enslaved Persons Collections Focused collection documenting the lives of enslaved individuals across Georgia.
  • Digital Library of Georgia — Antebellum Era Time-period filtered collections of plantation manuscripts, diaries, and account books.
  • Henry Ford Museum — Georgia Plantation Inventory (1823) Mulberry Hill and White Hall Plantations inventory listing over 130 enslaved individuals.

Cherokee Removal & Trail of Tears

  • National Museum of the American Indian — Indian Removal Act (1830) Original text and context of the law that led to Cherokee removal from Georgia.
  • Oklahoma Historical Society — American Indian Archives Cherokee, Creek, Choctaw, and Seminole records (1856–1906) documenting post-removal governance.
  • Trail of Tears National Historic Trail (NPS) Maps, documents, and interpretive materials on the Cherokee removal routes through Georgia.

Civil War & Reconstruction

  • Georgia Archives Virtual Vault — Civil War Records Sherman's evacuation order (September 4, 1864), Civil War maps, and military documents.
  • Library of Congress — Atlanta Siege Map (1864) Map of the Atlanta siege under Maj. Gen. William T. Sherman, July–August 1864.
  • Chronicling America — Sherman’s March in Newspapers Historic newspaper coverage of Sherman's March to the Sea (1863–1865).
  • NMAAHC — Freedmen’s Bureau Records Searchable portal to Freedmen's Bureau records including Georgia Reconstruction documents.
  • DPLA — Freedmen’s Bureau Documents and images including Georgia materials and Camilla Massacre affidavits (1868).

Jim Crow & Early 20th Century

  • Leo Frank Papers (Digital Library of Georgia) Correspondence (1912–1916) related to the trial and lynching of Leo Frank.
  • Emory University — Leo Frank Collection Trial records, attorneys' notes, and related documents from the 1913 case.
  • Chronicling America — Leo Frank Case Historic newspaper articles from the Atlanta Constitution, Georgian, and Journal covering the trial.
  • Leo Frank Case Archive 3,000+ pages of documents and contemporary newspaper coverage of the 1913–1915 case.

Civil Rights Movement

  • Civil Rights Digital Library (CRDL) Over 55,000 digitized items from 200+ institutions including Atlanta sit-ins, Albany Movement, and desegregation records.
  • CRDL — Albany Movement Interactive resources and documents from the 1961–1962 Albany Movement desegregation campaign.
  • King Institute — Albany Movement Martin Luther King Jr.'s own account of the Albany Movement with contextual essays.
  • Voter Education Project Materials (Digital Library of Georgia) VEP documents (1962–1970) including correspondence with John Lewis, press releases, and voting statistics.
  • New Georgia Encyclopedia — Voter Education Project Scholarly article on the VEP's role in voter registration campaigns across Georgia and the South.

Georgia Government & Law

  • Georgia Archives Official state repository for Georgia records from 1733 to present.
  • Georgia Archives Virtual Vault Digital access to manuscripts, photographs, maps, and government records dating from 1733.
  • Georgia Legislative Documents (Digital Library of Georgia) Digitized Georgia Acts and Resolutions from 1799–1999, searchable full-text.
  • Acts of the General Assembly (UPenn Online Books) Archive of Georgia legislative acts from 1755 to present.
  • National Archives at Atlanta Federal records repository covering Georgia, Alabama, Florida, Kentucky, Mississippi, and the Carolinas (1716–present).

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Digital Library of Georgia Over 830,000 digitized items from 200+ Georgia cultural institutions, searchable by topic, era, and format.
  • Georgia Historic Newspapers 850+ newspapers (2.7+ million pages) from the 1760s to present, including African American, religious, and college papers.
  • Chronicling America — Georgia Newspapers Digitized historic Georgia newspapers via the Library of Congress.
  • Historic Maps (Georgia Archives via DLG) Maps from the Office of Surveyor General (1740s onward) covering the state, towns, and southeastern U.S.
  • Georgia Historical Society — Digital Image Catalog Photographs, prints, artifacts, portraits, and maps from the 5+ million-item GHS collection in Savannah.
  • UGA Special Collections Libraries Over 5,000 archival collections, 200,000 audiovisual items, and 250,000 published volumes on Georgia.
  • New Georgia Encyclopedia 2,000+ scholarly articles with 8,000+ multimedia files on Georgia history, freely available.
  • Library of Congress — Georgia Primary Source Sets Curated sets on Georgia history spanning the colonial period to the 20th century.
