Regional collections spanning Kansas, Nebraska, North Dakota, Oklahoma, and South Dakota. See also individual state pages.

Regional Digital Portals

  • University of Nebraska–Lincoln — Encyclopedia of the Great Plains 1,316 entries by over 1,000 scholars covering the history, culture, climate, politics, and peoples of the Great Plains—a comprehensive digital humanities reference for the region.
  • Kansas Memory Hundreds of thousands of primary sources from the Kansas State Historical Society: photographs, maps, manuscripts, documents, letters, diaries, and state government records.
  • Oklahoma Digital Prairie 100+ years of Oklahoma history: archives, documents, photographs, newspapers, maps, and audio-visual materials including Land Run of 1889 materials and Tulsa Race Massacre documents.
  • Gateway to Oklahoma History Oklahoma Historical Society repository with hundreds of thousands of newspaper pages (1840s–1920s), photographs, maps, postcards, and books from pre-statehood to the present.

Native Nations of the Plains

  • Oklahoma Historical Society — American Indian Archives Over 3.5 million documents and 6,000 volumes representing 66 tribal nations, including records of the Cherokee, Chickasaw, Choctaw, Muscogee (Creek), and Seminole from 1856–1906.
  • University of Oklahoma — Indian-Pioneer Papers WPA Federal Writers’ Project oral history typescripts (1861–1936) from thousands of Oklahomans on the settlement of Oklahoma and Indian Territory.
  • National Archives — Fort Laramie Treaty (1868) Digitized text and facsimile of the treaty establishing the Great Sioux Reservation, with multiple viewing options. (See also Native Nations.)
  • National Indian Boarding School Digital Archive Records from Carlisle (PA), Genoa (NE), Pipestone (MN), and other boarding schools documenting the U.S. Indian boarding school era across the Plains and beyond.
  • Gateway to Oklahoma History — Indian Territory Maps Digitized nineteenth- and twentieth-century maps of Indian Territory documenting tribal boundaries, land allotments, and the progressive opening of Indian lands to non-Native settlement.

Homesteading & Settlement

  • History Nebraska — Solomon D. Butcher Collection Approximately 3,000 glass-plate negatives (1886–1912) documenting homesteading on the Nebraska Plains—sod houses, families, farms, and towns in one of the most important visual records of Great Plains settlement.
  • Homestead National Historical Park — Digital Records Digitized homestead case files with 1.6 million images from Nebraska’s 76,871 files: documents describing property improvements, naturalization records, and military service of homesteaders.
  • Bureau of Land Management — General Land Office Records Over 5 million title records for public land sales across the Plains: survey plats, field notes, homestead certificates, and railroad grants. (See also Westward Expansion.)
  • Library of Congress — The Northern Great Plains (1880–1920) 900+ photographs from the Hultstrand and Pazandak collections documenting settlement, agriculture, and daily life on the Northern Plains.

Cattle Trails & the Open Range

The Dust Bowl

  • DPLA — The Dust Bowl Curated primary sources on the ecological and economic catastrophe of the 1930s: photographs, government reports, personal accounts, and New Deal soil conservation records.
  • Library of Congress — FSA/OWI Photographs 164,000 photographs documenting Depression-era hardship on the Plains by Dorothea Lange, Arthur Rothstein, and others, including Rothstein’s iconic images of dust storms in Oklahoma and the Texas Panhandle.
  • Library of Congress — Dust Bowl Migration Primary sources and classroom materials on the mass exodus from the Plains to California, with emigrant accounts, government reports, and documentary photography.

Oklahoma Land Runs & Indian Territory

  • DPLA — Oklahoma Land Rush (1889–1895) Primary sources on the land runs that opened former Indian Territory: photographs, maps, personal accounts, and government documents.

Plains Agriculture & Populism

  • Northern Illinois University — American Populism (1876–1896) Primary sources on the People’s Party, Farmers’ Alliance, and agrarian reform movements that swept the Plains: political propaganda, manifestos, and contemporary documents.
  • UNT Digital Library — U.S. Experiment Station Record Abstracts of agricultural experiments from USDA stations across the Plains (1901–1954), documenting farm innovation, crop development, and extension work.

Major Research Collections

  • History Nebraska — Digital Collections 470,900+ digital assets: government records, photographs, manuscripts, maps, and sound recordings documenting Nebraska and the central Plains.
  • South Dakota Digital Archives 70,000+ images and 1,288 maps from the South Dakota State Historical Society, covering Plains history from the late 1800s to the 1980s.
  • State Historical Society of North Dakota — fiNDhistory Searchable photographs, documents, video, and oral histories depicting Northern Plains life from the late nineteenth century to the present.
  • NDSU — Germans from Russia Heritage Collection Research materials on German-Russian immigration to the northern Plains: family histories, community records, and cultural documentation of one of the region’s most distinctive ethnic communities.
