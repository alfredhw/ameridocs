Chamorro & Carolinian Peoples

  • Library of Congress — Northern Mariana Islands Folklife American Folklife Center materials including exhibit catalogs documenting the Spanish Administration (1668–1899), German Administration (1899–1914), and Japanese Administration (1914–1944), with folk arts and cultural materials on Chamorro and Carolinian heritage.
  • Northern Marianas Humanities Council — Digital Archive Historical collections related to Northern Mariana Islands culture, history, and heritage with materials from colonial and post-WWII periods.

World War II: The Battle of Saipan & Tinian

  • National Museum of the Pacific War — Digital Archive Digitized Marine Corps photographs from Saipan and Tinian, oral histories from Pacific War veterans including Saipan residents, and official military documents from the Marianas Campaign (1944).
  • Digital Pasifik (University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee) Primary source photographs and materials on WWII memorials, Suicide Cliff, Japanese military sites on Saipan, and cultural landmarks with context on Chamorro and Carolinian heritage.

Colonial Eras & Trusteeship Period

  • National Archives — Records of the Office of Territories Federal records documenting the Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands (1947–1986) and the transition to Commonwealth status, including administrative documents and government files.
  • National Archives at San Francisco 75,000+ cubic feet of federal records (1850–1980s) including Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands administrative documents, military records, and Japanese occupation materials.

Archives & Reference

  • Library of Congress — South Pacific Islands Web Archive Archived government documents, constitutions, legislation, census data, and development plans from Pacific island governments including the CNMI.
