Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • Chaco Research Archive Comprehensive online resource documenting archaeological research at Chaco Canyon: field records, photographs, slides, and reports on ancestral Pueblo civilization.
  • Palace of the Governors Photo Archives (UNM Digital Collections) Nearly 30,000 digitized photographs (1845–present) including extensive images of Native American communities, pueblos, and tribal cultures.
  • Indian Pueblo Cultural Center Digital Collections Manuscripts, photographs, and historical documents from the only research library dedicated to preserving history and culture of the 19 New Mexico pueblos.
  • American Folklife Center — New Mexico Collections (Library of Congress) Audio recordings, photographs, and manuscript field notes documenting Navajo, Apache, Acoma, Zuni, and Laguna cultural traditions.
  • Chaco Canyon Project Publications (tDAR) Comprehensive publications covering complex Native American occupation from Archaic through Navajo periods.

Spanish Colonial Era (1540–1821)

  • Spanish Archives of New Mexico I (UNM Digital Collections) Digitized administrative, judicial, census, military, and financial records of Spanish government in New Mexico (1621–1821).
  • Gilder Lehrman Institute — Coronado’s Conquest Authorization (1540) Primary authorization letter commissioning Francisco Vásquez de Coronado to explore northern lands—one of the earliest European authorizations in U.S. territory.
  • Declaration of Pedro Naranjo (1680 Pueblo Revolt) Testimony from an Indigenous perspective during the Pueblo Revolt, collected from sworn statements during Otermín's 1681–82 expedition.
  • American Journeys — Pueblo Revolt & Otermín’s Reconquest (1680–1682) Primary documents on the Revolt of the Pueblo Indians and the Spanish reconquest attempts.
  • Coronado Expedition Accounts (Wikisource) Full text of Pedro de Castañeda's primary account of the 1540–1542 expedition to Cíbola and the Great Plains.

Mexican Period & American Conquest (1821–1850)

  • Library of Congress — Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo Digital materials, manuscripts, government documents, maps, and newspaper articles related to the 1848 treaty ending the Mexican–American War.
  • National Archives — Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo (1848) Official exchange copy of the treaty with full text and historical context.
  • New Mexico State Library — Santa Fe Trail Resources George Champlin Sibley's journal (1825–1827), Dr. Rowland Willard's travel diaries, and compiled trail accounts.
  • Digital History — Santa Fe Trail Primary Sources Digitized diaries, accounts, and travel documents from the Santa Fe Trail period (1821–1880).

Territory & Early Statehood (1850–1920)

  • New Mexico State Records Center & Archives — Heritage Search Searchable finding aids to territorial government records: judicial, legislative, and executive branch documents (1846–1912).
  • Territorial Archives of New Mexico (UNM) Territorial government records (1851–1912): reports, minutes, correspondence, appointments, and legislative records, mostly in Spanish.
  • New Mexico State Archives — Billy the Kid Research Resources Primary documents including criminal cases (1878), reward notices, death warrant (April 30, 1881), and Lincoln County War materials.
  • New Mexico State Records Center — Land Grants Spanish and Mexican land grant records, court cases, and supporting evidence on colonial and territorial land distribution.

20th Century

Manhattan Project & Los Alamos

  • Department of Energy — Manhattan Project Historical Resources Searchable database of declassified documents: correspondence, reports, and materials on Los Alamos research.
  • Voices of the Manhattan Project (Atomic Heritage Foundation) Public archive of oral history interviews with Manhattan Project veterans and their families, with documentary materials.
  • Manhattan Project National Historical Park — Los Alamos (NPS) Historical overview, educational materials, and links to primary source documentation on Los Alamos operations.

Navajo Nation & Uranium Mining

  • Navajo Nation Uranium Mining History (Carleton College) Historical documents, federal reports, and oral history materials on uranium mining from the 1940s–1960s.
  • History of Uranium Mining and the Navajo People (AJPH) Full-text peer-reviewed article synthesizing primary sources and government documents on uranium mining impacts.

New Mexico Government & Law

  • New Mexico State Records Center & Archives Central repository for government records spanning Spanish (1621–1821), Mexican (1821–1846), and Territorial (1846–1912) periods.
  • New Mexico Archives Online (UNM Center for Southwest Research) Archive database with finding aids and links to digitized materials across multiple New Mexico repositories.
  • UNM — Spanish Archives of New Mexico: Land Records Guide Research guide on land grant records as primary sources, with links to digitized Spanish Archives.
  • New Mexico State Library — Spanish, Mexican & Territorial Archives Guide Access guide to land records, census documents, and vital records from colonial through territorial periods.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • New Mexico’s Digital Collections (UNM) Nearly 3,000 digitized items from UNM Libraries and the Palace of the Governors Photo Archives; includes student newspapers (1899–1985) and yearbooks.
  • Chronicling America — New Mexico Newspapers Digitized New Mexico newspapers (1852–1923) via the Library of Congress.
  • UNM Center for Southwest Research — Digital Newspapers Over 200,000 digitized newspaper pages from New Mexico (1844–present).
  • New Mexico State Library — Digital Collections State government documents, newspapers, and special collections in digitized form.
  • NMSU Library Digital Collections Railroad history, mining, land grants, Native peoples, and territorial governance materials from New Mexico State University.
  • New Mexico History Museum — Digital Collections Digitized photographs, documents, and archival materials from the museum's holdings (1 million+ objects).
  • Library of Congress — New Mexico Guide Curated guide to New Mexico resources across Library of Congress collections.
