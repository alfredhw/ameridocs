Spanish Colonial Heritage & Early Hispanic Communities

  • Briscoe Center — Béxar Archives Online 250,000+ pages of Spanish and Mexican hand-written manuscripts (1717–1821) documenting the Spanish province of Texas and the Mexican state of Coahuila y Texas: mission records, military dispatches, and civil administration. (See also Southwest.)
  • University of Houston — Recovering the US Hispanic Literary Heritage Open-access repository of thousands of original books, manuscripts, archival items, and ephemera from colonial times through 1980, including approximately 1,500 historical newspapers on microfilm and hundreds of thousands of digitized items documenting Hispanic literary and cultural production.
  • Early Caribbean Digital Archive 57+ pre-twentieth-century Caribbean texts, maps, and images: travel narratives, colonial documentation, natural histories, and diaries providing context for understanding Caribbean and early Hispanic colonial experiences in the Americas.
  • Library of Congress — Hispanic Reading Room Digital Collections Freely accessible digital collections including Puerto Rico at the Dawn of the Modern Age, East Florida Papers (1784–1821), PALABRA audio recordings of prominent Hispanic writers, and curated primary sources on Hispanic and Latinx history.

Mexican American History

  • Library of Congress — Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo Digital Collections Manuscripts, government documents, maps, and newspaper articles on the 1848 treaty that ceded the Southwest to the United States, including land grant documentation and the legal foundations for Mexican American civil status. (See also Southwest.)
  • Library of Congress — Tierra Amarilla Courthouse Raid Primary sources on the 1967 courthouse raid led by Reies López Tijerina and the Alianza Federal de Pueblos Libres, documenting land grant activism and New Mexico communities’ efforts to reclaim Spanish and Mexican land grants.
  • Bracero History Archive Oral histories, documents, and photographs from the Bracero guest worker program (1942–1964), a collaboration of George Mason, the Smithsonian, Brown, and UT El Paso documenting the largest contract labor program in US history. (See also Labor & Industry.)

The Chicano Movement

  • Library of Congress — United Farm Workers Primary sources on the founding of the United Farm Workers (1962), César Chávez and Dolores Huerta’s leadership, and the landmark 1965–1970 Delano grape strike—the longest strike in US history.
  • University of Washington — Chicano Movement in Washington State 300+ digitized newspaper articles, oral histories, photographs, and rare documents covering MEChA organizing, United Farmworker Cooperative, Brown Berets, Chicano theater, and student activism in the Pacific Northwest.
  • Colorado Chicano Movement Archives 25+ archival collections from Chicano activists and organizations in Colorado: land rights struggles, the Coors boycott, education activism, community organizing, and the “Voices of Protest” oral histories.
  • University of Washington — Farmworkers in Washington State 500+ digitized newspaper articles (1933–2002), oral histories, and photographs documenting agricultural labor organizing and the Chicano Movement’s fusion with farmworker activism in the Pacific Northwest.

The Puerto Rican Experience

  • Centro de Estudios Puertorriqueños — Library & Archives The largest research archive on the stateside Puerto Rican experience: 300+ collections of personal papers from artists, elected officials, social activists, and writers (1890s–present), with photographs, documents, oral histories, moving images, and audio clips documenting Puerto Rican communities across the Northeast, Midwest, Florida, California, and Hawai‘i.
  • Library of Congress — Puerto Rico Digital Collections Curated guide to freely accessible digital materials on Puerto Rican history and culture: historical documents, photographs, maps, newspapers, and literary materials covering colonial history, migration, political status debates, and contemporary Puerto Rican experience.
  • The Puerto Rican Experience in Hoboken Open-access digital collection documenting Puerto Rican migration and settlement in Hoboken, New Jersey: photographs, oral histories, and community narratives providing a local perspective on the Puerto Rican diaspora in the urban Northeast.

Cuban American History

  • University of Miami — Cuban Heritage Collection: Mariel Boatlift Multiple archival collections documenting the 1980 exodus of 125,000+ Cubans: the Alberto Muller Collection, Cuban Refugee Center Records, Fort Chaffee Collection, Revista Mariel records, and oral histories from key figures including bankers, artists, and human rights advocates.
  • University of Miami — Cuban Rafter Phenomenon Digital exhibit documenting the balsero (rafter) migration: primary documents, photographs, and historical context for understanding Cuban exile, displacement, and journey narratives across decades of US-Cuba relations.

Hispanic Civil Rights & Legal History

  • Library of Congress — Latinx Civil Rights Resource Guide Comprehensive research portal to primary sources on Hispanic/Latinx civil rights history: dedicated sections on Mendez v. Westminster, Hernandez v. Texas, LULAC history, land grant activism, and labor rights.
  • Mendez v. Westminster — Case History Documents, video, and educational resources on the landmark 1947 federal desegregation case—the first successful federal challenge to school segregation in the United States, predating Brown v. Board of Education by seven years.
  • Library of Congress — Hernandez v. Texas (1954) Primary documents, legal briefs, and historical context on the Supreme Court case establishing that Mexican Americans constitute a distinct class deserving of Fourteenth Amendment equal protection.

The Borderlands

  • University of Arizona — The Documented Border Open-access digital archive focusing on untold and silenced stories of the US-Mexico borderlands: photographs, documents, and oral histories from Special Collections. (See also Southwest.)
  • National Archives — Mexican Border Crossing Records Immigration records from Mexican border crossings (ca. 1903–1955): arrival records, naturalization documents, and border-crossing data. (See also Immigration.)
  • El Paso Public Library — Border Heritage Center Digital Collections 70+ archival collections documenting El Paso and the Southwest border: US Army on the Border, Trost Architecture, and Russell Metcalfe photographs of border life. (See also Southwest.)

Hispanic Media & Cultural Production

  • UCLA Chicano Studies Research Center — Digital Collections 40,000+ freely accessible digital objects (text, images, audio, video) from 648+ archival collections: the Oscar R. Castillo photographs, Garment Workers of Los Angeles photographs, Edward R. Roybal photographs, and the Strachwitz Frontera Collection of Mexican and Mexican American recordings.
  • Library of Congress — PALABRA Archive Audio recordings of prominent writers from Latin America, the Caribbean, and Luso-Hispanic heritage regions reading from their works at the Library of Congress—primary source oral documentation of Hispanic literary and cultural production.

Major Research Collections

  • University of Texas at Austin — LLILAS Benson Latin American Collection One of the world’s premier Latin American and Latina/o Studies libraries: 970,000+ books, 93,500 photographs, 4,000 linear feet of manuscripts, and 11,500 broadsides. The Mexican American Library Program (est. 1974) holds papers of Gloria E. Anzaldúa, Américo Paredes, José Ángel Gutiérrez, and other major figures.
  • UCLA — Chicano Studies Research Center Established 1969 as the first library in the US focused on the Mexican-descent population. Notable projects include the Religion, Spirituality & Faith in Mexican American Social History archive and the LGBT & Mujeres Initiative.
  • Hunter College — Centro de Estudios Puertorriqueños The nation’s leading institute on the stateside Puerto Rican experience, with a Digital Archives and Community Mapping project documenting migration’s influence on Puerto Rican visual culture and community growth across the United States.
