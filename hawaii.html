Native Hawaiian Language, Culture & Knowledge

  • Papakilo Database (Office of Hawaiian Affairs) A “Database of Databases” with 70+ collections and over 1.2 million records, including the Hawaiian Ethnological Notes Collection, Hawaiian Newspapers Collection (1834–1980), and diverse cultural and historical materials documenting Hawaiian people and places.
  • Ulukau: The Hawaiian Electronic Library Free bilingual (English/Hawaiian) digital library with Hawaiian-language newspapers, books, dictionaries, and cultural materials. One of the most-used Indigenous language websites globally.
  • Nūpepa: Hawaiian Newspaper Collection Comprehensive digital collection of Hawaiian-language newspapers published 1834–1948, featuring 69,000+ pages with OCR-corrected text and 21,000+ pages of full transcriptions. Searchable by word, title, date, and place.
  • Bishop Museum — Hawaiian Ethnological Notes Hawaiian Ethnological Notes Collection with Hawaiian-language materials translated by Mary Kawena Pukui, including articles from Hawaiian-language newspapers (1860s–1940s).

Hawaiian Kingdom Era

  • Ka Huli Ao Digital Archives / Punawaiola Approximately 300,000 digitized images of Hawaiian Kingdom historical and legal documents, including constitutions (1840, 1852, 1864, 1887, 1893), Attorney General records, Foreign Office files, and Privy Council records. First bilingual Hawaiian/English legal archive.
  • Waihona Aina — Māhele Records & Land Documents Transcribed and translated legal documents from the Great Māhele (1848–1853), including 14,500+ records with royal patents, land grants, boundary commission records, claimant names, and land descriptions.
  • University of Hawaiʻi School of Law — Hawaiʻi Laws Online Full text of Hawaiian laws from the Kingdom period (1822–1898) and territorial period through statehood, including constitutions and statutes.

Overthrow & Annexation (1893–1898)

  • U.S. Department of State — Blount Report (1893) Commissioner James H. Blount’s report to President Cleveland documenting U.S. involvement in the overthrow of the Hawaiian Kingdom, from the Foreign Relations of the United States archive.
  • National Archives — 1897 Kūʻē Petition Against Annexation The anti-annexation petition bearing 21,269 signatures—the largest petition against U.S. expansion in American history—with educational resources and historical context.
  • University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa — Kūʻē Petition Research Guide Digitized 559-page PDF of the petitions organized by location, representing approximately 38,000–40,000 Hawaiian signatures protesting annexation.
  • National Archives — Joint Resolution to Annex the Hawaiian Islands (1898) Full text and historical context of the congressional legislation annexing Hawaiʻi.

Sugar Plantations & Labor Immigration

  • Library of Congress — Hawaiʻi: Life in a Plantation Society Primary sources on Japanese immigration to Hawaiʻi for plantation labor, with materials covering Japanese, Chinese, Filipino, Portuguese, and other labor groups, including working conditions, wages, and labor organizing.
  • University of Hawaiʻi West Oʻahu — History of Labor in Hawaiʻi Comprehensive resource on plantation labor, multi-ethnic immigration waves, and the labor organizing movements that shaped modern Hawaiʻi.

Hawaiian Mission Era

  • Hawaiian Mission Houses — Digital Archive Archival materials from Protestant missionary impacts in Hawaiʻi, including mission station reports (1822–1865), The Friend newspaper (1843–1954), journals, and letters in Hawaiian and English.

Pearl Harbor & World War II

  • National Archives — Attack on Pearl Harbor Primary source materials including maps, speeches, and historical documents related to the December 7, 1941 attack, with captured Japanese military sources.
  • FDR Presidential Library — Selected Pearl Harbor Documents Primary documents from December 6–8, 1941, including presidential correspondence and executive decisions.
  • University of Hawaiʻi — Hawaii War Records Depository 880+ photographs from U.S. Army Signal Corps and U.S. Navy (1941–1946) documenting the impact of WWII in Hawaiʻi, plus organizational records, personal accounts, maps, and posters. Created in 1943 by the Hawaii Territorial Legislature.

Japanese American Incarceration: Honouʻuliʻuli

  • Densho Digital Repository 900+ video oral histories (1,700+ hours, fully transcribed), 10,700+ historic photographs, documents, and newspapers covering Japanese American incarceration, including Hawaiian Japanese Americans and the Honouʻuliʻuli detention facility.
  • University of Hawaiʻi — Japanese Incarceration in Hawaiʻi Research Guide Links to online resources, books, archival materials, and audiovisual materials on the wartime incarceration of Japanese and Japanese Americans in Hawaiʻi, including Honouʻuliʻuli—the territory’s largest and longest-used camp.

Statehood (1959)

  • National Archives — Hawaii Statehood Primary documents related to Hawaiʻi’s admission as the 50th state on August 21, 1959, including bills, resolutions, and legislative materials.
  • Eisenhower Presidential Library — Hawaii Statehood Executive documents including memos, letters, press releases, and presidential statements on Hawaiʻi statehood.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Digital Archives of Hawaiʻi Millions of digitized records including Hawaiian Kingdom documents, 20th-century photographs, and vital records indexes (marriages 1826–1929, divorces, naturalization, passports, probates, wills).
  • Hawaiʻi State Archives — Digital Collections Digitized materials since 2007, focusing on frequently requested materials, indexes, and fragile documents from the state archives.
  • Bishop Museum — Library & Archives One of the richest collections of primary source materials on Hawaiʻi and the Pacific, including manuscripts, photographs, artwork, oral histories, sound recordings, and maps.
  • University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa — Digital Collections Scanned historical and cultural materials including georeferenced historical maps (1885–1904), Hawaiian-language newspapers, and specialized archival collections.
  • Library of Congress — Hawaiʻi Digital Collections Guide Comprehensive guide to Hawaiʻi materials across Library of Congress holdings.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Hawaiʻi Newspapers (Library of Congress) Digitized Hawaiʻi newspaper titles including English-language papers from the Kingdom and territorial eras, fully searchable.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Hawaiʻi Aggregated search across Hawaiʻi cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
