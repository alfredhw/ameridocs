Native Peoples: Creek, Cherokee & Chickasaw

  • Muscogee (Creek) Nation National Library and Archives Original manuscripts, documents, photographs, and recorded oral histories from the Muscogee (Creek) Nation, whose territory encompassed much of present-day Alabama.
  • Oklahoma Historical Society — Five Civilized Tribes Archives Over 3.5 million documents and 6,000 volumes representing Cherokee, Chickasaw, Choctaw, Creek, and Seminole nations, including records predating removal from Alabama.

Creek War & Frontier Alabama (1813–1830s)

  • Library of Congress — Andrew Jackson Papers Over 26,000 items (1767–1874) including military records from the Creek War (1813–1814), the Treaty of Fort Jackson, and correspondence related to Fort Mims and frontier Alabama.
  • National Park Service — Fort Morgan & the Battle of Mobile Bay Primary sources including newspapers, letters, diaries, photographs, and maps on Fort Morgan and the 1864 naval battle.

Antebellum Cotton Kingdom & Slavery

  • Alabama Department of Archives and History — Digital Collections Over 15,000 digitized photographs, documents, audio, and video materials from the 19th century to present, including plantation-era records and antebellum governance documents.
  • ADAH — Civil War & Reconstruction Newspapers (1861–1875) Digitized newspapers from the Civil War and Reconstruction era, searchable by date and content.
  • National Archives — Alabama Slavery Records Civilian records relating to slavery including inward and outward slave manifests submitted to the Collector of Customs at Port of Mobile.

Tuskegee Institute: Washington & Carver

  • Tuskegee University — Booker T. Washington Digital Collection Papers, correspondence, and materials documenting Washington’s founding and leadership of Tuskegee Institute (1881–1915).
  • Tuskegee University — George Washington Carver Digital Collection Complete Carver Bulletins and research documents from the agricultural scientist’s transformative work at Tuskegee.
  • Library of Congress — Booker T. Washington Papers Correspondence, memoranda, book drafts, articles, speeches, and reports (1853–1946) relating to Tuskegee Institute history.

Scottsboro Boys (1931–1937)

  • Cornell University — Scottsboro Trials Collection Digitized briefs, motions, and other trial documents from the landmark Scottsboro cases, which twice reached the U.S. Supreme Court and transformed American criminal procedure.
  • Famous Trials — Scottsboro Boys 20 trial excerpts, 22 contemporary news articles, 10 appellate decisions, 8 letters, 28 photographs, and 16 participant quotes.

Civil Rights Movement

Montgomery Bus Boycott & Rosa Parks

  • Library of Congress — Rosa Parks Papers Approximately 7,500 items and 2,500 photographs (1866–2006), including Parks’s arrest report from December 1, 1955.
  • King Institute (Stanford) — Montgomery Bus Boycott Documents and analysis of the 1955–1956 boycott that launched the modern civil rights movement.
  • Civil Rights Movement Archive — Montgomery Bus Boycott Rosa Parks’s arrest report, the first boycott leaflet, and related primary documents.

Birmingham Campaign & 16th Street Baptist Church Bombing

  • King Institute (Stanford) — King Papers Complete King Papers collection including the “Letter from Birmingham City Jail” (April 16, 1963), correspondence, speeches, and sermons.
  • The King Center — Library and Archives Largest repository of primary sources on Dr. King and the Civil Rights Movement, including SCLC records and materials from eight major civil rights organizations.
  • Civil Rights Digital Library — 16th Street Baptist Church Bombing Photographs, newspaper clippings, and documents on the September 15, 1963 bombing that killed four girls.
  • Birmingham Public Library — Digital Collections Over 400,000 photographs and 30 million documents including 16th Street Baptist Church bombing materials, Gee’s Bend quilts documentation, and extensive Birmingham civil rights photographs.

Freedom Rides (1961)

  • American Archive of Public Broadcasting — Freedom Riders Interviews with Freedom Riders describing their motivations, experiences, and accounts of the Anniston bus burning (May 14, 1961).
  • Library of Congress — Freedom Riders Photograph Iconic photograph of CORE Freedom Riders outside their burning bus in Anniston, Alabama (1961).
  • Civil Rights Movement Archive — Freedom Rides Original documents, photographs, interviews, letters, and articles about the Freedom Rides through Alabama.

Selma to Montgomery Marches (1965)

  • Civil Rights Movement Archive — Selma Documents Primary documents, photographs, and accounts of march organization and events (1963–1965), including Bloody Sunday at the Edmund Pettus Bridge.
  • Penn State — Jack Rabin Collection 369 black-and-white photographs from March 6–17, 1965, plus audiotapes of the March 25 rally at the Alabama State Capitol.

Government, Archives & Reference

  • Alabama Department of Archives and History — Digital Collections Over 15,000 items including photographs, manuscripts, government records, state publications, and Alabama Historical Quarterly articles.
  • University of Alabama — Digital Collections Nearly 200 collections containing over 139,000 items including rare books, manuscripts, photographs, maps, and Civil War materials from the W.S. Hoole Special Collections Library.
  • Auburn University — Digital Library Digitized maps, postcards, newspapers, and materials focused on Alabama agriculture, rural life, architecture, and 20th-century politics.
  • AlabamaMosaic Aggregated digital collections from Alabama archives, libraries, and museums covering culture, history, and people from the territorial era through the Civil Rights Movement.
  • Civil Rights Digital Library Portal to 350+ collections from 200+ institutions documenting the Civil Rights Movement, with extensive Alabama coverage.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • Chronicling America — Alabama Newspapers (Library of Congress) 22 digitized Alabama newspaper titles, fully searchable.
  • Library of Congress — Alabama Digital Collections Guide Comprehensive guide to Alabama materials across LOC holdings including books, manuscripts, maps, photographs, and sound recordings.
  • Digital Public Library of America — Alabama Aggregated search across Alabama cultural heritage institutions contributing to DPLA.
