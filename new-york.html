Native Peoples & Pre-Contact

  • New York State Library — Native American Materials Digitized treaties, letters, maps, government reports, and manuscripts documenting Haudenosaunee and other Native peoples from the 17th–20th centuries.
  • National Museum of the American Indian — Archive Center Smithsonian archive of Haudenosaunee (Iroquois Confederacy) records, correspondence, and cultural documents.
  • University at Buffalo — Native American Primary Sources Research guide curating freely accessible online collections on northeastern Indigenous peoples.

Dutch & Colonial Era (1609–1770)

  • New York State Archives — Dutch Colonial Records World's largest online collection of Dutch West India Company colonial documents for New Netherland.
  • NYC Municipal Archives — New Amsterdam Records (1647–1675) Digitized 17th-century Dutch colonial records: court cases, property deeds, apprenticeships, and administrative documents.
  • New Netherland Institute — Online Publications Scholarly translations and transcriptions of Dutch colonial documents with context for early European–Indigenous relations.
  • DPLA — Dutch New Netherland Curated primary source set with maps, documents, and images from multiple institutions on Dutch settlement.

Revolution & Early Republic (1770–1830)

  • New York State Library — Revolutionary War Collection Digitized muster rolls, Loyalist records, diaries, military bounty land records, broadsides, and personal papers.
  • America in Class — Making the Revolution (1763–1791) Comprehensive primary source collection with documents, speeches, and correspondence from the Revolutionary period.
  • American Revolution Institute — Manuscripts Personal papers, military records, and correspondence from Revolutionary War figures and civilians.
  • New York State Library — Erie Canal Collection Primary source documents, maps, and illustrations on the construction and impact of the Erie Canal (completed 1825).
  • Founders Online (National Archives) Over 184,000 searchable documents from Hamilton, Jay, and other New York founders alongside Washington, Jefferson, Madison, Franklin, and Adams.

Antebellum & Civil War

  • New-York Historical Society — Slavery & the Underground Railroad Manuscripts, documents, and research materials on slavery, abolition, and Underground Railroad operations in New York.
  • Library of Congress — Seneca Falls Convention Report (1848) Digitized proceedings of the Woman's Rights Convention with Declaration of Sentiments and participant signatures.
  • NPS — Declaration of Sentiments Full text and interpretive materials on the founding document of the American women's rights movement.
  • New York Public Library — Digital Collections (Civil War) Photographs, documents, and artifacts from New York during the Civil War, including draft riot documentation.

Gilded Age & Immigration (1870s–1920s)

  • Statue of Liberty & Ellis Island Foundation — Passenger Search Searchable archive of ship manifests for 51 million immigrants arriving at Ellis Island (1892–1957).
  • FamilySearch — New York Passenger Lists (1892–1925) Digitized passenger arrival lists with searchable database and images of original manifests.
  • Cornell University — Triangle Factory Fire Primary Sources Trial transcripts, survivor testimonies, Fire Marshal reports, and Factory Investigating Commission documents from the 1911 fire.
  • Gilder Lehrman Institute — Triangle Shirtwaist Factory Fire Annotated primary source documents and historical context on the 1911 disaster.
  • National Archives — New York Immigration Records Federal records related to immigration processing in New York with research guides.

20th Century

Harlem Renaissance

  • Library of Congress — Harlem Renaissance Digital Collections Research guide linking to digitized manuscripts, photographs, and sound recordings from the 1920s–1930s.
  • DPLA — Visual Art During the Harlem Renaissance Curated primary source set with artwork, photographs, and documents from NYPL, Columbia, and other partners.
  • NYPL — Schomburg Center Collections Manuscripts, periodicals, photographs, and documents on African American history including the Harlem Renaissance.

Stonewall & LGBTQ+ History

  • Library of Congress — LGBTQIA+ Studies: Stonewall Era Research guide with links to digitized police records, protest photographs, and contemporary coverage of the 1969 Stonewall Uprising.
  • OutHistory — Stonewall Documentation NYPD records from June 28, 1969, and comprehensive bibliography of primary sources on the Stonewall Riots.

General 20th Century

  • Consider the Source NY New York State Archives-sponsored site with digitized primary sources on 20th-century New York history.

New York Government & Law

  • New York State Archives Official repository with search portal for government records and 6,000+ record series.
  • New York State Archives — Digital Collections Digitized photographs, documents, audio files, and films from state archives.
  • New York State Archives — Legislative Bill & Veto Jackets Digitized gubernatorial materials documenting legislative decision-making from 1995 forward.
  • New York State Library — Digital Collections Digitized government publications, historical documents, and subject-specific collections.
  • New York City Municipal Archives 1.6 million digitized images from municipal records dating from the Dutch colonial period to present.

Maps, Newspapers & General Collections

  • New York Public Library — Digital Collections Over 755,000 digitized items: manuscripts, maps, photographs, posters, prints, and rare books; 180,000+ items in the public domain.
  • New York Heritage Digital Collections 275,000+ digitized items from 430+ New York libraries, museums, and archives.
  • Chronicling America — New York Newspapers Digitized historic New York newspapers (1757–1963) including the Tribune, World, and Sun.
  • New-York Historical Society — Digital Collections 100,000+ photographs and documents spanning New York City and state history from the 17th century to present.
  • Cornell University — Rare and Manuscript Collections 500,000+ printed volumes and 80 million manuscripts: regional New York history, reform movements, and agricultural records.
  • Library of Congress — New York Guide Curated guide to New York resources across Library of Congress collections.
